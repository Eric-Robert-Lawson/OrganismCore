## PART II: WHAT IS EMPATHY?

### 2.1 Traditional Definitions

**PHILOSOPHICAL DEFINITIONS:**

**Empathy (from German "Einfühlung"):**
- Original meaning: "feeling into"
- Projection of own feelings onto external objects
- Later: understanding another's mental states

**Key Theorists:**

**Adam Smith (1759):**
> "By the imagination we place ourselves in his situation, we conceive ourselves enduring all the same torments, we enter as it were into his body, and become in some measure the same person with him."

**Emphasis:** Imaginative projection, becoming the other

---

**Edith Stein (1917):**
> "Empathy is the experience of foreign consciousness in general... We can grasp the experiences of others in their bodily expressions."

**Emphasis:** Grasping other's experience through expression

---

**Martin Hoffman (1981):**
> "An affective response more appropriate to someone else's situation than to one's own."

**Emphasis:** Affective (feeling-based) response

---

**PSYCHOLOGICAL DEFINITIONS:**

**Affective Empathy:**
- Sharing emotional states
- Emotional contagion
- Feeling what other feels
- **Bottom-up: feeling leads to understanding**

**Cognitive Empathy:**
- Theory of mind
- Perspective-taking
- Understanding mental states
- **Top-down: understanding leads to (possibly) feeling**

**Compassionate Empathy:**
- Understanding + feeling + motivation to help
- Action-oriented
- Includes moral component

---

### 2.2 Affective vs Cognitive Components

**TRADITIONAL MODEL:**

**Empathy = Affective + Cognitive**

Both components usually present in humans:
- Feel resonance (affective)
- Understand perspective (cognitive)
- Integrated into unified empathic response

**But can occur separately:**

**Pure Affective (without cognitive):**
- Emotional contagion (feeling other's emotion without understanding why)
- Young children (feel distress but don't understand other's perspective)
- "Feeling with" without "knowing why"

**Pure Cognitive (without affective):**
- Psychopathy (understand perspectives but don't feel concern)
- Alexithymia (understand concepts but don't access feelings)
- "Knowing what they feel" without "feeling it yourself"

**QUESTION:**

**Is pure cognitive understanding still "empathy"?**

**Traditional answer:** Often no (reserves "empathy" for affective component)

**Modern answer:** Increasingly yes ("cognitive empathy" recognized as legitimate category)
