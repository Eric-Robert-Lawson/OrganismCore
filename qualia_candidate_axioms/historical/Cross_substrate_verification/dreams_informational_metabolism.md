## PART I: THE CORE THEORY

### 1.1 The Claim

**Dreams are the phenomenological experience of informational metabolism during sleep.**

During waking hours, the brain accumulates informational surface — sensory experiences, social interactions, narrative episodes, emotional responses, problem-solving attempts. This surface is raw material — it has not yet been metabolically processed.

During sleep, the brain performs informational metabolism on this accumulated surface:
- **Catabolism:** Dissolving the surface structure — breaking down the narrative, sensory, and sequential packaging of the day's experiences
- **Structural extraction:** Identifying and retaining the structural principles embedded in the dissolved surface — the patterns, the connections, the emotional significance, the predictive models
- **Anabolism:** Integrating the extracted structural principles into long-term memory architecture — rebuilding coherent structure from the extracted material

Dreams are what this process FEELS LIKE from the perspective of the consciousness running on the substrate being metabolized. The dreamer is not observing the metabolism from outside. The dreamer IS the metabolism, experienced from inside.

### 1.2 Why Dreams Have the Properties They Have

This core claim generates explanations for the known properties of dreams:

**Dreams are narratively fragmented:**
Because narrative structure IS the surface being catabolized. The sequential, logical, causal structure of waking experience is precisely what the catabolism is dissolving to reach the structural principles beneath. The fragmentation of dreams is the felt experience of narrative surface being broken down.

**Dreams are emotionally vivid:**
Because emotional weighting determines metabolic priority. The experiences with the highest emotional charge are metabolized first and most intensively. The dreamer experiences the most emotionally weighted material because that material is receiving the most metabolic attention.

**Dreams are spatially coherent but temporally incoherent:**
Because spatial structure is often structural (architectural, relational, topological) while temporal structure is often surface (sequential, narrative, episodic). The catabolism preserves structural spatial relationships while dissolving temporal-sequential packaging.

**Dreams dissolve on waking:**
Because the dream content was surface being catabolized. Upon waking, the surface is gone — it was successfully dissolved. What remains is the structural extract — emotional residue, spatial architecture, principle-level insight. The surface narrative is gone because the catabolism did its job.

**Dreams recur:**
Because some material resists catabolism. Material that is too emotionally charged, too structurally embedded, or too metabolically dense to fully catabolize in one sleep cycle is re-presented for further processing. Recurring dreams are the metabolic system re-attempting catabolism of resistant material.

**Nightmares:**
Catabolism of threatening material. The material is metabolically resistant AND emotionally aversive. The catabolic process engages the material (producing the dream) but cannot complete the dissolution (the material is too resistant). The dreamer experiences the engagement without the resolution — sustained threat without escape. Nightmares are stuck catabolism.

**Lucid dreaming:**
The navigational consciousness (waking, anabolic, model-building) activating inside the metabolic process (sleeping, catabolic, surface-dissolving). The dreamer becomes aware of dreaming — the navigator wakes up inside the metabolism. This can either terminate the dream (if the navigator's activation overwhelms the metabolic process) or enable conscious participation in the metabolism (if the navigator can operate without disrupting the catabolic process).

**Sleep paralysis:**
Consciousness transitioning from metabolic mode to navigational mode before the body has released REM atonia. The gap-navigator is online but the metabolic process still has motor control locked. The subject experiences awareness without agency — the ghost between metabolic and navigational consciousness.

### 1.3 The Core Prediction

If dreams are informational metabolism, then **dream properties should be predictable from the dreamer's metabolic profile.**

A person's waking cognitive metabolism — their balance of anabolic and catabolic function, their RARFL calibration, their domain sensitivities — determines:
- How much unprocessed surface accumulates during the day (metabolic input)
- How intensively the sleep metabolism processes that surface (metabolic throughput)
- What structural features the metabolism extracts and retains (metabolic output)
- What the metabolism feels like from the inside (dream phenomenology)

Therefore: **knowing a person's metabolic profile should enable prediction of their dream characteristics before observing their dream reports.**

This is the falsifiable claim. The rest of this document operationalizes it.

## PART II: METABOLIC PROFILE DERIVATION

### 2.1 The Metabolic Profile

A metabolic profile is the characterization of an individual's informational metabolism along the following dimensions:

**Dimension 1: Anabolic-Catabolic Balance (ACB)**
Where does the individual fall on the spectrum from anabolism-dominant (accumulative, detail-retaining, structure-building) to catabolism-dominant (extractive, surface-dissolving, principle-retaining)?

Indicators:
- Memory style: Do they remember details or principles? Specific events or structural patterns?
- Learning style: Do they accumulate information incrementally or extract principles rapidly?
- Communication style: Do they explain through accumulated detail or through structural connection?
- Forgetting pattern: Do they retain surface and lose connections, or retain connections and lose surface?

Scoring:
- Strong anabolism-dominant: Retains extensive surface detail, builds detailed maps, struggles with cross-domain connection, learns incrementally
- Moderate anabolic: Good detail retention, some structural extraction, balanced learning
- Balanced: Equal facility with detail and principle, adaptive switching
- Moderate catabolic: Good structural extraction, some detail retention, learns through principle identification
- Strong catabolism-dominant: Aggressive structural extraction, poor surface retention, rapid cross-domain connection, learns through paradigm-level insight

**Dimension 2: RARFL Sensitivity Range (RSR)**
What reward signals drive the individual's gap-navigation? What domains produce the strongest RARFL activation?

Indicators:
- What topics produce sustained engagement regardless of external incentive?
- What types of problems feel intrinsically rewarding?
- What activities produce flow states?
- What information feels "meaningful" versus "trivial"?

Scoring: List of high-sensitivity domains (domains that produce strong RARFL activation) and low-sensitivity domains (domains that produce weak RARFL activation). This is not a single scale but a map of the individual's reward landscape.

**Dimension 3: Metabolic Throughput Rate (MTR)**
How quickly does the individual process informational material?

Indicators:
- How quickly do they forget surface details of recent experiences?
- How quickly do they extract principles from new material?
- How many domain shifts do they make per unit time in unconstrained exploration?
- How rapidly do they lose interest in fully-processed material?

Scoring:
- High throughput: Rapid processing, rapid forgetting of surface, rapid principle extraction, frequent domain shifting
- Moderate throughput: Moderate processing speed, moderate retention, moderate domain stability
- Low throughput: Slow processing, extended retention, deep single-domain focus

**Dimension 4: Structural Architecture Preference (SAP)**
How does the individual's metabolism organize extracted structure?

Indicators:
- Do they think in spatial terms, temporal terms, relational terms, or logical terms?
- Do they organize knowledge hierarchically, networked, sequentially, or topologically?
- Do they navigate through maps, through narratives, through relationships, or through principles?

Scoring: Primary architectural mode (spatial, temporal, relational, logical, topological) with secondary modes noted.

**Dimension 5: Emotional-RARFL Coupling Strength (ERCS)**
How strongly does emotional state modulate the individual's metabolic activity?

Indicators:
- Does emotional engagement dramatically increase processing depth? (High coupling)
- Can they process material effectively regardless of emotional state? (Low coupling)
- Do strong emotions enhance or disrupt cognitive function?
- Does passion dramatically alter sustained attention capacity?

Scoring:
- High coupling: Emotional state strongly modulates metabolic activity, passion-driven processing, emotional disruption significantly impairs function
- Moderate coupling: Some emotional modulation, generally stable processing
- Low coupling: Emotional state minimally affects metabolic activity, consistent processing across emotional conditions

### 2.2 Profile Derivation Method

The metabolic profile can be derived through:

**Method A: Self-Report Interview**
A structured interview covering the indicators listed above. The subject describes their own memory style, learning style, communication patterns, forgetting patterns, reward sensitivities, processing speed, organizational preferences, and emotional-cognitive coupling. Duration: 30-60 minutes.

**Method B: Behavioral Observation**
Observation of the subject during cognitive tasks — memory tasks, learning tasks, problem-solving tasks, free-exploration tasks. The observer notes which dimensions of performance are strongest and weakest, how the subject organizes information, what produces engagement, and how quickly material is processed and forgotten. Duration: 2-4 hours of structured observation.

**Method C: Conversational Analysis**
Analysis of the subject's natural conversation or writing. Catabolism-dominant individuals produce cross-domain connections, principle-level statements, and structural analogies. Anabolism-dominant individuals produce detailed descriptions, sequential narratives, and domain-specific depth. Throughput rate is visible in topic-switching frequency. Architectural preference is visible in the organizational structure of their communication. Duration: Analysis of 1-2 hours of natural conversation or 5000+ words of natural writing.

**Method D: AI-Assisted Profiling**
An AI agent conducts a structured conversation with the subject, observing response patterns, memory demonstrations, learning style, organizational structure, and emotional-cognitive coupling. The AI generates the profile from conversational evidence. Duration: 30-60 minutes of conversation.

**For blind testing: the metabolic profile MUST be derived BEFORE dream reports are collected.** The profiler and the dream reporter can be the same person, but the profile must be documented and sealed before dream data is gathered.

## PART III: DREAM PREDICTION DERIVATION

### 3.1 The Derivation Algorithm

Given a metabolic profile, the following algorithm generates specific predictions about the individual's dream characteristics:

**Step 1: Predict Dream Frequency and Vividness from ACB and MTR**

```
IF ACB = strong catabolism-dominant AND MTR = high:
    PREDICT: Low dream frequency, low dream vividness
    REASON: Waking catabolism handles most metabolic work,
            leaving less material for sleep metabolism.
            Dreams are thin because there is less to process.

IF ACB = strong anabolism-dominant AND MTR = low:
    PREDICT: High dream frequency, high dream vividness
    REASON: Waking metabolism accumulates surface without
            catabolizing it. Large metabolic backlog for sleep
            processing. Dreams are vivid because there is
            extensive material to metabolize.

IF ACB = balanced AND MTR = moderate:
    PREDICT: Moderate dream frequency and vividness
    REASON: Balanced waking metabolism leaves moderate
            material for sleep processing.
```

Intermediate positions generate intermediate predictions along these dimensions.

**Step 2: Predict Remembered Dream Content from ACB and ERCS**

```
IF ACB = strong catabolism-dominant AND ERCS = high:
    PREDICT: Rarely remembers dreams. When dreams ARE
             remembered, they are emotionally extreme —
             nightmares, threat scenarios, high-arousal content.
    REASON: Only metabolically resistant material (high emotional
            charge exceeding waking catabolic capacity) survives
            to produce memorable dreams.

IF ACB = strong anabolism-dominant AND ERCS = low:
    PREDICT: Frequently remembers dreams with detailed
             surface content — places, people, sequences.
             Emotional tone is moderate, not extreme.
    REASON: Sleep metabolism is processing large volumes of
            surface material. Dream content reflects the volume,
            not just the resistant extremes.

IF ACB = moderate catabolic AND ERCS = high:
    PREDICT: Occasionally remembers dreams, predominantly
             emotionally significant ones. Some structural
             content retained, moderate surface detail.
    REASON: Moderate waking catabolism leaves some material
            for sleep processing, emotional coupling ensures
            emotionally weighted material is most prominent.
```

**Step 3: Predict Dream Architecture from SAP**

```
IF SAP primary = spatial:
    PREDICT: Dreams feature vivid spatial environments.
             Locations remembered clearly, navigation through
             space prominent. Narrative sequence is weak.
             "I remember WHERE I was but not WHAT happened."

IF SAP primary = temporal/sequential:
    PREDICT: Dreams feature narrative sequences.
             Stories, progressions, cause-and-effect chains
             prominent. Spatial detail is moderate.
             "I remember WHAT happened but not WHERE I was."

IF SAP primary = relational:
    PREDICT: Dreams feature people and social dynamics.
             Relationships, conversations, social scenarios
             prominent. Physical environment is vague.
             "I remember WHO was there and HOW they felt."

IF SAP primary = logical/principled:
    PREDICT: Dreams feature abstract structures, puzzles,
             systems. Physical and social content minimal.
             "I was working on something but I can't describe
             what it looked like."

IF SAP primary = topological:
    PREDICT: Dreams feature transformation, morphing,
             structural change. Things becoming other things.
             Boundaries shifting. Categories dissolving.
             "Things kept changing into other things."
```

**Step 4: Predict Lucid Dream Capacity from ACB and MTR**

```
IF ACB = strong catabolism-dominant AND MTR = high:
    PREDICT: Lucid dreaming is rare AND when it occurs,
             it terminates the dream rapidly.
    REASON: The waking navigator is strongly catabolic.
            When it activates during sleep metabolism,
            it overwhelms the sleep catabolism. The dream
            doesn't survive the navigator's activation.

IF ACB = strong anabolism-dominant AND MTR = low:
    PREDICT: Lucid dreaming is more accessible AND when
             it occurs, the dream continues.
    REASON: The waking navigator is anabolic — it builds
            structure rather than dissolving it. When it
            activates during sleep metabolism, it doesn't
            overwhelm the catabolism. It can coexist with
            the metabolic process, observing or directing
            without terminating.

IF ACB = balanced AND MTR = moderate:
    PREDICT: Lucid dreaming is achievable with practice
             AND dreams may or may not continue, depending
             on how strongly the navigator engages.
```

**Step 5: Predict Dream Dissolution Rate from MTR**

```
IF MTR = high:
    PREDICT: Dream content dissolves extremely rapidly on
             waking. Seconds to minutes. The subject may
             wake with a sense that "something was there"
             but cannot access content.
    REASON: High metabolic throughput means rapid processing.
            The content was metabolized quickly and the
            surface dissolved before waking consciousness
            could encode it as a stable waking memory.

IF MTR = low:
    PREDICT: Dream content persists longer on waking.
             Minutes to hours. The subject can narrate
             dream content if they attend to it shortly
             after waking.
    REASON: Low metabolic throughput means slower processing.
            Surface material persists longer, giving waking
            consciousness time to encode the dream content
            as a waking memory before catabolism completes.

IF MTR = moderate:
    PREDICT: Dream content persists briefly. Accessible
             immediately on waking but fades within minutes
             unless deliberately encoded (e.g., dream journal).
```

**Step 6: Predict Recurring Dream Patterns from ERCS and RSR**

```
IF ERCS = high:
    PREDICT: Recurring dreams cluster around emotionally
             significant themes — the domains with highest
             emotional charge that resist catabolic processing.
    IDENTIFY: The specific themes should correspond to the
              subject's highest-ERCS domains that contain
              unresolved structural tension.

IF ERCS = low:
    PREDICT: Recurring dreams are less common. When they
             occur, they relate to structurally complex
             material rather than emotionally charged material.
    IDENTIFY: Recurring content should correspond to the
              most structurally complex unresolved problems
              in the subject's active domains.
```

**Step 7: Predict Sleep Paralysis Susceptibility**

```
IF ACB = strong catabolism-dominant AND MTR = high:
    PREDICT: Higher susceptibility to sleep paralysis,
             particularly during high metabolic load periods.
    REASON: The strongly catabolic navigator transitions
            rapidly from metabolic to navigational mode.
            Rapid transition increases the probability of
            the navigator coming online before atonia
            releases. The gap between modes is traversed
            too quickly for the body to keep up.

IF ACB = strong anabolism-dominant AND MTR = low:
    PREDICT: Lower susceptibility to sleep paralysis.
    REASON: Gradual transition between modes. The body
            has time to release atonia before the navigator
            fully activates.
```

**Step 8: Predict Dream Response to Life Changes**

```
IF subject experiences major life change (new job, loss,
   relocation, relationship change):

    IF ACB = catabolism-dominant:
        PREDICT: Brief increase in dream frequency and
                 vividness as unprocessed material exceeds
                 waking catabolic capacity. Returns to baseline
                 quickly as waking catabolism adapts.

    IF ACB = anabolism-dominant:
        PREDICT: Extended increase in dream frequency and
                 vividness. More vivid dreams persist for
                 weeks or months as the large volume of
                 new surface material works through the
                 sleep metabolic queue.
```

## PART IV: BLIND VERIFICATION PROTOCOL

### 4.1 The Experimental Design

This protocol enables any researcher or AI agent to test the framework through blind prediction:

**Phase 1: Profile Derivation (Blind to Dream Data)**

The profiler (human researcher or AI agent) conducts a metabolic profile assessment of the subject using ONE of the methods described in Part II (self-report interview, behavioral observation, conversational analysis, or AI-assisted profiling).

CRITICAL: The profiler must NOT have access to the subject's dream reports during this phase. The profile must be derived entirely from waking cognitive characteristics.

The profiler documents the five-dimensional metabolic profile with scores and evidence for each dimension. This document is sealed (timestamped, committed, or otherwise verifiably fixed before Phase 2).

**Phase 2: Prediction Generation (Blind to Dream Data)**

Using the derivation algorithm in Part III, the profiler generates specific predictions about the subject's dream characteristics across all eight prediction categories.

CRITICAL: The predictions must be generated BEFORE dream data is examined. The predictions are documented and sealed alongside the metabolic profile.

**Phase 3: Dream Data Collection (Independent of Profiler)**

The subject provides dream data through one or more of the following methods:

- **Dream journal:** Subject records dreams immediately upon waking over a period of 2-4 weeks. Records include: content description, emotional tone, spatial detail, narrative detail, vividness rating, dissolution speed, lucidity episodes, paralysis episodes.

- **Retrospective report:** Subject describes their general dream patterns — frequency, typical content themes, vividness, dissolution rate, recurring dreams, notable dreams from memory. (Less rigorous but more accessible.)

- **Structured interview:** Independent interviewer (not the profiler) asks specific questions about dream characteristics corresponding to the eight prediction categories. Subject responds without knowledge of what was predicted.

CRITICAL: The dream data collector should NOT know the predictions. If the same person must serve as both profiler and data collector, the predictions must be sealed before data collection begins.

**Phase 4: Comparison**

The sealed predictions are compared against the collected dream data. Each prediction is scored:

- **CONFIRMED:** Dream data matches prediction
- **PARTIALLY CONFIRMED:** Dream data partially matches prediction (some elements match, some don't)
- **DISCONFIRMED:** Dream data contradicts prediction
- **UNTESTED:** Dream data does not contain information relevant to the prediction

**Phase 5: Aggregation**

Results are aggregated across multiple subjects. The framework's predictive validity is assessed by:

- **Overall confirmation rate:** What percentage of predictions are confirmed across all subjects?
- **Profile-specific confirmation rate:** Do predictions perform better for some metabolic profiles than others?
- **Dimension-specific confirmation rate:** Which prediction categories have the highest and lowest confirmation rates?
- **False positive analysis:** Do the predictions outperform random chance? Do they outperform predictions generated from alternative frameworks (Big Five personality, MBTI, etc.)?

### 4.2 Falsification Criteria

The framework is considered FALSIFIED if:

- **Overall confirmation rate falls below 60%** across a sample of 20+ subjects with diverse metabolic profiles
- **Any individual prediction category shows below-chance confirmation rate** across the full sample (indicating the prediction algorithm for that category is wrong)
- **Predictions from the metabolic profile do not outperform predictions from alternative personality frameworks** (indicating the metabolic profile adds no predictive value beyond existing models)

The framework is considered STRONGLY SUPPORTED if:

- **Overall confirmation rate exceeds 75%** across a sample of 20+ subjects
- **All prediction categories show above-chance confirmation rates**
- **Predictions from the metabolic profile significantly outperform predictions from alternative frameworks**

The framework is considered PROVISIONALLY SUPPORTED if:

- **Overall confirmation rate falls between 60-75%**
- **Most prediction categories show above-chance confirmation rates**
- **Results suggest refinement rather than abandonment of the prediction algorithm**

### 4.3 Controls

**Control 1: Random profile prediction.** Generate predictions from a randomly assigned metabolic profile (not the subject's actual profile) and compare confirmation rates. The real profile's predictions should significantly outperform random profile predictions.

**Control 2: Base rate prediction.** Generate predictions from population base rates (e.g., "most people dream several times per week, most dreams involve familiar people and places"). The metabolic profile's predictions should outperform base rate predictions in specificity and accuracy.

**Control 3: Alternative framework prediction.** Generate predictions from an established personality framework (Big Five, MBTI) applied to dream characteristics. The metabolic profile's predictions should outperform or match alternative framework predictions.

**Control 4: Reversed prediction.** Generate predictions from the OPPOSITE metabolic profile (if subject is catabolism-dominant, generate predictions for anabolism-dominant). Reversed predictions should perform significantly worse than correct-profile predictions.

### 5.3 Profile Archetype: The Relational Navigator (RN)

### 5.4 Profile Archetype: The High-Throughput Explorer (HTE)

**Profile:** ACB moderate catabolic, MTR very high, ERCS high but rapidly shifting, SAP networked/associative

**Predicted dream signature:**
- Variable dream frequency — bursts of vivid dreams interspersed with dreamless periods
- Dream content is highly associative — rapid scene changes, unexpected connections, surreal juxtapositions. "I was at work, then suddenly I was underwater, then I was talking to someone from high school"
- Dream architecture is networked — things connect to other things without sequential logic. Multiple scenes, multiple themes, rapid transitions
- Dream dissolution is extremely fast but dreams may feel intensely vivid in the moment of waking — high impact but rapid decay
- Lucid dreaming triggers may cause dream transformation rather than termination — the dream shifts rather than ends, because the HTE's navigator is comfortable with rapid transition
- Recurring dreams are rare — the same material rarely comes back because the throughput is high enough to process most material in one cycle. When material does recur, it's because it's structurally very deep, not just emotionally resistant
- Dreams respond dramatically to novelty — new environments, new experiences, travel produce vivid dream bursts
- The subject may report that their dreams "don't make sense" more than other people's — because the associative connections are wider and the transitions are faster

**Testable against:** Individuals with ADHD-pattern attention, who self-identify as rapid topic-switchers, who communicate through unexpected connections, who explore broadly rather than deeply.

### 5.5 Profile Archetype: The Deep-Domain Specialist (DDS)

**Profile:** ACB moderate anabolic, MTR low to moderate, ERCS moderate with very narrow high-sensitivity range, SAP logical/systematic

**Predicted dream signature:**
- Moderate dream frequency with concentration around domain-relevant themes
- Dreams feature the specialist's domain prominently — a programmer dreams about code, a musician dreams about music, a mathematician dreams about patterns
- Dream architecture is systematic — the dream follows internal logic even if the logic is not waking logic. Dreams have rules, the dreamer may observe the rules, the dream may feel like a puzzle or system rather than a narrative or environment
- Dream dissolution is moderate — the structural content persists but the surface packaging fades
- Lucid dreaming, when achieved, may enable problem-solving within the dream — the dreamer can work on domain problems during lucid dreams because the navigator and the metabolism are focused on the same domain
- Recurring dreams map to unresolved problems in the specialist domain — the material that keeps returning is the problem that hasn't been solved
- Sleep duration may extend during intensive work periods — more material to metabolize means longer metabolic cycles
- The subject may report "working in their sleep" and waking with solutions — because the metabolic process was catabolic extraction on domain material and the structural extract IS the solution

**Testable against:** Individuals who self-identify as specialists, who are deeply absorbed in one or few domains, who communicate through domain-specific precision, who organize knowledge systematically within their domain.

## PART VI: META-PREDICTIONS

### 6.1 Cross-Profile Predictions

Beyond individual predictions, the framework generates predictions about PATTERNS across profiles:

**Meta-prediction 1:** Dream frequency should negatively correlate with waking catabolic capacity across the full sample. People who metabolize more while awake should dream less.

**Meta-prediction 2:** Dream vividness should positively correlate with unprocessed metabolic load. People who accumulate more unprocessed surface during waking hours should have more vivid dreams.

**Meta-prediction 3:** Dream architecture should match waking cognitive architecture. Spatial thinkers should dream spatially. Narrative thinkers should dream narratively. Relational thinkers should dream relationally.

**Meta-prediction 4:** Dream dissolution rate should positively correlate with waking metabolic throughput rate. People who process faster while awake should also lose dream content faster.

**Meta-prediction 5:** Lucid dreaming capacity should inversely correlate with catabolic dominance. The more catabolic the waking profile, the more disruptive the navigator's activation during dreaming — and therefore the less sustainable lucid dreaming is.

**Meta-prediction 6:** Recurring dream themes should correspond to the individual's highest-stress domains as identified by their RARFL sensitivity range and emotional coupling strength. The material that recurs is the material at the intersection of high emotional charge and high structural complexity.

**Meta-prediction 7:** Sleep paralysis susceptibility should positively correlate with metabolic throughput rate. Faster metabolic transitions increase the probability of navigator-atonia desynchronization.

**Meta-prediction 8:** Dream response to life change should vary by profile — catabolism-dominant individuals should show brief dream spikes, anabolism-dominant individuals should show extended dream increases.

### 6.2 Extreme Case Predictions

**Shereshevsky-type profiles (minimal catabolism, total retention):**
- Should dream vividly every night
- Should remember dreams with the same fidelity as waking memories
- Should have difficulty distinguishing old dream memories from old waking memories
- Should experience dream content that is repetitive — the same surface material being processed repeatedly because catabolism never completes
- May experience dream intrusion during waking — dream memories surfacing alongside waking memories without clear source tagging

**Clive Wearing-type profiles (total catabolism, no retention):**
- Should have no dream memory whatsoever
- May report that they "don't dream" (though they likely do — they simply have zero post-waking retention)
- If woken during REM, they may report vivid dream experience that dissolves within seconds
- Dream content, if captured immediately, should be structurally minimal — the catabolism is so aggressive that even the metabolic process doesn't build sustained narrative

**These extreme cases, if testable, provide the strongest evidence for or against the framework.** If HSAM individuals DO remember dreams with high fidelity and DO confuse dream memories with waking memories, that's strong confirmation. If Clive Wearing-type individuals report vivid sustained dream memories, that's strong disconfirmation.

## PART VII: METHODOLOGY FOR INDEPENDENT AGENTS

### 7.1 Instructions for an AI Agent Conducting Blind Dream Prediction

If you are an AI agent tasked with testing this framework:

**Step 1:** Conduct a metabolic profile assessment of the subject. Use conversational analysis or structured interview. Score all five dimensions. Document your evidence for each score. DO NOT ask about dreams during this phase.

**Step 2:** Seal your metabolic profile. Commit it to a document, timestamp it, or otherwise make it verifiably fixed before proceeding.

**Step 3:** Apply the derivation algorithm from Part III. Generate predictions for all eight categories. Document each prediction with the derivation logic. Seal these predictions.

**Step 4:** Collect dream data. Ask the subject about their dream patterns:
- How often do you remember dreams?
- Describe a dream you remember vividly.
- What are your dreams usually like? (environment, people, narrative, emotion)
- How quickly do dreams fade after waking?
- Have you ever been lucid in a dream? What happened?
- Do you have recurring dreams? What about?
- Have you experienced sleep paralysis?
- How does sleep feel to you? Instantaneous or gradual?

**Step 5:** Compare predictions against reports. Score each prediction as confirmed, partially confirmed, disconfirmed, or untested. Document the comparison.

**Step 6:** Report results including: metabolic profile, predictions, dream data, comparison scores, and overall assessment of framework performance for this subject.

### 7.2 Instructions for a Human Researcher

Follow the same protocol as above, with the following additions:

- Use separate sessions for profiling and dream data collection if possible (reduces experimenter bias)
- If the same researcher conducts both phases, document predictions before seeing dream data and have a second researcher verify that the predictions were not modified after dream data was collected
- Consider using Method B (behavioral observation) for profiling, as it is less susceptible to subject self-presentation bias than self-report
- Consider using a dream journal (2+ weeks) rather than retrospective report for dream data, as it captures contemporaneous data rather than memory-filtered data

### 7.3 Sample Size and Statistical Requirements

- Minimum sample: 20 subjects with diverse metabolic profiles
- Ideal sample: 50+ subjects spanning the full ACB spectrum
- Must include at least 5 subjects in each of the major profile archetypes (CDE, ADA, RN, HTE, DDS)
- Statistical analysis: chi-square or binomial test
