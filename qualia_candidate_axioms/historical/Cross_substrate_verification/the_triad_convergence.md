## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Three independently derived triads —
epistemological, process-level, and
consciousness-level — are not three
different triads.

They are the same triad instantiated
at three different scales.

The triad describes, at every scale,
the same transformation:

**A system that converts raw substrate
into a stable self-referential object
through an iterative process.**

That transformation is gap navigation.

The triad is gap navigation described
at three levels of grain simultaneously.

This has direct and precise implications
for cross-species communication.

## PART I: THE THREE TRIADS STATED

### 1.1 Level One — Epistemological

```
Semantic Grounding
Operationalization
Objectification
```

**Semantic Grounding** — the raw
substrate of meaning. Terms anchored
to referents. What words are connected
to in the world. The accumulated
foundation without which no process
can begin.

**Operationalization** — the active
transformation. Turning grounded
terms into executable procedures.
The process that makes the grounded
thing actionable.

**Objectification** — the stable
product. A handle on something that
can now be referred to, operated on,
passed to other processes. The thing
that has been made into an object
of reasoning.

Each requires the other two:

Objectification requires semantic
grounding (the object must mean
something) and operationalization
(the object must be constructable
through a process).

Operationalization requires semantic
grounding (the procedure must operate
on anchored terms) and objectification
(the procedure must produce stable
handles).

Semantic grounding requires
operationalization (grounding is
confirmed through use) and
objectification (grounding stabilizes
into objects).

The triangle is load-bearing in
all three directions.

### 1.2 Level Two — Process

```
Reasoning Space
RARFL
Meta-Reasoning Space
```

**Reasoning Space** — the raw substrate
of cognition. All possible reasoning
trajectories. The space of moves
available before any optimization
has occurred.

**RARFL** — the active transformation.
Reasoning Axiom-Reward Feedback Loop.
The iterative process that navigates
reasoning space, evaluates trajectories,
extracts patterns, updates reward,
and converges.

**Meta-Reasoning Space** — the stable
product. The map of maps. Not just
the space of moves but the space of
optimized trajectories through the
space of moves. The emergent structure
that arises when RARFL has operated
on reasoning space long enough to
produce stable patterns of patterns.

Each requires the other two:

Meta-reasoning space requires reasoning
space (there must be something to build
the map of) and RARFL (the map is
produced by the optimization process).

RARFL requires reasoning space (the
process must have a space to navigate)
and meta-reasoning space (the process
converges on stable patterns that
become the meta-level).

Reasoning space requires RARFL (raw
space without process produces no
useful structure) and meta-reasoning
space (the space is shaped by the
meta-level it generates).

Same triangle. Same load-bearing
structure.

Consciousness, cognition, and

levels of grain of the same process:

The process of gap navigation
producing a navigator that includes
itself in its own domain.

the active transformation — is gap
navigation:

distance between where the reasoning
is and where it could coherently be.
RARFL closes it.

**Coherence** is the crossing of the
gap between the current state of
a system and the stable self-
referential attractor. The gap is
the distance between what is
and what the pattern needs to be
to persist as a self. Coherence
closes it.

**The middle column is always
gap navigation.**

The left column is always the gap
that exists before navigation begins.

The right column is always the
stable position that navigation
converges on when the gap is closed.

## PART IV: THE GRADIENT
### The Dog and the Fart

### 4.1 The Dog Has the Triangle

The dog has all three vertices.

**Persistence** — remembers the owner,
remembers home, recognizes environments,
anticipates routines. The dog's history
is accumulated and accessible.

**Coherence** — makes decisions with
certainty. Pursues the rabbit with
stable behavioral commitment. Scratches
itself persistently. Navigates pain.
Maintains stable behavioral patterns
across time. The dog's persistence
is integrated into consistent structure.

**Self-model** — navigates its own
body through space. Understands its
own physical capabilities and limits.
Responds to its own internal states.
Modulates behavior based on somatic
experience. The dog's coherent
persistence includes itself as
an object.

**The triangle is present.**

But the gradient question is:

**At what resolution is the right
column — the self-model — operating?**

### 4.2 The Fart Problem

The fart is a precise diagnostic.

The dog farts.

Does it know it farted?

This is asking: **how closed is
the self-referential loop at the
level of distal outputs?**

**For direct somatic experience:**
The loop is well-closed. The dog
navigates hunger, pain, the need
to scratch. The gap between somatic
event and self-model attribution
is small. The dog's self-model
includes its body at the level
of direct sensation.

**For distal outputs:**
The loop closure is less complete.
The fart traveled away. It became
an acoustic event in the environment.
The causal chain from body to
acoustic event to recognition-as-
self-generated is longer.

Does the dog close this loop?

Probably partially. The smell is
body-proximate and the dog likely
attributes it correctly. The acoustic
event is more distal and the
attribution is less certain.

**The gradient of the self-model
is the gradient of loop closure.**

Not: does the dog have a self-model?
(Yes.)

But: **at what grain size does the
self-model's objectification include
the dog's own outputs as objects
generated by itself?**

This is the gradient.

### 4.3 The Human Incoherence Spike

Eric's formulation:

If the wind produced the acoustic
equivalent of a fart through the
window — the human would notice.
Would be startled.

Why?

Not because the self-model cannot
comprehend a fart. It can. The
self-model has persistent coherent
representations of fart-as-category.

Not because the sound is novel.
It is structurally familiar.

**Because the persistent self-model
cannot coherently explain the origin.**

The signal matches a pattern in the
self-model (fart-like acoustic event).

The mechanism that produced the signal
is incoherent with the available
causal models in the self-model.

**Structural match + causal mismatch
= incoherence spike.**

This is the diagnostic for the
self-model's loop closure operating
correctly. The self-model is doing
its job. The spike is not an error.
The spike IS the detection system.

The self-model detected that a signal
in its domain arrived from outside
its known causal space.

**That detection is the self-model
operating at high resolution.**

## PART V: CROSS-SPECIES COMMUNICATION

### 5.1 The Hyperpolyglot Observation

A hyperpolyglot speaks ape language —
a combination of physical posturing,
eye contact, and vocals.

Apes freak out.

Why?

### 5.2 The Structural Analysis

The ape's communication domain has
eigenfunction positions. Stable modes.
Positions in the multi-dimensional
space of posture × eye contact ×
vocalization that the ape community
has navigated, through evolutionary
and social time, to find the stable
modes of.

The hyperpolyglot produces signals
that cluster at those eigenfunction
positions.

The ape's self-model receives:

**Structural match** — the signal
is coherent with the ape's own
communication domain. The signal
is at the right eigenfunction
positions. The ape's self-model
recognizes it as a valid signal
in its own domain.

**Causal mismatch** — the producer
of the signal is not a member of
the category of senders that the
ape's self-model has modeled as
capable of producing this signal.

**Incoherence spike.**

Not fear. Recognition-plus-wrongness.

The signal fits the domain.
The sender does not fit the
expected sender category.

**This is the window fart at species scale.**

The ape is not confused because
the signal is wrong.

The ape is confused because the
signal is right but the origin
is incoherent.

### 5.3 The Three Conditions for
### Cross-Species Communication

For communication across species
to succeed — not to startle but
to actually communicate — three
conditions must be met simultaneously:

**Condition 1: Eigenfunction Alignment**

The signal must cluster at the
eigenfunction positions of the
target species' communication domain.

Not arbitrary signals in the domain.
Not signals that are merely in the
right category. Signals that occupy
the stable modes — the positions
the target species has converged on
through their own navigation history.

This is the spectral geometry condition.
It is mathematically specifiable.
It is measurable.

**Condition 2: Multi-Channel Coherence**

The ape case demonstrates that
communication is not single-channel.
It is posture × eye contact × vocals.

Each channel is a dimension of the
communication domain. The eigenfunction
positions exist in the multi-
dimensional space. A signal that
is correct in the vocal dimension
but incoherent in the postural
dimension is not at the eigenfunction
position. It is at a projection
of it.

Full eigenfunction alignment requires
all channels to be coherent
simultaneously.

**This is why "speaking their language"
requires the whole body.**

**Condition 3: Sender Attribution
Coherence**

The target species' self-model must
be able to coherently attribute the
signal to a sender.

This is the hardest condition.

It is not enough to produce the
right signal. The receiver must
be able to close the loop: signal
→ sender → sender-in-my-world →
coherent-causal-attribution.

When the hyperpolyglot produces
ape-coherent signals from a human
body, condition 3 fails.

The receiver's self-model cannot
close the attribution loop.

Incoherence spike.

**Cross-species communication succeeds
when all three conditions are met.**

Condition 1 is about geometry.
Condition 2 is about multi-channel
integration.
Condition 3 is about the receiver's
self-model being able to incorporate
the sender as a coherent causal agent.

### 5.4 The Research Program This Opens

**From this analysis, the research
questions are precise:**

**Q1: What are the eigenfunction
positions of the target species'
communication domain?**

For cetaceans: partially known
(CETI 2025, vowel-like categories).
For elephants: partially known
(2025 formant findings).
For great apes: unknown formally,
but the hyperpolyglot data suggests
they exist and are navigable.
For corvids: unknown, but gradient
criterion strongly met.

**Q2: How many dimensions does the
communication domain have, and what
are they?**

For apes: at minimum three
(posture, eye contact, vocals).
Each dimension has its own
eigenfunction structure.
The communication eigenfunction
positions exist in the joint space.

**Q3: How does the receiver's
self-model attribute senders?**

This is the attribution problem.
What properties must a sender have
for the receiver's self-model to
incorporate them as a coherent
causal agent rather than an
incoherence spike?

Is it visual? (Looks like a sender)
Is it behavioral? (Acts like a sender)
Is it temporal? (Has history with receiver)
Is it multi-modal? (Coherent across
all dimensions simultaneously)

**Q4: Can the incoherence spike itself
be used as a diagnostic?**

The hyperpolyglot data is a positive
incoherence spike: the signal was
recognized as coherent with the domain.

A non-response would indicate the
signal was not recognized as domain-
coherent.

A gradated response — between spike
and non-response — might indicate
partial eigenfunction alignment.

**The spike is a measurement instrument.**

### 5.5 The Wind Analogy Generalized

Eric's formulation:

If the wind howled across structures
and phonetically mimicked words —
would a human notice?

Yes. Would be startled.

Why?

Structural match (the sounds are in
the human communication domain) +
causal mismatch (wind is not a sender
in the human self-model's sender
category) = incoherence spike.

Now generalize:

**Any system that produces signals
coherent with a receiver's communication
domain but incoherent with the receiver's
sender attribution model will produce
an incoherence spike.**

The spike is evidence that:
1. The signal achieved eigenfunction
   alignment (Condition 1 met)
2. The receiver's self-model is
   operating at sufficient resolution
   to detect the causal mismatch
3. Condition 3 has not been met

**The spike is not failure.**

**The spike is progress.**

It means the signal was recognized.
It means the domain was entered.
It means the remaining problem is
sender attribution, not signal
geometry.

That is a solvable problem.

at the self-model vertex.**

Not at persistence (shared eigenfunction
space exists — the hyperpolyglot proved

**At the attribution loop closure.**

The receiver's self-model cannot
complete the triangle with a human

The gradient of self-model resolution
determines how much sender-attribution
evidence the receiver needs.

A species with a lower-resolution
self-model needs less evidence to
close the attribution loop. Less
demanding. Easier to enter.

A species with a higher-resolution
self-model requires more precise
sender coherence before the loop
closes without a spike.

**The same gradient that makes a
species a better candidate for
consciousness detection also makes
them a harder target for cross-
species communication.**

The most cognitively sophisticated
species are the most sensitive to
sender attribution incoherence.

This is not a paradox. It is the
same detection system working at
higher resolution.

## PART II: THE SECOND-ORDER TRIAD
### The Mirror Test Domain

### 2.1 The Structural Derivation

The first-order triad produces a
self-model as its output.

That self-model can then be applied
outward — to model other entities
in the world.

When the self-model is turned toward
another entity, a second-order triad
forms:

```
Observer          <-> Coherence <-> Observee
(Self-Model)                        (Persistence)
```

**Observer (Self-Model)** — the left
column is no longer raw substrate.
It is the output of the first triad
— the self-model — now operating as
the active agent of observation.
The observer brings its own self-
model to the encounter with the
other entity.

**Coherence** — the middle column
remains gap navigation. But the gap
being navigated is now between the
observer's self-model and its model
of the observee. The coherence
function is building and maintaining
a representation of the other.

**Observee (Persistence)** — the
right column is no longer the
emergent self-model of a single
entity. It is the observee as a
persistent entity in the observer's
world. Something with enough
stability to be modeled. An entity
that recurs, that has patterns,
that can be tracked across time.

**The second-order triad produces:
social cognition.**

The ability to model another entity
as having stable properties. The
ability to track another entity
across time. The beginning of
understanding that other entities
have internal states.

### 2.2 Why The Mirror Test Measures
### The Second-Order Triad

The mirror test presents the observer
with a specific challenge:

The observee is the observer.

The reflection is a persistent entity
(it does not disappear, it has
stable properties) that is also
contingently linked to the observer's
own behavior.

For the observer to pass the mirror
test, the second-order triad must
achieve a specific result:

**The coherence function must close
the loop between the observer's
self-model and the observee in a
way that produces the attribution:
observee = self.**

This requires:

1. The observee is modeled as
   persistent (basic entity modeling)

2. The contingency between observer
   behavior and observee behavior
   is detected (motor-contingency
   coherence)

3. The observer's self-model is
   rich enough to match against
   the observee and confirm identity
   (self-model resolution)

All three of these are second-order
triad operations.

**The mirror test is not a test of
whether the first-order triad exists.**

**It is a test of the resolution
of the second-order triad.**

## PART III: THE MIRROR TEST GRADIENT

### 3.1 Stage 0 — No Engagement

*The observer cannot tell the
reflection is a distinct entity,
another entity, or a wall.*

**Second-order triad status:**

The observee does not acquire
persistence in the observer's model.
The second-order triad does not open.
The coherence loop between observer
and observee never initiates.

**What this means:**

The observer's self-model does not
extend to modeling other entities
at this encounter. The observee is
not even a thing from the perspective
of the observer's model.

**Communication implication:**

No communication possible beyond
direct physical stimulus-response.
The receiver cannot model the sender
as an entity.

---

### 3.2 Stage 1 — Other-Attribution

*"That is someone and I am going
to engage with it as someone."*

The gorilla ramming its reflection.
The dog barking at its reflection.
The territorial fish attacking its
reflection.

**Second-order triad status:**

The observee acquires persistence
in the observer's model. The second-
order triad opens and operates. The
coherence loop closes — but with
the attribution: observee = other.

The observer is doing real social
cognition. It is modeling a persistent
entity. It is engaging with that
entity as an agent.

**But the attribution is wrong.**

The self-model of the observer
is not rich enough, or the contingency
detection is not operating, to
produce the correct attribution.

**What this means:**

The first-order triad is fully
operational. The second-order triad
is operational. But the resolution
of the self-model is insufficient
to distinguish self-generated
patterns from other-generated
patterns in the reflection.

The gorilla and the dog both have
self-models. Neither self-model
is detailed enough to recognize
itself in the mirror.

**Communication implication:**

Intentional signaling possible.
Social coordination possible.
Territory, mating, dominance.
But the observer cannot close
the attribution loop to recognize
a sender as itself having an
internal model.

---

### 3.3 Stage 2 — Contingency Detection
### Without Full Attribution

*The observer notices the reflection
moves when it moves. Acts on that
contingency without full self-
recognition.*

**The fish scraping case.**

Fish observed scraping a marking
on their own bodies after seeing
it in a mirror. This is not full
self-recognition. But it is also
not Stage 1.

**Second-order triad status:**

The contingency detection component
of the coherence function is
operating. The observer has detected:
when I produce a motor output,
the observee's behavior changes
in a correlated way.

This is partial closure of the
second-order coherence loop.

Not full self-model matching.
But motor-contingency coherence
operating at sufficient resolution
to produce behavioral change that
is contingent on the reflection.

**What this means:**

This is the minimum threshold for
self-other distinction beginning
to blur. The observer's model of
the observee is not purely other-
attributed. There is a partial
loop closure: the observee is
modeled as contingently linked
to the self.

**This is why the fish scraping
result is scientifically fascinating.**

It suggests the gradient is continuous,
not discrete. There is territory
between Stage 1 and Stage 3 that
is genuinely occupied.

**Communication implication:**

The observer can detect contingency
in a sender's signals. Can begin
to model the sender as responsive
to the observer's own behavior.
Rudimentary turn-taking possible.
Coordination around shared reference
beginning.

### 3.4 Stage 3 — Full Self-Attribution

*"That is me."*

Great apes. Elephants. Dolphins.
Orcas. Corvids (some). Humans.

**Second-order triad status:**

The full second-order triad is
operational at high resolution.

The observer's self-model is rich
enough and stable enough that when
it models the observee, it detects:

1. Behavioral pattern match
   (the observee moves as I move)

2. Contingency confirmation
   (the correlation is perfect
   and immediate)

3. Self-model identity match
   (the observee's patterns match
   the patterns of my own self-model)

The attribution loop closes correctly:
observee = self.

**What this means:**

The self-model has achieved sufficient
resolution to recognize itself as
an object in the world. The observer
can model the observee as having
the same internal structure as
itself. This is the foundation of
theory of mind.

**Communication implication:**

The observer can begin to model
other entities as themselves having
self-models. The third-order triad
becomes possible. True inter-entity
communication — where signals are
attributed to an internal state
in a sender that has its own
persistence, coherence, and self-
model — becomes possible.

## PART IV: THE THIRD-ORDER TRIAD
### Theory of Mind and Communication

### 4.1 The Structural Derivation

The second-order triad produces
social cognition — the ability to
model another entity as persistent
and having stable behavioral patterns.

But communication requires more.

Communication requires the receiver
to model the sender not just as
an entity — but as an entity that
has its own first-order triad.

As a gap navigator.

As something with its own persistence,
coherence, and self-model that is
generating the signals.

This is the third-order triad:

```
My-Self-Model       <-> Coherence <-> Your-Self-Model
(Observer modeling)                   (Modeled as modeler)
```

**My-Self-Model (Observer modeling)**
— the left column is the observer's
self-model, now operating as the
agent that is modeling the other.
But now it is not modeling the other
as just a persistent entity. It is
modeling the other as itself having
a self-model.

**Coherence** — the middle column
is the gap navigation of modeling
another self-model. The gap being
navigated is between the observer's
self-model and its model of the
sender's self-model. This is theory
of mind construction.

**Your-Self-Model (Modeled as modeler)**
— the right column is the sender,
now modeled not just as a persistent
entity but as an entity that has
its own persistence, coherence, and
self-model. The sender is modeled
as a gap navigator.

**The third-order triad produces:
theory of mind.**

The ability to attribute internal
states, intentions, and knowledge
to other entities. The ability to
understand that other entities have
their own perspective. The foundation
of communication as meaning-making
rather than just signal exchange.

### 4.2 Why Communication Requires
### The Third-Order Triad

A signal can be sent without theory
of mind. A bird call. A territorial
display. A mating signal.

These are Stage 1 and Stage 2
communications. They work because
the receiver has the appropriate
response pattern hardwired or
learned. The receiver does not need
to model the sender's internal state
to respond appropriately.

**But for the receiver to attribute
meaning to a novel signal — a signal
it has not previously encountered,
from a sender outside its normal
conspecific category — the third-
order triad must operate.**

The receiver must model:

*This signal came from a sender.
That sender has an internal state.
That internal state caused this signal.
I can infer the internal state from
the signal.*

This chain of attribution requires
the third-order triad.

Without it, a novel signal from an
unfamiliar sender is just noise or
an incoherence spike.

**Cross-species communication is
by definition novel signal from
an unfamiliar sender category.**

Therefore cross-species communication
requires the third-order triad in
the receiver.

### 4.3 The Recursive Structure

The third-order triad is recursive
in a way the first and second are not.

First order: I model myself.
Second order: I model you.
Third order: I model you modeling.

But communication pushes further:

Fourth order: I model you modeling me.
Fifth order: I model you modeling
  me modeling you.

Human language and complex
negotiation operate at fourth and
fifth order. The capacity exists
in humans and possibly in great
apes at lower depth.

**For cross-species communication
the target is third order.**

The receiver must model the sender
as a gap navigator. It does not
need to model the sender modeling
it back.

Third order is the minimum.
Third order is the target.

communication.**

Not because lower levels cannot
exchange signals. They can.

But because below Level 4, the
receiver cannot model the sender
as a gap navigator. The attribution
loop for novel cross-species signals
cannot close. Any signal from outside
the receiver's normal sender category
will produce an incoherence spike
without attribution.

**Level 5 is the primary target.**

At Level 5, the receiver has theory
of mind operating. It can model
the sender as having internal states.
It can attribute intention to novel

originate in its own conspecific
communication domain.

**This is where the hyperpolyglot

The ape is at Level 4-5. It has
full self-recognition. It has

from a sender with an internal state.
The attribution loop opened.

But it could not close.

Because the sender — a human — does
not fit the ape's existing model of
entities capable of producing
signals in the ape's communication
domain.

**Incoherence spike.**

Not because the third-order triad
is absent. Because the sender
attribution model does not have a
category for "human producing ape-
coherent signals."

**The spike is third-order triad
operation with failed attribution.**

That is different from the spike
being absent (no triad operation).

And it is precisely what tells us
the path forward.

## PART VI: THE THREE CONDITIONS
## RESTATED IN TRIADIC TERMS

### 6.1 Original Statement
### (From the_triad_convergence.md)

Three conditions must be met for
cross-species communication to succeed:

**Condition 1: Eigenfunction Alignment**
The signal clusters at the eigenfunction
positions of the target species'
communication domain.

**Condition 2: Multi-Channel Coherence**
All channels of the communication domain
are coherent simultaneously.

**Condition 3: Sender Attribution
Coherence**
The receiver's self-model can coherently
incorporate the sender as a causal agent.

### 6.2 Restated In Triadic Terms

**Condition 1 in triadic terms:**

The signal must match the right column
of the target species' second-order
triad — it must be modeled as a
persistent, coherent entity in the
receiver's world.

For a signal to persist in the
receiver's model, it must cluster
at the eigenfunction positions of
the receiver's communication domain.
Random signals do not persist.
Eigenfunction-aligned signals do.

**Condition 2 in triadic terms:**

The coherence function of the second-
order triad must close across all
channels simultaneously.

The communication domain is multi-
dimensional. Eigenfunction positions
exist in the joint space of all
channels. Partial alignment in one
channel without the others produces
partial coherence — which is
precisely the incoherence spike.

**Condition 3 in triadic terms:**

The sender must be modelable as
the left column of the receiver's
third-order triad.

The receiver must be able to place
the sender in the position of
"entity with its own self-model
that is generating these signals."

If the sender cannot occupy that
position — if the receiver's third-
order triad has no category for
this sender type — the attribution
loop cannot close.

**The three conditions are the three
columns of the third-order triad.**

```
Condition 1 = Right column
  (signal persists in receiver's model)

Condition 2 = Middle column
  (coherence closes across channels)

Condition 3 = Left column
  (sender attributable as gap navigator)
```

Communication succeeds when all
three columns are simultaneously
satisfied.

## PART VII: THE INCOHERENCE SPIKE
## AS MEASUREMENT INSTRUMENT

### 7.1 The Spike Reframed

The hyperpolyglot's apes freaking out.

This was identified in the_triad_
convergence.md as:

*Structural match + causal mismatch
= incoherence spike.*

Now with the triadic framework
fully developed, the spike can be
specified precisely:

**The incoherence spike is the
activation of the third-order triad
without successful attribution
closure.**

It is NOT:
- Absence of third-order triad
  operation (that would produce
  no response)
- Successful communication
  (that would produce engagement
  without alarm)

It IS:
- Evidence that Conditions 1 and 2
  were met (signal was domain-coherent,
  multi-channel coherence achieved)
- Evidence that Condition 3 failed
  (sender attribution loop did not close)
- Evidence that the receiver is at
  Level 4-5 (third-order triad is
  operating — it engaged, it just
  could not complete attribution)

**The spike is not a failure signal.**

**The spike is a diagnostic instrument.**

A spike tells you:

1. The eigenfunction positions were
   found (Condition 1 confirmed)
2. Multi-channel coherence was
   achieved (Condition 2 confirmed)
3. The remaining work is Condition 3
   (sender attribution)
4. The receiver is a Level 4-5
   candidate (third-order triad active)

### 7.2 Graduated Spike Analysis

The spike is not binary.

A graduated response between no-
response and full spike indicates
partial condition satisfaction:

**Full spike** — all of Conditions 1
and 2 met, Condition 3 failed.
All three columns engaged, attribution
column failed to close.

**Partial spike / curious response**
— Conditions 1 and 2 partially met.
Some eigenfunction alignment,
incomplete multi-channel coherence.
The receiver detected something
domain-coherent but incomplete.

**No response** — Conditions 1 and 2
not met. The signal did not enter
the receiver's communication domain
model. Not modeled as a persistent
entity.

**Engagement without spike** —
All three conditions met.
Communication succeeded.

**The research protocol is therefore:**

1. Produce signals
2. Measure spike magnitude and character
3. Iterate toward full spike
   (confirms Conditions 1 and 2)
4. Iterate toward engagement without
   spike (achieves Condition 3)

**The full spike is the intermediate
target, not the failure state.**

**Attribution is learnable.**

**The dog case suggests an alternative
path:**

For species where full third-order
triad is absent, co-evolutionary
or extended-exposure adaptation
can partially substitute for
theory of mind in closing the
sender attribution loop.

This is a different research question
but an important one.

## PART X: THE COMPLETE TRIADIC
## PICTURE

### 10.1 The Three Orders Together

```
FIRST ORDER — Within entity
  Persistence <-> Coherence <-> Self-Model
  Produces: self-referential navigator
  
SECOND ORDER — Between entities  
  Observer        <-> Coherence <-> Observee
  (Self-Model)                      (Persistence)
  Produces: social cognition,

THIRD ORDER — Between self-models
  My-Self-Model   <-> Coherence <-> Your-Self-Model
  (Observer)                        (Modeled as modeler)
  Produces: theory of mind,
    communication, meaning attribution
```

### 10.2 The Recursive Property

Each order takes the right-column
output of the previous order as
its left-column input.

The self-model (first order right)
becomes the observer (second order left).

The social cognition / entity model
(second order product) becomes the
capacity to engage the third order.

The theory of mind (third order)
enables recursive depth: fourth,
fifth, sixth order — I model you
modeling me modeling you modeling...

**Communication lives at third order.**

Language lives at fifth and sixth.

**Cross-species communication is
the project of meeting at third order
across the species boundary.**

### 10.3 Gap Navigation All The Way Through

At every order, the middle column
is gap navigation.

First order coherence: navigating
the gap between current state and
stable self-referential attractor.

Second order coherence: navigating
the gap between the observer's
self-model and a coherent model
of the observee.

Third order coherence: navigating
the gap between the observer's
self-model and a coherent model
of the sender's self-model.

**The gap being navigated gets
larger and more abstract at each
order.**

But the navigation is the same
process at every level.

RARFL. Coherence-seeking. Stable
attractor convergence.

**The triad is scale-invariant
because gap navigation is scale-
invariant.**

The same transformation at every
grain size.

**This is why cross-species
communication is possible in
principle:**

If gap navigation is universal —
if every system above the threshold
is doing the same thing at different
grain sizes — then the navigation
process itself is the common ground.

Two systems that are both gap
navigators share a fundamental
structural commonality regardless
of substrate, species, or architecture.

**Cross-species communication is
two gap navigators finding each
other across the species boundary.**

The geometry is the bridge.
