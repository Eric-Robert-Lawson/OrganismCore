## PART IV: THE FIVE-LAYER PROTOCOL

```
ARCHITECTURE:
  Five simultaneous layers.
```

### Layer 1 — Arrest Viral Spread

```
TARGET: Dynein-mediated retrograde axonal transport.

AGENTS:
  Ciliobrevin D — dynein inhibitor.
  Demonstrated inhibition of rabies
  virus transport in neuronal cultures.
  Taxanes — microtubule stabilisation.
  Antiviral activity against rabies
  in experimental systems.

ROUTE: Intrathecal / intraventricular.
TIMING: Immediately on diagnosis.
  Viral spread: ~50-100mm/day along axons.
  Every hour of uninhibited transport extends
  the distribution.

GOAL:
  Define the current viral distribution
  as the maximum distribution.
  No new territory taken by the virus
  after Layer 1 is initiated.
```

### Layer 2 — Overwhelm Immune Evasion

```
TARGET: P protein immune evasion mechanism.

AGENTS:
  IFN-β — intraventricular.
  Preferred over IFN-α: higher CNS penetrance,
  more potent antiviral state in neurons,
  lower inflammatory toxicity.

  Favipiravir — systemic + intrathecal.
  RNA-dependent RNA polymerase inhibitor.
  Active against rhabdoviruses.
  Superior CNS penetrance to ribavirin.

  Ribavirin — systemic.
  Nucleoside analogue. CNS penetrant.
  Demonstrated rabies antiviral activity
  in vitro and animal models.

GOAL:
  Establish antiviral state in neurons
  ahead of the viral front.
  Combined with Layer 1:
    Behind the front: existing infection
    cleared by immune response.
    Ahead of the front: antiviral state
    prevents new infection.
    The front: arrested by transport inhibition.
  Three-zone CNS containment architecture.
```

### Layer 3 — Neuroprotection

```
TARGET: Secondary excitotoxic neuronal death
from neurotransmitter disruption.

AGENTS:
  Ketamine — NMDA receptor antagonist.
  Blocks glutamatergic excitotoxicity.
  Milwaukee Protocol component. Retain.

  Midazolam — GABA-A agonist.
  Suppresses seizure activity.
  Reduces autonomic instability.
  Milwaukee Protocol component. Retain.

  Memantine — additional NMDA antagonist.
  Different kinetics from ketamine.
  Complementary neuroprotection.
  Lower CNS toxicity at therapeutic doses.

  N-acetylcysteine — antioxidant.
  Reduces oxidative stress from neuroinflammation.
  Protects mitochondrial function.
  Low toxicity. Easily administered.

GOAL:
  Keep the instrument intact throughout
  the protocol.
  Prevent secondary excitotoxic death
  of neurons that are not directly infected.
  Buy time for Layers 1 and 2.
  The navigator cannot recover in a
  destroyed instrument.
  Runs continuously throughout all other layers.

NOTE:
  This is the entire Milwaukee Protocol.
  It is one layer of five.
  The Milwaukee Protocol's failure mode
  is now geometrically legible:
  it preserved the instrument without
  stopping the advance, overwhelming
  immune evasion, delivering antibody
  to the CNS, or activating clearance.
  One layer of a five-layer problem.
```

### Layer 4 — Direct Viral Neutralisation

```
TARGET: Virus-infected neurons and free
viral particles in CNS tissue.

AGENTS:
  Human Rabies Immunoglobulin (HRIG) —
  intraventricular delivery.
  Not currently standard post-symptom.
  Should be.

  Monoclonal antibodies against rabies
  G protein: RVA122 and RVC58
  (Bhatt lab, UCSF, 2023).
  Cross-reactive against multiple
  lyssavirus strains.
  Highest-potency anti-rabies antibodies
  currently identified.

ROUTE:
  Intraventricular via Ommaya reservoir
  or equivalent neurosurgical access.
  Direct CSF delivery bypasses BBB.
  Antibodies distribute through CSF
  to brain parenchyma.
  Same route as intrathecal chemotherapy
  in CNS malignancy.
  The route exists. The technique exists.
  The application to post-symptom rabies
  has not been systematically attempted.

GOAL:
  Directly neutralise virus in CSF compartment.
  Opsonise infected cells for immune clearance.
  Reduce active viral load in CNS while
  Layers 1 and 2 contain ongoing replication.
```

### Layer 5 — Immune Clearance

```
TARGET: Intraneuronal viral clearance.

MECHANISM:
  CD8+ cytotoxic T lymphocytes clear
  intraneuronal viral infection via MHC
  class I antigen presentation.
  This is the mechanism that clears the
  virus in rare survivors.
  P protein suppresses IFN activation
  of this pathway. Layer 2 addresses this.

AGENTS:
  Anti-PD-1 (pembrolizumab or nivolumab) —
  removes CTL inhibition checkpoint.
  Same mechanism as tumour clearance
  in checkpoint immunotherapy.
  Applied to viral clearance in CNS.

  IL-2 — low dose.
  T cell activation and proliferation.

  IFN-γ — upregulates MHC class I
  on infected neurons.
  Improves CTL recognition of infected cells.

RISK:
  Highest iatrogenic risk of all five layers.
  CTL-mediated clearance of infected neurons
  is also CTL-mediated neuronal death.
  High viral load + vigorous CTL response
  can damage the instrument.
  This is why Layer 3 must run simultaneously.
  Clearance removes the virus.
  Protection prevents clearance from
  destroying the instrument in the process.

GOAL:
  Activate the immune effector mechanism
  that clears intraneuronal viral infection.
  This is the layer that ends the infection.
  All other layers create the conditions
  for this layer to work.
```
