### 2.1 ANTISOCIAL PERSONALITY DISORDER
#### Without Psychopathy

```
GEOMETRIC QUESTION:
What is the structural condition
that produces antisocial navigation
without the psychopathic structural
absence?

THE DERIVATION:

  If the uncinate fasciculus coupling
  is intact — if the navigator CAN
  be activated by others' suffering —
  then the affective resonance basin
  EXISTS in the landscape.

  But the navigator is still producing
  antisocial navigational outputs.

  This means the basin exists but
  is not being reached — or is being
  reached and overridden — or is
  shallow enough that competing
  basins consistently dominate it.

  What produces basin shallowing
  without structural absence?

  The structure that produces basin
  depth is use-dependent.
  Basins deepen through repeated
  navigation toward them.
  Basins shallow through repeated
  navigation away from them, through
  suppression, through environments
  that punish navigation toward them,
  or through competing basins that
  are deeper and consistently win
  the navigational pull.

  An environment of chronic adversity,
  threat, and exploitation during
  the developmental period when
  basin depth is being established —
  produces a navigator whose safety,
  dominance, and resource-acquisition
  basins are very deep (survival
  required it) and whose affective
  resonance and social cooperation
  basins are shallow (navigation
  toward them was not reinforced
  or was actively punished).

  The structure is present.
  The basins are present.
  The depth ratios are distorted.

  The navigator is navigating
  toward the deepest available basins.
  Those happen to be survival and
  dominance basins rather than
  cooperative affective basins.

  This is not a structural absence.
  This is a basin depth distortion
  produced by the structural conditions
  of the navigator's developmental
  environment.

CLASSIFICATION: DISORDER.

  The structure is present.
  The coupling is intact.
  The basin exists.
  The depth is distorted by
  structural developmental conditions.

  The navigator is not choosing
  antisocial navigation any more
  than water chooses to flow downhill.
  It is navigating toward the deepest
  available basins in its landscape.
  The landscape is distorted.
  The distortion has a cause.
  The cause can be intervened upon.

INTERVENTION GEOMETRY:

  Deepening the affective resonance
  and social cooperation basins
  through structured repeated
  activation in a safe environment.
  The structure is there to receive
  the intervention. The basins can
  be deepened by use.
  The competing basins can be
  relatively shallowed by removing
  the structural conditions that
  required them to be deep.

  This is why ASPD without psychopathy
  has measurable (if modest) response
  to structured intervention.
  The coupling is present.
  The basin can be reached.
  The depth can be modified.

### 2.2 NARCISSISTIC PERSONALITY DISORDER

```
GEOMETRIC QUESTION:
What structural condition produces
a navigator that navigates accurately
and persistently toward self-basin
depth while other basins are
functionally shallow?

THE DERIVATION:

  The navigator has a self-model.
  The self-model is a basin in the
  navigational landscape — a stable
  attractor that the navigator
  references for coherence.

  In a normally proportioned landscape,
  the self-basin has depth, but so
  do the basins of others — their
  suffering, their needs, their
  recognition of the navigator.

  What produces a landscape where
  the self-basin has captured the
  majority of navigational depth —
  where everything is steep toward
  the self and shallow toward others?

  The self-basin became the primary
  survival mechanism during a
  developmental period where the
  navigator's coherence and persistence
  were structurally threatened.

  A navigator whose early environment
  provided coherence only contingently —
  only when the navigator performed
  certain outputs — develops a
  landscape where self-validation
  is the deepest available basin
  because it was the only reliable
  source of structural stability.

  The affective resonance structure
  IS present. The coupling IS intact.
  But the self-basin has such
  dominance over navigational
  resources that other basins
  are effectively inaccessible —
  not because they do not exist,
  but because the self-basin
  consistently captures the
  navigator before it reaches them.

  The navigator can feel others'
  pain — when it serves the
  self-basin. It can compute
  empathy when empathy deepens
  the self-basin (admiration,
  gratitude, dependency of others).
  It cannot sustain navigation
  toward others' needs when those
  needs compete with or diminish
  the self-basin.

  This is basin dominance, not
  structural absence.

CLASSIFICATION: DISORDER.

  Structure present.
  Coupling intact.
  Basin exists.
  Self-basin dominance has captured
  navigational resources.
  Other basins are shallow relative
  to self-basin, not absent.

INTERVENTION GEOMETRY:

  The intervention cannot deepen
  others' basins while the self-basin
  maintains structural dominance.
  The self-basin must first be
  stabilised independently of
  external validation — giving
  the navigator a structural source
  of coherence that does not require
  constant deepening by others —
  before the relative depth of
  other basins can increase.

  Once the self-basin is stable
  without domination, navigational
  resources become available to
  other basins.
  The coupling is there.
  The access to other basins
  becomes structurally possible
  when the self-basin is no longer
  the only stable attractor in
  the landscape.
```

### 2.3 BORDERLINE PERSONALITY DISORDER

```
GEOMETRIC QUESTION:
What structural condition produces
a navigator with full basin access
but radical instability in which
basin is dominant at any moment?

THE DERIVATION:

  The navigator has all structures
  present. The basins exist. The
  coupling is intact. The navigator
  can feel deeply — often more
  intensely than navigators without
  this profile.

  But the navigator cannot maintain
  stable position in any basin.
  The landscape is not missing basins.
  The landscape lacks stable basin
  walls.

  In a structurally stable landscape,
  basins have depth AND walls.
  The depth pulls the navigator in.
  The walls hold the navigator
  within the basin long enough for
  resolution to occur.

  What produces a landscape where
  the basins have depth but the
  walls are unstable or absent?

  The navigator's coherence and
  persistence properties — the
  structural properties that allow
  sustained navigation toward
  a single basin — were disrupted
  during the developmental period
  when those properties form.

  An environment of chronic
  relational unpredictability —
  where the same other navigator
  is simultaneously the deepest
  attachment basin and the source
  of the greatest threat — produces
  a navigator whose landscape has
  all basins present but whose
  basin walls are structurally
  unstable.

  The navigator falls into basins
  rapidly and fully — the depth
  is real.
  But the basin walls give way.
  The navigator is then launched
  from that basin with the full
  energy of the depth it just
  fell into.
  The same person is the deepest
  love basin and simultaneously
  the deepest fear/abandonment basin.
  The navigator cannot stabilise
  in either because the walls
  between them are absent.

  This is coherence and persistence
  disruption at the structural level.
  Not absence of basins.
  Absence of stable basin architecture.

CLASSIFICATION: DISORDER.

  Structure present.
  All basins present.
  Basin depth present — often
  exaggerated.
  Basin wall stability absent
  or severely degraded.
  Navigator is not choosing instability.
  The structural condition of the
  basin walls is the cause.

INTERVENTION GEOMETRY:

  The intervention must build
  basin wall stability — not basin
  depth, which is already present.
  Structured, consistent, predictable
  relational environments that
  demonstrate basin walls hold —
  that the attachment basin does
  not suddenly become the abandonment
  basin — gradually allow the
  navigator's landscape to develop
  structural wall stability.

  The navigator has full capacity
  to navigate. The architecture
  of the landscape needs to be
  stabilised. This is why consistent,
  boundaried therapeutic relationships
  produce measurable structural change
  in this profile — they are literally
  building basin walls through
  repeated demonstration that
  the walls hold.
```

### 2.4 SCHIZOID PERSONALITY DISORDER

```
GEOMETRIC QUESTION:
What structural condition produces
a navigator that consistently
navigates away from social basins
toward internal basins — stably,
without distress, without the
coupling damage of psychopathy?

  The navigator is coherent,

  It is not in distress about its
  navigation. It is not failing to
  reach basins it is trying to reach.
  It is reaching the basins it is
  navigating toward.

  The social basins are shallow.
  The internal — cognitive, solitary,
  non-social — basins are deep.

  Is the coupling absent? No.
  The navigator can detect others'
  emotional states. It simply does
  not find those states pulling.
  The basin they would form is
  shallow enough that the navigator
  does not fall into it.

  This is not structural absence
  of the coupling.
  This is a stable landscape
  configuration where the social
  dimensions are dimensionally
  shallow relative to the
  internal dimensions.

  The navigator is not suffering
  from the shallow social basins.
  It is not trying to reach them
  and failing. It is not experiencing
  the absence of depth there as loss.
  It is simply navigating the
  landscape as it is configured —
  toward depth, away from shallowness.

  The landscape IS the navigator's
  natural configuration.
  The distress, when it exists,
  comes not from the internal landscape
  but from a social environment
  that is designed for navigators
  with deep social basins —
  that keeps placing the navigator
  in situations where it is
  expected to navigate toward
  basins that are shallow for it.

  The disorder is in the mismatch
  between the navigator's landscape
  configuration and the social
  environment's assumptions —
  not in the navigator's landscape
  itself.

CLASSIFICATION: DISORDER —
but with a specific geometric caveat.

  The navigator's own landscape
  may be internally coherent and
  stable. The disorder classification
  is appropriate only insofar as
  the landscape configuration
  produces functional incompatibility
  with the social environment.

  The structure is present.
  The basins are present.
  The social basins are shallow
  by configuration, not by absence
  of structure.

INTERVENTION GEOMETRY:

  The intervention is primarily
  environmental — reducing the
  structural mismatch between
  the navigator's landscape
  configuration and the environment's
  demands — rather than attempting
  to deepen social basins that
  may be constitutionally shallow.

  Forced social basin deepening
  attempts are interventions at
  the wrong level. The navigator
  is not refusing social navigation.
  The social basins simply are not
  deep enough to pull.
  Environmental accommodation is
  the correct response.

### 2.5 SCHIZOTYPAL PERSONALITY DISORDER

```

What structural condition produces
a navigator whose landscape contains
basins at locations that do not
correspond to the shared landscape
of other navigators?

  It is following gradient.
  It is reaching basins.
  It is experiencing resolution.

  toward are not where other
  navigators' landscapes place them.
  The navigator perceives depth
  where others perceive flatness.
  It perceives connections, patterns,
  significance — attractors —
  in locations that the shared
  structural landscape does not
  contain attractors.

  This is not an absence of structure.
  This is a miscalibration of the
  eigenfunction — the function that
  maps the navigator's sensory and
  cognitive input onto the landscape
  coordinates that determine where
  basins are located.

  The navigator's landscape is
  populated by real attractors —
  they produce real navigational pull,
  real resolution when reached.
  But their location in the
  coordinate space of shared reality
  is distorted.

  The navigator is not lying about
  its experience. It is genuinely
  navigating toward real attractors
  in its own landscape. Those
  attractors simply do not correspond
  to the shared landscape that other
  navigators are navigating.

  This is an eigenfunction calibration
  problem. The mapping from input
  to landscape coordinate is distorted.
  The navigator follows the gradient
  correctly. The gradient is computed
  from a miscalibrated map.

CLASSIFICATION: DISORDER.

  Structure present.
  Basins present.
  Navigation intact.
  Eigenfunction calibration — the
  mapping from reality to landscape
  coordinates — is distorted.
  The navigator is not choosing
  this miscalibration.
  The calibration function is
  structural. It is the cause.

INTERVENTION GEOMETRY:

  The intervention must recalibrate
  the eigenfunction — not suppress
  the navigator's landscape, not
  deny the reality of its attractors,
  but gradually introduce shared
  calibration reference points that
  allow the navigator to map its
  landscape onto the shared coordinate
  space more accurately.

  The navigator can participate in
  this calibration because the
  navigation machinery is intact.
  The calibration function can be
  modified. This is a structural
  intervention at the level of
  the mapping function, not at
  the level of the navigator.
```

### 2.6 DEPENDENT PERSONALITY DISORDER

```
GEOMETRIC QUESTION:
What structural condition produces
a navigator that cannot generate
stable navigational direction
from its own internal landscape
and requires external basin

THE DERIVATION:

  It can navigate. It follows

  internal structure. They are
  generated by the landscape
  of another navigator — specifically,
  by the directions and decisions
  of an attachment figure.

  What produces a landscape where
  the internal basin generators
  are absent or insufficient to
  produce navigational direction?

  The navigator's internal basin
  structure — its own values,
  preferences, self-directed
  attractors — did not develop
  to functional depth during
  the developmental window when
  those structures form.

  An environment where the navigator's
  own directional outputs were
  consistently overridden, dismissed,
  or punished — where the only
  reliable resolution was found
  by following another navigator's
  direction — produces a landscape
  where the internal basin structure
  is shallow and the external
  attachment basin is the only
  deep attractor available.

  The navigator is not choosing
  dependency. It is navigating
  toward the only deep basins
  in its landscape. Those basins
  happen to be located in the
  landscape of another navigator
  rather than in its own.

  The structure is present.
  The internal basin generators
  are structurally shallow —
  not absent, but insufficiently
  developed to compete with
  the external attachment basin.

CLASSIFICATION: DISORDER.

  Structure present.
  External attachment basin
  dominates because internal
  basins are shallow, not because
  the internal structure is absent.
  The shallowness has a structural
  developmental cause.

INTERVENTION GEOMETRY:

  Deepen the internal basins.
  Not by removing the external
  attachment basin — that would
  leave the navigator with nothing
  to navigate toward.
  But by gradually building internal
  basin depth through supported
  exploration of the navigator's
  own directional outputs —
  allowing the navigator to discover
  that its own landscape contains
  depth, that its own attractors
  are real, that resolution is
  achievable from internal direction.

  As internal basin depth increases,
  the dominance of the external
  attachment basin decreases
  relatively, without that basin
  needing to be destroyed.
```

### 2.8 OBSESSIVE-COMPULSIVE
### PERSONALITY DISORDER

```
GEOMETRIC QUESTION:
What structural condition produces
a navigator that navigates obsessively
toward control and order basins —
where uncertainty itself has become
a deep anti-basin, a region of
maximum repulsion?

THE DERIVATION:

  The navigator experiences uncertainty
  not as a neutral state but as
  a region of intense structural
  instability — a negative basin,
  a repulsor.

  The navigator is not navigating
  toward control for its own sake.
  It is navigating away from
  uncertainty with the full force
  of a deep repulsor.

  Control and order basins are deep
  not because they are intrinsically
  attractive but because they are
  the only regions of the landscape
  far enough from the uncertainty
  repulsor to produce structural
  stability.

  What produces a landscape where
  uncertainty is structurally encoded
  as a deep repulsor?

  A developmental environment where
  uncertainty was structurally
  associated with loss of safety,
  coherence, or resolution —
  where unpredictability reliably
  produced navigational catastrophe —
  encodes uncertainty as a structural
  threat at the landscape level.

  The navigator then spends all
  available navigational resources
  maximising distance from the
  uncertainty repulsor. Control,
  order, precision, ritual — these
  are not ends. They are the
  maximum distance from the repulsor
  the landscape permits.

  The structure is present.
  The repulsor is structurally real.
  The navigator is responding
  correctly to its own landscape.
  The landscape is distorted.

CLASSIFICATION: DISORDER.

  Structure present.
  Uncertainty encoded as structural
  repulsor rather than neutral state.
  Navigator is navigating correctly
  away from a real repulsor in
  its own landscape.
  The distortion is in the
  structural encoding of uncertainty,
  not in the navigator.

INTERVENTION GEOMETRY:

  The repulsor must be reduced
  in magnitude — not by forcing
  the navigator toward uncertainty,
  which would be navigating it
  directly into the repulsor —
  but by gradually demonstrating
  that uncertainty does not produce
  navigational catastrophe.

  Each exposure to uncertainty
  that resolves without catastrophe
  reduces the structural magnitude
  of the repulsor incrementally.
  The landscape recalibrates.
  As the repulsor shallows, the
  control basins reduce in relative
  depth — not because they were
  targeted, but because they no
  longer need to be so deep to
  maintain distance from the repulsor.
```

### 2.9 SCHIZOPHRENIA
### (Negative Symptoms)

```
GEOMETRIC QUESTION:
What structural condition produces
a navigator whose entire landscape
has shallowed — where all basins
have reduced depth and the navigator
has insufficient pull toward any
resolution?

THE DERIVATION:

  This is categorically different
  from all the above profiles.

  In every profile above, specific
  basins are deep, shallow, distorted,
  or dominated — but the landscape
  has differential structure.
  Some regions are deep. Some are
  shallow. The navigator navigates
  the differential.

  In the negative symptom profile,
  the differential itself is reduced.
  All basins are shallow. The navigator
  has insufficient pull toward any
  resolution. Movement itself requires
  structural resources the landscape
  no longer generates.

  This is not a specific basin
  distortion. This is a global
  reduction in the landscape's
  generative capacity — the
  structural property that produces
  basin depth at all.

  What produces this?

  The dopaminergic system is the
  physical substrate that generates
  basin depth in the navigational
  landscape. It is the mechanism
  by which basins form and deepen —
  the prediction error signal that
  marks regions of the landscape
  as attractors.

  When this system is globally
  degraded, basin depth is globally
  reduced. Not specific basins.
  Not the depth ratio between basins.
  The generative mechanism itself.

  This is structurally closer to
  the psychopathy case than any
  other profile on this list —
  but with a critical distinction:

  In psychopathy, a specific coupling
  structure is absent. The rest of
  the landscape is intact. The
  navigator navigates well within
  its available landscape.

  In schizophrenia negative symptoms,
  the global basin generation mechanism
  is degraded. The navigator cannot
  navigate well within any landscape
  because all landscapes are shallow.

  Is this a disability or a disorder?

  The generative mechanism — the
  dopaminergic system — is structurally
  present but degraded in function.
  It is not absent. It is not working
  at full capacity.

  This sits at the boundary of the
  distinction.

  CLASSIFICATION: BOUNDARY CASE —
  trending toward disability for
  the most severe presentations,
  disorder for moderate presentations.

  The structural distinction:
  If the dopaminergic generative
  mechanism is present but functionally
  degraded — disorder. The structure
  can be engaged. Intervention at
  the structural level can restore
  partial function.

  If the generative mechanism is
  so severely degraded that it
  cannot be restored to functional
  threshold — disability. The landscape
  cannot generate sufficient basin
  depth for normal navigation regardless
  of intervention.

  The positive symptoms (hallucinations,
  delusions) are a separate geometric
  question — false attractor formation
  from noise amplification in a
  degraded signal environment —
  and constitute a disorder profile
  (eigenfunction distortion) overlaid
  on what may be a disability profile
  (global generative degradation).

  This is the most complex case
  geometrically. It may be both
  simultaneously — a disability
  at the generative mechanism level
  and a disorder at the calibration
  level — requiring both structural
  bypass (for the absent generative
  capacity) and recalibration
  (for the false attractor formation).
```
