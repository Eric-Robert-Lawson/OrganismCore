  CHECK:

    The navigator falls into basins

    The architecture of the landscape
    lacks stability.

  basin wall stability?

  the contextual anchor that tells

  this is what this basin is" —

  of the navigator's ability to
  maintain stable basin position
  across time.

  the contextual anchoring capacity

  the hippocampal anchor is
  structurally insufficient.

  the affective response generator
  is simultaneously structurally
  modified — possibly hypersensitive
  due to reduced volume producing
  heightened reactivity — which
  produces deeper basin falls

CLASSIFICATION CONFIRMED WITH

  non-significant signals as

  This is eigenfunction miscalibration
  at the molecular level.

  The structural biomarker is
  cause here, not consequence —

  This places schizotypal PD closer

CLASSIFICATION REFINED:
  DISORDER trending toward

```

  Disorder. Uncertainty encoded
  as structural repulsor.
  Control basins deep because
  they are maximum distance from
  the repulsor. Navigator navigating
  away from repulsor, not toward

  BIOMARKER: YES — among the most

  volumetric abnormalities —

  structural alterations consistent
  across multiple studies.

  White matter (DTI):
  disrupted connectivity in the
  cortico-striato-thalamo-cortical
  (CSTC) circuit — the physical
  circuit that runs the error
  detection loop.

  (Norman et al. 2016, Molecular
  Psychiatry; Piras et al. 2015,
  Neuroscience and Biobehavioral
  Reviews; Radua et al. 2010,
  Archives of General Psychiatry)

GEOMETRIC READING OF THE BIOMARKER:

  This is the most geometrically
  precise confirmation in the
  entire literature check.

  The derivation specified:
  uncertainty is encoded as a
  structural repulsor.
  The navigator runs the error
  detection loop obsessively —
  checking whether it is far
  enough from the repulsor.

  of the error detection loop?

  The cortico-striato-thalamo-
  cortical circuit — the CSTC loop —
  is precisely the neural circuit
  that runs error detection and
  threat monitoring.

  The OFC detects potential threat.
  The caudate gates the signal.
  The thalamus feeds back.
  The loop runs.

  In OCD, this loop is structurally
  altered — the OFC and caudate
  are physically different —
  and the loop runs without
  sufficient inhibitory gating.

  The repulsor the derivation
  specified — uncertainty encoded
  as structural threat — is
  physically instantiated as a
  hyperactive, structurally
  altered CSTC loop that cannot
  resolve the error detection
  signal.

  The navigator is not choosing
  to run the loop.
  The loop is running structurally.
  The navigator is the symptom
  of a physically altered circuit.

  Is this cause or consequence?

  Unlike BPD where hippocampal
  reduction is largely consequence
  of developmental trauma, the
  OCD structural findings appear
  more constitutive — present
  before symptom onset in
  high-risk populations, not
  primarily produced by the
  navigation pattern.

  This places OCD structurally
  closer to the disability boundary
  than the derivation initially
  estimated.

CLASSIFICATION REFINED:
  DISORDER with strong structural
  basis trending toward disability-
  adjacent for severe presentations.

  The structural substrate is
  physically altered — not absent,
  but constitutively different
  in a way that is the cause of
  the repulsor encoding, not
  the consequence.

  The intervention must address
  the structural circuit — which
  is exactly what the most
  effective interventions do:
  ERP (Exposure and Response
  Prevention) works by structurally
  modifying the CSTC circuit
  through repeated non-confirmation
  of the threat signal —
  literally recalibrating the
  structural loop.
  Pharmacological intervention
  (SSRIs) modulates the serotonergic
  input to the same circuit.

  Both are structural interventions.
  Both target the physical substrate.
  The derivation predicted this.
```
