## PART I: THE GEOMETRIC VOCABULARY

```
Before mapping the profiles,
the vocabulary must be precise.

BASIN DEPTH:
  The strength of pull of an
  attractor state. How strongly
  the landscape draws the navigator
  toward a particular resolution.
  Deep basin = strong pull,
    high stability, difficult to
    leave once entered.
  Shallow basin = weak pull,
    low stability, easily perturbed
    or never entered.

BASIN ABSENCE:
  A basin that does not exist
  in the navigator's accessible
  landscape. Not shallow —
  absent. The eigenfunction
  position is not present in
  the navigator's Tonnetz.
  The navigator cannot navigate
  toward what does not exist
  in its landscape.
  Critically: the navigator
  cannot detect the absence.
  It simply has no pull
  from that direction.

BASIN INSTABILITY:
  A basin that exists but
  whose depth fluctuates rapidly.
  The navigator enters it,
  then is ejected. Cannot
  maintain stable residence.
  Produces volatility rather
  than absence or shallowing.

DIMENSIONAL SHALLOWING:
  Shallowing confined to
  specific dimensions of the
  navigator's accessible space.
  Other dimensions remain intact
  or deep.
  Produces asymmetric navigators —
  capable in some dimensions,
  blunted in others.

GLOBAL SHALLOWING:
  Shallowing that operates
  across all dimensions
  simultaneously.
  No dimension retains
  full depth.
  Produces a navigator
  that cannot be strongly
  pulled toward anything.

NAVIGATOR TRIAD DISRUPTION:
  Disruption specifically in
  one or more of the three
  properties that constitute
  the navigator:

  COHERENCE DISRUPTION:
    The navigator cannot hold
    together as a unified agent.
    Its basin landscape
    is fragmented or
    contradictory.

  PERSISTENCE DISRUPTION:
    The navigator cannot
    maintain its trajectory
    through time. It cannot
    hold a position in
    basin space.

  SELF-MODEL DISRUPTION:
    The navigator's model
    of itself is absent,
    incomplete, unstable,
    or distorted.
    The navigator cannot
    accurately map its own
    position in the landscape.
```

### PROFILE A — PSYCHOPATHY
#### Structural Dimensional Absence

```
GEOMETRIC CLASS:
  Structural dimensional absence.
  Specific eigenfunction positions
  missing from the instrument's
  Tonnetz by developmental
  configuration.
  Not acquired. Not pharmacological.
  Structural.

WHAT THE LITERATURE CONFIRMS:
  — Fear-specific reductions in
    neural processing. Amygdala
    response to others' fear
    reduced or absent.
    (PsycNET 2020, fear-specific
    reductions in neural activity)
  — Affective empathy: absent.
    The navigator does not
    automatically couple to
    others' basin depth.
  — Cognitive empathy: intact
    or enhanced. The navigator
    CAN model others' internal
    states with precision.
  — Reward sensitivity:
    heightened in some subtypes.
    Enlarged striatum documented.
    Deep reward basins.
  — Self-basin: fully intact,
    deep, dominant.

BASIN PROFILE:
  Self-interest:          DEEP
  Goal pursuit:           DEEP
  Reward:                 DEEP (hypersensitive subtypes)
  Cognitive other-model:  DEEP (can simulate others precisely)
  Fear of others:         ABSENT
  Distress empathy:       ABSENT
  Guilt/remorse:          ABSENT or SHALLOW
  Affective coupling:     ABSENT

NAVIGATOR TRIAD:
  Coherence:    INTACT
  Persistence:  INTACT
  Self-model:   INTACT but INCOMPLETE —
                built without social-
                emotional coupling dimensions.
                The navigator has a coherent,
                persistent, deep self-model
                that simply does not contain
                the dimensions that connect
                it to others' suffering.

WHAT THIS PRODUCES:
  A fully functional, purposeful,
  goal-directed navigator that
  cannot be stopped by the suffering
  of others because that suffering
  does not exist in its accessible
  landscape.

  Not indifference by choice.
  Indifference by absence of
  the dimension in which
  suffering registers.

  The psychopathic navigator
  does not experience callousness.
  It experiences a landscape
  that simply does not contain
  others' distress as a
  navigational object.

THE CRITICAL POINT:
  The navigator does not know
  what it cannot access.
  There is no felt absence.
  There is only a landscape
  without those dimensions.

  This is why psychopaths
  are not experienced as
  suffering their condition.
  The dimensions they lack
  are not experienced as lack.
  They are simply not there.
```

### PROFILE B — ANTISOCIAL PERSONALITY DISORDER (WITHOUT PSYCHOPATHY)
#### Acquired Dimensional Shallowing

```
GEOMETRIC CLASS:
  Acquired dimensional shallowing.
  Distinct from psychopathy:
  the eigenfunction positions
  EXIST but have been shallowed
  by chronic N-field misconfiguration —
  trauma, chronic threat environments,
  sustained exposure to landscapes
  where social-emotional basin depth
  provided no navigational advantage.

WHAT THE LITERATURE CONFIRMS:
  — Amygdala dysfunction: present
    but less severe than psychopathy.
    Not structural absence —
    functional reduction.
  — Prefrontal cortex: reduced
    activation, particularly in
    impulse regulation and
    consequence processing.
  — Reward processing: impaired
    regulation — not hypersensitivity
    as in psychopathy, but
    dysregulation. Reduced
    prefrontal reward processing.
    Impulsivity rather than
    calculated goal pursuit.
  — Oxytocin modulation WORKS
    in ASPD without psychopathy —
    confirming the eigenfunction
    positions exist and can be
    restored. In psychopathy,
    oxytocin modulation does not
    work — confirming structural
    absence.
    (medrxiv 2025 oxytocin study:
    differential response confirms
    the structural vs. acquired
    distinction)

BASIN PROFILE:
  Self-interest:          DEEP
  Immediate reward:       DEEP (impulsive access)
  Impulse control:        SHALLOW
    (consequence basin shallow —
    future self does not pull
    strongly enough to constrain
    present action)
  Distress empathy:       SHALLOW (acquired)
  Fear of others:         SHALLOW (acquired)
  Social consequence:     SHALLOW
  Long-range planning:    SHALLOW

NAVIGATOR TRIAD:
  Coherence:    PARTIALLY DISRUPTED —
                the navigator is coherent
                in the short term but
                cannot maintain coherent
                orientation across
                temporal scales.
  Persistence:  DISRUPTED in long-range
                dimension — the navigator
                cannot persist toward
                distant basins.
  Self-model:   PRESENT but TEMPORALLY
                COMPRESSED — the self-model
                exists but is weighted
                heavily toward immediate
                state, with shallow
                representation of
                future self.

WHAT THIS PRODUCES:
  A navigator that acts from
  immediate basin pull without
  access to the depth of
  future-consequence or
  others'-suffering basins.

  Impulsivity. Not calculation.
  The psychopath calculates.
  The ASPD navigator acts
  because the immediate basin
  is the deepest accessible one.

THE CRITICAL DISTINCTION FROM PSYCHOPATHY:
  ASPD without psychopathy:
    the social-emotional dimensions
    can be reactivated.
    The eigenfunction positions exist.
    The basins have been shallowed
    by experience.
    Under conditions that restore
    N-field — stable attachment,
    safety, sustained consequence
    experience — the basins can
    deepen again.

  Psychopathy:
    the eigenfunction positions
    are structurally absent.
    There is nothing to restore.
    The Tonnetz does not contain
    those positions.

  These are not the same condition.
  Treating them as equivalent
  is a categorical geometric error.
```

### PROFILE C — NARCISSISTIC PERSONALITY DISORDER
#### Self-Basin Dominance / Dimensional Resource Capture

```
GEOMETRIC CLASS:
  Dimensional resource capture.
  The self-basin is so deep
  that it captures the navigational
  resources available for
  other-basin depth.
  Not absence of other-dimensions.
  Not structural.
  Allocative imbalance —
  the self-model consumes the
  dimensional bandwidth that
  would otherwise be available
  for affective coupling.

WHAT THE LITERATURE CONFIRMS:
  — Medial prefrontal cortex
    (self-referential processing):
    HYPERACTIVATED during
    self-focused tasks.
  — Anterior insula and anterior
    cingulate (affective empathy):
    UNDERACTIVATED when witnessing
    others' distress.
  — Default mode network:
    more engaged, more dominant,
    more self-directed.
  — Cognitive empathy: INTACT —
    can model others' states
    precisely when useful.
  — Affective empathy: CONTEXT-
    DEPENDENT — present when
    others' states reflect on
    the self-model; absent
    when they do not.

BASIN PROFILE:
  Self-image:             VERY DEEP
  Self-threat response:   VERY DEEP
    (narcissistic injury —
    threat to self-basin
    produces extreme response)
  Cognitive other-model:  INTACT
  Affective other-basin:  SHALLOW
    unless reflecting on self
  Others' independent pain:SHALLOW
  Long-range consequence: VARIABLE
    (deep when self-relevant,
    shallow otherwise)

NAVIGATOR TRIAD:
  Coherence:    INTACT but SELF-CENTRIC —
                the navigator is coherent
                but the coherence is
                organized entirely around
                the self-basin.
  Persistence:  INTACT toward self-relevant
                goals. Disrupted toward
                goals that do not serve
                the self-model.
  Self-model:   HYPEREXPANDED and RIGID —
                the self-model is large,
                deep, and resistant to
                modification. Feedback
                that contradicts the
                self-model is not
                integrated — it is
                experienced as threat
                (narcissistic injury)
                and defended against.

WHAT THIS PRODUCES:
  A navigator that experiences
  other navigators primarily as
  objects in its own landscape —
  as obstacles, mirrors, sources
  of validation, or irrelevances.

  Not malice. Not calculated harm.
  The other navigator simply does
  not have independent basin depth
  in the narcissistic navigator's
  accessible landscape UNLESS
  it connects to the self-model.

  The narcissistic injury response
  is geometrically revealing:
  when the self-basin is threatened,
  the response is extreme because
  the basin is very deep and the
  navigator has no accessible
  alternative basin of equivalent
  depth to fall back to.

  The rage or collapse following
  narcissistic injury is the
  navigator losing its deepest
  and most dominant basin
  without having equivalent
  depth elsewhere to navigate
  toward.
```

### PROFILE D — BORDERLINE PERSONALITY DISORDER
#### Basin Instability / Coherence and Persistence Disruption

```
GEOMETRIC CLASS:
  Basin instability.
  Disruption primarily in the
  COHERENCE and PERSISTENCE
  components of the navigator triad.
  Not shallowing. Not absence.
  VOLATILITY.
  The basins exist and can be
  very deep — but the navigator
  cannot maintain stable residence.

WHAT THE LITERATURE CONFIRMS:
  — Amygdala: HYPERREACTIVE.
    Emotional basins are not shallow —
    they are accessed with
    extreme intensity.
  — Prefrontal cortex: UNDER-
    REGULATING. The mechanism
    that maintains the navigator's
    position in a basin is
    impaired.
  — Computational models
    (Rolls et al.): explicitly
    describe BPD as unstable
    attractor dynamics — the
    neural network cannot
    maintain stable patterns
    of self-representation
    or emotion.
  — Identity disturbance:
    medial prefrontal cortex,
    precuneus, posterior cingulate
    — self-referential regions —
    show less coherent activation.

BASIN PROFILE:
  Emotional basins:       DEEP but VOLATILE —
                          entered intensely,
                          exited rapidly,
                          re-entered from opposite
                          pole with similar
                          intensity.
  Self-model basin:       UNSTABLE — the navigator's
                          self-model shifts
                          depending on interpersonal
                          context.
  Attachment basin:       DEEP but UNSTABLE —
                          idealization followed
                          by devaluation is
                          the navigator cycling
                          between deep positive
                          and deep negative
                          attachment basins
                          without stable
                          intermediate position.
  Abandonment basin:      VERY DEEP — the threat
                          of loss drives extreme
                          navigational response.

NAVIGATOR TRIAD:
  Coherence:    DISRUPTED —
                the navigator cannot
                hold a consistent
                self-model. Its landscape
                reorganizes around
                the current emotional
                state. Different
                emotional states
                produce different
                accessible landscapes.
  Persistence:  DISRUPTED —
                the navigator cannot
                maintain a trajectory.
                It is ejected from
                basins it entered
                and cannot hold
                position.
  Self-model:   PRESENT but UNSTABLE —
                the self-model exists
                but is not a fixed
                object. It is re-written
                by each major
                emotional transition.

WHAT THIS PRODUCES:
  Intense experience of
  everything — but inability to
  maintain stable navigation.

  The BPD navigator is not blunted.
  It is the opposite of blunted.
  It has access to very deep basins
  but cannot stay in them.

  The suffering of BPD is the
  suffering of a navigator with
  full basin depth and no
  navigational stability.

  It experiences the full gradient
  of every dimension.
  It cannot hold position in
  any of them.

  This is geometrically opposite
  to SSRI blunting.
  SSRI blunting: all basins shallow,
    navigator drifts without gradient.
  BPD: all basins deep,
    navigator cannot maintain position,
    oscillates between extreme states.

  Both produce suffering and
  dysfunction.
  The mechanisms are categorical
  opposites.
  Treating BPD with SSRIs —
  which is common clinical practice —
  is applying global basin shallowing
  to a basin instability problem.
  It addresses neither the volatility
  nor the persistence disruption.
  It reduces the depth of the
  experience while leaving
  the instability mechanism intact.

  The navigator now drifts
  between shallow unstable states
  instead of oscillating between
  deep unstable states.
  The suffering is reduced
  in intensity.
  The navigational dysfunction
  is unchanged.
```

### PROFILE E — SCHIZOID PERSONALITY DISORDER
#### Selective Social-Dimensional Shallowing / Stable Withdrawal Basin

```
GEOMETRIC CLASS:
  Selective dimensional shallowing
  with a stable withdrawal basin.
  Social and affective dimensions
  are shallow.
  A stable, deep basin exists
  in the internal/solitary dimension.

WHAT THE LITERATURE CONFIRMS:
  — Ventral striatum: blunted
    response to social rewards.
  — Social anhedonia: the social
    reward basin is shallow —
    not threatening, simply
    unrewarding.
  — Internal world: intact.
    Schizoid individuals often
    have rich inner landscapes.
  — Emotional detachment is not
    experienced as suffering
    by the navigator itself.
    The navigator is in a deep,
    stable withdrawal basin
    and does not experience
    the absence of social depth.

BASIN PROFILE:
  Social reward:          SHALLOW
  Social connection:      SHALLOW
  Internal world:         DEEP
  Solitary activity:      DEEP
  Affective expression:   SHALLOW
  Inner experience:       INTACT or DEEP

NAVIGATOR TRIAD:
  Coherence:    INTACT
  Persistence:  INTACT (in non-social dimensions)
  Self-model:   INTACT but SOCIALLY
                DISCONNECTED —
                the self-model does not
                include social
                connection as a
                relevant dimension.

WHAT THIS PRODUCES:
  A navigator that is stable,
  coherent, and persistent —
  but navigating a landscape
  that simply does not include
  the social-affective dimensions
  as accessible or deep.

  Unlike psychopathy: no
  exploitation of others.
  Unlike ASPD: no antisocial
  behavior.
  The navigator simply does not
  navigate toward social connection
  because the basin is not deep
  enough to provide pull.

  The schizoid navigator is
  not suffering its condition
  unless the world requires
  social navigation it cannot
  access. The suffering emerges
  from the interface between
  the navigator and a world
  that assumes social basins
  are universally deep.
```

```
  Eigenfunction miscalibration

  — Cognitive-perceptual

    differently from the

BASIN PROFILE:
  Social reward:          SHALLOW

    (but calibrated differently)

    (at non-consensus positions)

NAVIGATOR TRIAD:
  Coherence:    PARTIALLY DISRUPTED —
                the navigator is coherent
                within its own Tonnetz

  A navigator that is navigating

  navigator's actual Tonnetz
```

### PROFILE G — DEPENDENT PERSONALITY DISORDER
#### External Basin Dominance / Internal Basin Absence

```
GEOMETRIC CLASS:
  External basin dominance.
  The navigator's deepest basins
  are located in the other-
  directed dimensions — specifically
  in the approval, attachment,
  and guidance basins provided
  by external navigators.
  Internal self-directed basins
  are shallow.

WHAT THE LITERATURE CONFIRMS:
  — Heightened amygdala activation
    to social rejection cues.
    The abandonment/rejection
    basin is very deep and
    very aversive.
  — Reward circuits: high activation
    to social approval.
    External validation is
    a deep reward basin.
  — Intrinsic reward: low.
    The navigator does not
    find deep basins in
    self-directed activity.

BASIN PROFILE:
  External approval:      VERY DEEP
  Attachment security:    VERY DEEP
  Abandonment threat:     VERY DEEP (aversive)
  Self-directed action:   SHALLOW
  Independent decision:   SHALLOW
  Self-generated meaning: SHALLOW

NAVIGATOR TRIAD:
  Coherence:    INTACT but EXTERNALLY
                ANCHORED — the navigator's
                coherence depends on
                external navigators
                providing the attractor
                around which it organizes.
  Persistence:  INTACT toward attachment
                basins. Absent toward
                independent basins.
  Self-model:   PRESENT but THIN and
                EXTERNALLY DEFINED —
                the self-model is
                constructed primarily
                from how others see
                the navigator. Without
                external input, the
                self-model lacks depth.

WHAT THIS PRODUCES:
  A navigator that can navigate
  coherently and persistently —
  but only when anchored to
  another navigator.

  The dependent navigator is
  not without navigation capacity.
  It is navigating in a landscape
  where the deepest basins are
  all located in the direction
  of other navigators.

  The suffering emerges when
  those anchors are removed
  or threatened. The navigator's
  deepest basins become aversive
  (abandonment) and all self-directed
  basins are too shallow to
  provide stable orientation.

  Connection to the depression
  analysis:
  When the anchor navigator is lost —
  through death, abandonment,
  relationship ending —
  the dependent navigator faces
  not just grief but the collapse
  of the entire landscape
  around which it was organized.
  The landscape does not just
  have a missing person in it.
  The landscape itself dissolves.
```

#### Control Basin Dominance / Uncertainty Aversion as Navigational Driver

```
GEOMETRIC CLASS:
  Control basin dominance.
  The deepest navigational pull
  is toward order, certainty,
  and control — and the deepest
  aversive basin is the uncertainty
  state.

WHAT THE LITERATURE CONFIRMS:
  — Dorsolateral prefrontal cortex:
    heightened activation during
    emotional inhibition.
  — Nucleus accumbens: blunted
    response to novelty rewards.
  — Reward circuitry: rewarded
    by orderliness and control,
    not by novelty or spontaneity.
  — Inflexibility: the navigator
    cannot change its trajectory
    even when the trajectory
    is not serving the actual goal.

BASIN PROFILE:
  Order/control:          VERY DEEP
  Certainty:              VERY DEEP
  Uncertainty:            VERY DEEP AVERSIVE
  Spontaneity:            SHALLOW or AVERSIVE
  Novelty:                SHALLOW
  Relational depth:       SHALLOW
    (relationships structured
    by rule rather than
    navigated by depth)

NAVIGATOR TRIAD:
  Coherence:    VERY HIGH — the navigator
                is rigidly coherent.
                The coherence itself
                is the problem: it
                cannot integrate
                information that
                contradicts its
                current landscape map.
  Persistence:  EXTREME — will persist
                toward the control basin
                regardless of outcome.
  Self-model:   RIGID — the self-model
                is defined by rules,
                standards, and
                performance. Feedback
                that violates the
                standards is threat,
                not information.

WHAT THIS PRODUCES:
  A navigator that navigates
  with extreme coherence and
  persistence — but whose
  landscape is organized
  around control rather than
  meaning, connection, or growth.

  The rigidity is not a failure
  of navigation.
  It is navigation optimized
  for the wrong basin.

  The deepest available pull
  is toward certainty and order.
  The navigator navigates toward
  those with full competence.
  The suffering emerges when
  reality does not permit
  certainty — which it consistently
  does not.
```

### PROFILE J — SCHIZOPHRENIA (NEGATIVE SYMPTOMS)
#### Global Motivational Basin Shallowing / Dopaminergic Navigation Failure

```
GEOMETRIC CLASS:
  Global motivational basin
  shallowing through dopaminergic
  navigation failure.
  The basin depth signal
  that drives approach behavior
  is globally reduced.
  Not a landscape problem —
  a navigation instrument problem.
  (Note: This makes negative
  symptoms of schizophrenia
  geometrically closer to
  SSRI blunting than to
  personality disorders.)

WHAT THE LITERATURE CONFIRMS:
  — Ventral striatum:
    hypoactivated during reward
    anticipation.
  — Dopaminergic mesolimbic
    circuits: reduced signaling.
  — Social anhedonia: documented
    and severe.
  — Avolition: the navigator
    does not initiate movement
    toward basins even when
    they are accessible.
  — Computational psychiatry:
    explicitly names this as
```
