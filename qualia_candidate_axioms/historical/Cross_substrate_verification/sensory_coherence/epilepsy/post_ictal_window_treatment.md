## PART II: THE FIVE INTERVENTION
## WINDOWS AND THEIR DERIVED TARGETS

### Window 1: T+0 to T+5 — Calcium Truncation

```
TARGET: Residual glutamate-driven
calcium influx immediately post-seizure.

GEOMETRIC OPERATION:
  Interrupt the first messenger
  of the entire cascade before
  any downstream signalling begins.
  If calcium influx is blocked:
  No IEG induction.
  No BDNF release.
  No mTOR activation.
  No neuroinflammation from this cascade.
  The entire post-seizure modification
  is prevented at the root.

DERIVED MECHANISM:
  NMDA receptor antagonist given
  immediately post-seizure.
  Not to stop the seizure (it is over).
  To block the residual NMDA
  activation from post-seizure
  glutamate spillover.

EXISTING DRUG CANDIDATES:

  Memantine (approved: Alzheimer's):
    NMDA receptor antagonist.
    Blood-brain barrier penetrant.
    Low-affinity, voltage-dependent.
    Does not block normal NMDA
    function completely — only
    blocks sustained or excessive
    NMDA activation.
    THIS IS GEOMETRICALLY IDEAL:
    At T+5 post-seizure, residual
    glutamate is creating sustained
    NMDA activation in a network
    that is no longer actively seizing.
    Memantine blocks this sustained
    activation specifically while
    being relatively permissive
    to normal-range NMDA activity.
    Dose used in Alzheimer's:
    5-20mg oral.
    Route for post-ictal use:
    intranasal (faster CNS delivery)
    or subcutaneous.

  Ketamine (approved: anaesthesia):
    More potent NMDA antagonist.
    Dissociative side effects at
    full anaesthetic doses.
    Sub-anaesthetic doses (low-dose
    ketamine) have CNS effects
    with manageable side effect profile.
    Intranasal ketamine already used
    in some clinical contexts.
    Higher risk than memantine
    for post-ictal application.
    Reserve for severe cases.

  PREFERRED WINDOW 1 CANDIDATE:
  Intranasal memantine.
  Given at seizure detection + 0-5 min.
  Rationale: blocks residual NMDA
  calcium cascade before IEG
  induction begins.
  Safety profile: established.
  BBB penetration: confirmed.
  Selectivity: moderate.
  Clinical status: approved but
  not for this indication.
  Repurposing potential: HIGH.

  LIMITATION:
  Window 1 is only 5 minutes.
  Manual administration requires
  a caregiver present and alert
  within 5 minutes of seizure end.
  This is not reliable.
  Automated wearable delivery
  is required for this window.
  (See Part IV — Delivery Architecture)
```

### Window 2: T+5 to T+30 — IEG Suppression

```
TARGET: Immediate early gene
transcription (c-fos, Arc).

GEOMETRIC OPERATION:
  Prevent the genomic record of
  the seizure from being written.
  Without c-fos and Arc induction,
  the downstream structural cascade
  has no initiation signal.
  The basin deepening process
  cannot consolidate because the
  genomic instruction is not issued.

DERIVED MECHANISM:
  A compound that specifically
  reduces activity-driven IEG
  transcription in the post-seizure
  window without globally suppressing
  gene expression.

EXISTING DRUG CANDIDATES:

  There is no approved drug that
  specifically targets IEG induction
  in the post-ictal context.

  The mechanism would require:
    Inhibition of CREB (cAMP response
    element binding protein) — the
    transcription factor that drives
    c-fos and Arc in response to
    calcium/CaM kinase signalling.
    Or: inhibition of CaM kinase IV
    (CaMKIV) — the kinase that
    phosphorylates CREB in response
    to calcium influx.
    Or: inhibition of ERK/MAPK
    pathway — also upstream of CREB
    and IEG induction.

  PD0325901 (MEK/ERK inhibitor):
    Highly potent ERK inhibitor.
    BBB penetrant (developed for
    brain tumour applications).
    Preclinical use in neurological
    contexts.
    Not approved for clinical use.
    Novel territory.

  ASSESSMENT:
  Window 2 IEG suppression requires
  a novel compound or research-stage
  compound. No approved drug fits
  the specificity requirement.
  This window is the IDENTIFIED GAP
  that requires novel compound
  development.
  (Derived below in Part III.)

  However:
  If Window 1 (memantine) is effective
  at truncating calcium influx,
  IEG induction is also diminished
  because calcium is the upstream
  signal for IEG transcription.
  Window 1 and Window 2 may be
  partially addressed by the
  same compound.
```

```
TARGET: TrkB-PLCγ1 signalling arm.
Not TrkB globally.
The specific pathway that drives
epileptogenic synaptic potentiation.

GEOMETRIC OPERATION:
  Block the basin-deepening
  synaptic potentiation pathway
  while preserving the neuronal
  survival pathway.
  The false attractor basin does
  not deepen after this seizure.
  The next seizure faces the same
  rim height as this one.
  Progressive epileptogenesis stops.

DERIVED MECHANISM:
  A compound that prevents BDNF-TrkB
  from phosphorylating PLCγ1 and
  activating IP3/DAG downstream
  signalling — specifically at the
  TrkB-PLCγ1 docking interface —
  without blocking TrkB-Shc-Akt
  neuroprotective signalling.

THE pY816 PEPTIDE — CONFIRMED LEAD:

  McNamara lab, Duke University.
  (Gu et al., Neuron 2015)

  The pY816 peptide specifically
  uncouples TrkB from PLCγ1.
  Animal model results:
    Three days of post-SE treatment.
    Significantly fewer spontaneous
    seizures at four weeks.
    Reduced hippocampal damage.
    Preservation of neuroprotective
    TrkB-Shc-Akt signalling.

  Current status:
    CURE Epilepsy Foundation funded
    preclinical IND-enabling studies.
    (IND = Investigational New Drug)
    Moving toward first-in-human.
    Not yet in clinical trials.

  This is the closest existing
  compound to the derived target.
  It is in preclinical IND-enabling
  stage — the step before Phase I.
  The geometry derived it
  independently from the same logic.
  The McNamara lab found it
  empirically from the same pathway.
  Full convergence.

THE SMALL-MOLECULE REQUIREMENT:
  The pY816 peptide has limitations:
    Peptides have poor oral
    bioavailability.
    Peptides have short half-lives.
    Peptides require careful
    formulation for BBB penetration.
    Subcutaneous delivery is likely
    required — compatible with the
    wearable auto-injector architecture.

  A small-molecule TrkB-PLCγ1
  interface inhibitor would be
  preferred for clinical use.
  None exists currently.
  (Derived in Part III.)

EXISTING APPROVED DRUG PARTIAL FIT:
  ANA-12 (TrkB antagonist):
    Non-selective.
    Blocks both PLCγ1 and Shc-Akt.
    The neuroprotection concern makes
    this inappropriate for post-ictal
    use as currently formulated.
    Could be used at ultra-low doses
    where partial PLCγ1 blockade
    is achieved with minimal Shc-Akt
    impact — but this is speculative
    and requires pharmacological
    characterisation.

  CONCLUSION FOR WINDOW 3:
  The pY816 peptide is the
  current lead compound.
  It requires IND and Phase I/II.
  A small molecule with the same
  selectivity is the ultimate target.
  Part III derives its specifications.
```

TARGET: mTOR-driven structural

  A second strike targeting mTOR
  at its peak activation window

  even if Window 3 is incomplete.

TIMING CRITICALITY:
  Literature confirmed:
  Rapamycin within 24 hours of
  insult: ANTIEPILEPTOGENIC.
  After 7 days: NO EFFECT.

  NOVEL REFINEMENT FROM GEOMETRY:
  The current TSC literature uses

  effect (preventing landscape

```

GEOMETRIC OPERATION:

  (Approved: rheumatoid arthritis,
  rare inflammatory diseases;
  clinical use in FIRES)

    at the IL-1 receptor.
    Confirmed: reduces neuroinflammation.
    Confirmed in FIRES: reduces
    seizure frequency in severe
    inflammatory epilepsy syndromes.
    BBB penetration: LOW to moderate.
    This is the weakness.

    achieve limited CNS levels.
    Higher doses (up to 8mg/kg/day

    CNS effect.

    In the post-ictal window, the BBB

    (seizure-induced BBB disruption).
    This is normally a clinical concern.
    Geometrically it is a DELIVERY
    ADVANTAGE:
    Post-ictal BBB permeability
    enhancement is a brief window
    in which drugs that normally
    have poor CNS penetration may
    achieve higher CNS levels.
    Anakinra given at T+6 to T+8
    hours — during peak BBB
    permeability from the seizure —
    may achieve CNS levels that
    it cannot achieve in the steady
    state.
    The same seizure that opens
    the inflammatory cascade also
    opens the door for the
    anti-inflammatory drug.

    PREFERRED WINDOW 5A CANDIDATE:
    Subcutaneous anakinra (100mg)

    Leverages post-ictal BBB opening.

  CANDIDATE B: Celecoxib (approved:
  arthritis, pain, anti-inflammatory):
    Selective COX-2 inhibitor.
    Good CNS penetration.
    Oral bioavailability excellent.
    COX-2 upregulation peaks 6-24h
    post-seizure.
    Celecoxib given at T+6 hours
    directly targets the COX-2
    induction peak.
    Literature confirms: COX-2
    inhibition reduces post-seizure
    neuroinflammation and impedes
    epileptogenesis in animal models.

    Cardiac risk of COX-2 inhibition:
    Relevant with CHRONIC use.
    A single post-ictal dose has
    minimal cardiovascular risk.
    The risk profile for pulsed use
    is fundamentally different from
    chronic use.

    PREFERRED WINDOW 5B CANDIDATE:
    Oral celecoxib (200-400mg) at T+6h.
    Safe, available, established
    mechanism, correct window target.
    Pulsed. Not chronic.

  CANDIDATES A + B COMBINED:
    IL-1β blockade (anakinra) hits
    the upstream cytokine cascade.
    COX-2 blockade (celecoxib) hits
    the prostaglandin synthesis arm.
    These are parallel pathways.
    Blocking both simultaneously

    cascade interruption than
    either alone.
    No significant drug-drug

    Combined post-ictal anti-

    and geometrically precise.

### Novel Compound Specification 1:
### Small-Molecule TrkB-PLCγ1 Interface Inhibitor

```
GEOMETRIC REQUIREMENT:
  Block the TrkB-PLCγ1 protein-protein
  interaction interface — prevent
  PLCγ1 from docking at the pY816
  phosphotyrosine site on TrkB —
  without affecting TrkB-Shc
  interaction at the pY490 site.

MOLECULAR SPECIFICATION:

  The interaction geometry:
    TrkB has two key tyrosine
    phosphorylation sites:
      pY816: docks PLCγ1 (epileptogenic).
      pY490: docks Shc (neuroprotective).
    These sites are spatially
    separated on the TrkB
    intracellular domain.
    A compound that selectively
    occupies the pY816 docking
    groove would block PLCγ1
    binding without touching pY490.

  What the compound must be:
    1. Small molecule (not peptide)
       for oral/intranasal bioavailability.
    2. BBB penetrant:
       Molecular weight <450 Da
       (ideally <400 Da for CNS).
       LogP 1-4 (lipophilic enough
       for BBB but not too insoluble).
       Low number of hydrogen bond
       donors (<3).
    3. Selective for pY816 groove
       over pY490 groove.
    4. Competitive or allosteric
       inhibitor of PLCγ1 docking
       rather than full TrkB kinase
       inhibitor (kinase inhibitors
       block the ATP site and affect
       all TrkB signalling).

  DRUG DISCOVERY APPROACH:
    Structure-guided approach:
    The pY816 groove 3D structure
    is known from TrkB crystal
    structures (PDB available).
    Computational fragment-based
    screening of the pY816 groove.
    Identify fragments that occupy
    the groove selectively.
    Grow fragments into lead compounds.
    Optimise for CNS drug-likeness
    properties.

  LEAD OPTIMISATION PRIORITIES:
    Half-life: 4-8 hours
    (matching the BDNF peak window).
    Not chronic — designed for
    pulsed post-ictal use.
    Formulation: compatible with
    subcutaneous injection or
    intranasal delivery.

  DEVELOPMENT STATUS:
    pY816 peptide (McNamara lab):
    proof of concept in animals.
    IND-enabling studies funded
    (CURE Epilepsy Foundation).
    Small-molecule equivalent:
    not yet reported in literature.
    The geometric framework
    specifies it.
    The structural biology to
    pursue it exists.

NAME DERIVED:
  Working designation: PIC-001
  (Post-Ictal Consolidation
  inhibitor — compound 001).
  Target: TrkB pY816 groove.
  Operation: selective PLCγ1
  docking inhibition.
  Window: T+30min to T+3h.
  Route: subcutaneous or intranasal.
```

### Novel Compound Specification 2:
### Post-Ictal IEG Suppressor

```
GEOMETRIC REQUIREMENT:
  Suppress activity-driven IEG
  transcription (c-fos, Arc) in the
  post-seizure network specifically,
  without globally suppressing
  neuronal gene expression in
  networks that were not involved
  in the seizure.

  This specificity requirement is
  the hard part.
  Globally suppressing CREB or
  ERK would impair normal plasticity,
  memory consolidation, and
  neuroprotective responses across
  the entire brain.

  The geometric solution:
  The post-seizure IEG induction
  in the epileptogenic zone is
  driven by CALCIUM influx at
  concentrations higher than
  those seen in normal plasticity.
  The magnitude of calcium is
  what makes it a seizure-driven
  signal rather than a learning
  signal.

  A compound that inhibits CREB
  phosphorylation specifically
  at HIGH calcium concentrations —
  but not at normal plasticity
  calcium concentrations — would
  achieve the selective suppression.

  This is a THRESHOLD-SENSITIVE
  CREB inhibitor.
  A compound that disrupts the
  CaMKIV-CREB interaction only
  when CaMKIV is in its maximally
  phosphorylated (high-activity)
  state — achieved only by the
  calcium flood of seizure activity,
  not by normal synaptic activity.

MOLECULAR SPECIFICATION:

  Target: CaMKIV-CREB interaction
  in its maximally active state.

  What the compound must be:
    1. Conformation-selective —
       binds the CaMKIV-CREB complex
       only when CaMKIV is fully
       phosphorylated (post-seizure
       state) rather than the
       partially phosphorylated
       state of normal activity.
    2. Small molecule, BBB penetrant
       (same criteria as PIC-001).
    3. Short half-life (2-4 hours)
       since the IEG window is narrow.
    4. Reversible — must not
       permanently suppress CREB
       phosphorylation, which is
       required for long-term memory.

  ALTERNATIVE APPROACH — ARC SPECIFIC:
  Arc (activity-regulated cytoskeleton-
  associated protein) specifically
  is the IEG most directly responsible
  for AMPA receptor trafficking
  (the receptor that implements
  the synaptic strengthening).
  A selective Arc expression inhibitor
  given post-ictally would prevent
  the AMPA receptor upregulation
  that constitutes the most direct
  step of basin deepening.
  Arc mRNA has an untranslated
  region (UTR) that regulates its
  expression in an activity-dependent
  manner — a targetable structure
  for antisense oligonucleotides
  (ASOs) or small molecules that
  bind the UTR.
