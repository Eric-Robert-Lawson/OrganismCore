### What Is Actually Happening

```
The post-ictal work established
that after a seizure, the landscape
is modified by a molecular cascade.

The pre-ictal question is the inverse:
What is happening as the seizure
is ABOUT TO OCCUR?

The geometry describes it precisely.

THE NAVIGATOR AND THE RIM:

  The false attractor basin has a rim.
  The navigator normally operates
  in the stable landscape far from
  that rim.

  The pre-ictal state is not
  a sudden event.
  It is a process.
  A convergence.

  Two things can move toward
  each other:
    The navigator can move toward
    the rim (increasing excitation).
    The rim can move toward the
    navigator (decreasing threshold).
  Either achieves rim proximity.
  In the pre-ictal state, both

THE GEOMETRY OF RIM APPROACH:

  Imagine the navigator as a ball
  rolling on a landscape.

  The false attractor basin is
  nearby, separated by a rim.

  Pre-ictally:
    The rim is lowering.

    The navigator's position

  The two movements are distinct
  geometrically and mechanistically.

  The pre-ictal intervention

THE CRITICAL GEOMETRIC INSIGHT:

  The pre-ictal state is not

  It is a TRAJECTORY.
  The navigator is on a path
  toward the rim.
  The path has a direction, a speed,

    The earlier the intervention
    on the path, the easier.
    The closer to the rim,
    the harder.
    The trajectory can be reversed
    if the intervention is large

  A pre-ictal intervention is

  ACUTE TRAJECTORY REVERSAL.
```

### The Five Geometric Features
### of the Pre-Ictal Trajectory

```
The pre-ictal trajectory has
five geometrically distinct
features, each of which is a
distinct drug target.

FEATURE 1: RISING EXCITATORY TONE
  As the navigator approaches
  the rim, glutamatergic (excitatory)
  tone rises in the epileptogenic
  network.
  AMPA receptor trafficking
  increases synaptic strength.
  Voltage-gated sodium channels
  become more available.
  The network is becoming
  progressively easier to depolarise.

  GEOMETRIC DESCRIPTION:
  The navigator is being pushed
  toward the rim by an increasing
  excitatory vector force.
  The force has a direction (toward
  the rim) and a magnitude
  (proportional to excitatory tone).

  TARGET: Reduce the excitatory
  vector force.
  Mechanism: glutamate modulation,
  sodium channel stabilisation,
  AMPA receptor internalisation.

FEATURE 2: DECLINING INHIBITORY TONE
  Simultaneously, GABAergic
  inhibitory tone declines.
  GABA release decreases or
  GABA receptor sensitivity
  falls in the pre-ictal period.
  The interneurons that normally
  constrain the network's firing
  are losing effectiveness.

  GEOMETRIC DESCRIPTION:
  The rim height is decreasing.
  The inhibitory system that
  maintains the rim is being
  suppressed or exhausted.
  The rim is descending toward
  the navigator.

  TARGET: Support or restore
  inhibitory tone.
  Mechanism: GABA-A receptor
  enhancement, GABA reuptake
  inhibition, interneuron support.

FEATURE 3: NEURONAL HYPERSYNCHRONY
  Pre-ictally, neurons in the
  epileptogenic network begin
  firing in increasing synchrony.
  Normally, the network has
  temporal diversity of firing —
  neurons fire at different times,
  which distributes load and
  prevents summation.
  Pre-ictally, this diversity collapses.
  Neurons begin to synchronise.
  Synchronous firing summates.
  The summed output is the seizure.

  GEOMETRIC DESCRIPTION:
  The navigator's trajectory is
  becoming less random and more
  directed — it is being channelled
  toward the rim rather than
  diffusing across the landscape.
  Synchrony is the mechanism
  of channelling.

  TARGET: Disrupt or reduce
  hypersynchrony.
  Mechanism: compounds that
  increase temporal diversity
  of neural firing, reduce
  gap junction conductance,
  or disrupt the synchronising
  signal.

FEATURE 4: FAILING METABOLIC BUFFER
  The pre-ictal network is
  consuming energy at an increasing
  rate as inhibitory neurons
  work harder to prevent seizure.
  Glucose metabolism accelerates.
  ATP reserves in the affected
  tissue deplete.
  When ATP falls, sodium-potassium
  ATPase pumps slow.
  Ion gradients collapse.
  The membrane depolarises.
  The seizure becomes inevitable.

  GEOMETRIC DESCRIPTION:
  The landscape itself is degrading
  under the navigator's feet.
  The valley floor (healthy attractor)
  is rising toward the rim as
  metabolic failure erases the
  height difference between them.
  The rim and the valley floor
  converge.

  TARGET: Support metabolic
  buffer capacity in the
  pre-ictal period.
  Mechanism: glucose substrate
  availability, ketone body
  supplementation (alternative
  fuel that bypasses glycolytic
  bottlenecks), ATP synthesis support.

FEATURE 5: RISING NEUROINFLAMMATORY
  SIGNAL AS TRIGGER
  The pre-ictal period in many
  patients is preceded or accompanied
  by rising inflammatory tone.
  IL-1β, COX-2, prostaglandins.
  These inflammatory mediators:
    Directly lower GABA-A receptor
    sensitivity (target Feature 2).
    Enhance NMDA receptor activation
    (target Feature 1).
    Reduce glymphatic clearance
    of excitatory metabolites.
  Neuroinflammation is both a cause
  and consequence of the pre-ictal
  state. It is a positive feedback
  loop within the pre-ictal trajectory.

  GEOMETRIC DESCRIPTION:
  Neuroinflammation is simultaneously:
    Lowering the rim (via GABA-A
    receptor desensitisation).
    Pushing the navigator toward
    the rim (via NMDA enhancement).
    Accelerating the trajectory
    by fuelling both movements.
  It is a multiplier on the
  pre-ictal convergence speed.

  TARGET: Acutely reduce the
  neuroinflammatory multiplier.
  Mechanism: acute anti-inflammatory
  intervention at the first
  warning sign.

SUMMARY OF FIVE FEATURES AND TARGETS:

  Feature 1 → Reduce excitatory tone.
  Feature 2 → Restore inhibitory tone.
  Feature 3 → Disrupt hypersynchrony.
  Feature 4 → Support metabolic buffer.
  Feature 5 → Reduce inflammatory multiplier.

  These are the five derived
  drug targets of the pre-ictal window.
  They are derived from the geometry.
  Not from the drug catalogue.
  Not from what already exists.
  From what the geometry of the
  approaching trajectory REQUIRES
  to be reversed.
```

## PART II: THE TRAJECTORY REVERSAL
## OPERATIONS IN DETAIL

### Operation 0A: Reduce Excitatory Tone

```
GEOMETRY:
  The excitatory vector force
  pushing the navigator toward the rim
  must be reduced acutely.
  This is not chronic rim elevation —
  it is acute suppression of the
  force pushing toward the rim
  RIGHT NOW.

MECHANISM CLASS 1: Glutamate reduction
  NMDA receptor partial blockade.
  NOT full blockade —
  the navigator still needs to
  operate. Full NMDA blockade
  causes dissociation, anaesthesia.
  PARTIAL blockade reduces
  the pathological excess while
  preserving normal function.
  This is the memantine mechanism —
  it is already identified as
  a post-ictal compound but
  it is also a pre-ictal compound
  by the same mechanism.

  Magnesium (OTC):
  The endogenous NMDA partial blocker.
  Pre-ictally: acute magnesium
  loading directly inserts into
  NMDA channels and reduces the
  pathological calcium influx
  that is driving the excitatory
  escalation.

MECHANISM CLASS 2: Sodium channel
  stabilisation.
  Voltage-gated sodium channels
  (Nav) have an inactivated state.
  AEDs like phenytoin, carbamazepine,
  lamotrigine work by stabilising
  the inactivated state —
  making channels harder to reopen
  after firing.
  Pre-ictal rapid sodium channel
  stabilisation would acutely
  reduce the network's ability
  to sustain the rapid firing
  that drives the excitatory spiral.

  Prescription target: A fast-acting
  sodium channel stabiliser with
  rapid CNS access.
  Existing candidates: intranasal
  or sublingual lamotrigine
  (formulation development required —
  current formulations are oral
  daily doses, not acute single doses).
  Novel target: A rapidly-absorbed
  sodium channel stabiliser
  designed for as-needed use.

MECHANISM CLASS 3: AMPA receptor
  internalisation.
  AMPA receptors are the primary
  mediator of fast excitatory
  transmission.
  Pre-ictally, surface AMPA
  receptor density increases —
  part of the excitatory escalation.
  A compound that accelerates
  AMPA receptor internalisation
  (removing them from the synapse)
  would acutely reduce the gain
  of the excitatory network.

  Existing AMPA antagonists:
  Perampanel (Fycompa) — approved AED.
  It works at the AMPA receptor.
  But it is a daily oral medication.
  Not designed for as-needed
  acute pre-ictal use.
  The geometric framework derives
  that an as-needed, rapid-onset
  AMPA receptor blocker is
  a priority drug target for
  pre-ictal intervention.
  This does not exist in clinical
  practice as an acute formulation.
  It is a derived novel target.
```

### Operation 0B: Restore Inhibitory Tone

```
GEOMETRY:
  The rim is descending.
  The inhibitory system is failing.
  Acute GABA support raises the rim
  back to its normal height before
  the navigator reaches it.

MECHANISM: GABA-A receptor enhancement.
  The most direct way to restore
  inhibitory tone is to enhance
  the receptor that mediates it.
  GABA-A receptors, when activated,
  open chloride channels.
  Chloride influx hyperpolarises
  the neuron — raises the local
  barrier to further depolarisation.
  This directly raises the rim.

  THIS IS THE MECHANISM OF
  BENZODIAZEPINES.

  Benzodiazepines (diazepam,
  midazolam, lorazepam, clonazepam):
  They are GABA-A positive
  allosteric modulators.
  They increase the frequency
  of GABA-A channel opening
  in response to GABA.
  They acutely raise the rim.

  This is why benzodiazepines are
  the primary rescue medication
  for ongoing seizures.
  But the framework derives
  something the clinical literature
  does not emphasise:

  BENZODIAZEPINES ARE MORE GEOMETRICALLY
  EFFICIENT AS PRE-ICTAL AGENTS
  THAN AS ICTAL AGENTS.

  Ictal use: the navigator has
  already crossed the rim.
  The false attractor basin walls
  are steep and the navigator is
  inside. The benzodiazepine must
  work against an already-established
  seizure discharge.

  Pre-ictal use: the navigator
  is approaching the rim.
  The benzodiazepine raises the rim
  BEFORE the navigator reaches it.
  The trajectory reversal happens
  when the rim is close but not
  yet crossed. Less drug needed.
  Higher probability of success.
  Less post-ictal cognitive burden.
  No status epilepticus risk.

  DERIVED CLINICAL PROTOCOL:
  Benzodiazepines should be
  considered as PRE-ICTAL agents
  when reliable warning symptoms exist.
  Not only as ICTAL rescue.
  The patient with reliable prodrome
  symptoms (like the described
  fist clenching, photophobia,
  fatigue pattern) has an intervention
  window that opens BEFORE the
  rim is crossed.
  Use of a small benzodiazepine
  dose at first warning symptom
  may prevent the full seizure.
  This is not currently standard
  clinical practice.
  It is derived from the geometry.

  WHAT THIS REQUIRES CLINICALLY:
    A prescribed standing order
    for a pre-ictal benzodiazepine
    dose from the neurologist.
    Identification of reliable
    warning symptoms by the patient
    and neurologist together.
    Agreement on the dose threshold
    (low enough to not cause excessive
    sedation from every warning event,
    high enough to raise the rim
    sufficiently).

  OTC PATHWAY:
  No GABA-A enhancer is available
  OTC without risk.
  Alcohol is a GABA-A enhancer —
  it raises the rim acutely.
  This is why some patients
  with epilepsy anecdotally note
  that a small amount of alcohol
  seems to suppress warning symptoms.
  But alcohol is not a safe or
  reliable pre-ictal intervention —
  tolerance, withdrawal, rebound
  excitation (alcohol withdrawal
  is a seizure trigger), and
  interaction with AEDs all
  disqualify it.

  INDIRECT OTC GABA SUPPORT:
  L-theanine (amino acid, tea):
  Crosses the BBB.
  Increases GABA synthesis indirectly.
  Reduces glutamate:GABA ratio.
  Not a benzodiazepine.
  Not a direct GABA-A enhancer.
  But it shifts the excitatory-
  inhibitory balance toward
  inhibition.
  Available OTC.
  Safe interaction profile.
  Fast-acting (peaks within 30-60min).
  Relevant mechanism for the
  pre-ictal window.

  Taurine (amino acid, OTC):
  Acts as a GABA-A and glycine
  receptor ligand.
  Inhibitory neurotransmitter
  function.
  Naturally found in the brain.
  Can be supplemented OTC.
  Less potent than benzodiazepines
  by orders of magnitude but
  mechanistically relevant.
```

### Operation 0C: Disrupt Hypersynchrony

```
GEOMETRY:
  Synchrony is what channels the
  navigator's trajectory toward
  the rim rather than diffusing it.
  Disrupting synchrony diffuses
  the trajectory — spreads the
  excitation across more time
  and space, reducing its ability
  to summate into a seizure.

  THINK OF IT THIS WAY:
  A seizure requires many neurons
  to fire together at the same time.
  That synchrony is like an army
  marching in step.
  Disrupting synchrony is like
  causing the army to march out
  of step — the individual soldiers
  (neurons) are still moving
  but the collective march
  (seizure) cannot organise.

MECHANISMS FOR SYNCHRONY DISRUPTION:

  MECHANISM A: Gap junction blockade.
  Gap junctions are direct electrical
  connections between neurons.
  They are a primary mechanism of
  rapid synchronisation.
  When many neurons are connected
  by gap junctions, an electrical
  signal spreads instantly across
  the network — perfect synchrony.
  Blocking gap junctions slows
  signal propagation and reduces
  the network's ability to
  achieve hypersynchrony.

  Existing gap junction blocker:
  Quinine (OTC-adjacent: tonic water).
  Quinine blocks connexin-36
  gap junctions — the specific
  type expressed in neurons.
  Tonic water contains quinine.
  This is NOT a clinical dosing
  route — the quinine concentration
  in tonic water is far below
  pharmacological dosing.
  But the mechanism is real
  and the compound is identified.

  A novel connexin-36 selective
  blocker optimised for CNS
  delivery and pre-ictal use
  is a derived drug target.
  Does not exist currently
  as a clinical compound.
  The geometry defines it.

  MECHANISM B: Serotonin tone
  maintenance.
  Serotonergic neurons in the
  raphe nuclei project widely
  across the cortex and modulate
  network synchrony.
  Higher serotonin tone:
    Reduces burst firing in
    pyramidal neurons.
    Increases inhibitory interneuron
    activity in some circuits.
    Reduces network synchronisability —
    makes it harder for the
    network to achieve the coherent
    firing required for seizure onset.

  The 5-HT system is already
  identified in this framework
  as the SUDEP prevention target
  (prucalopride for brainstem
  arousal).
  Here it appears AGAIN as
  a pre-ictal hypersynchrony
  disruptor.
  The serotonin system is a
  multi-window target:
    Pre-ictal: synchrony disruption.
    SUDEP: brainstem arousal.
    Both via 5-HT mechanisms.

  OTC 5-HT precursor loading:
  5-HTP (5-hydroxytryptophan):
  Available OTC.
  Direct serotonin precursor.
  Crosses the BBB.
  Increases brain serotonin synthesis.
  Fast-acting: serotonin levels
  begin rising within 30-60 minutes.
  Pre-ictal timing: at first
  warning symptom, 50-100mg 5-HTP.

  MECHANISM C: Adenosine enhancement.
  Adenosine is the brain's
  endogenous anticonvulsant.
  It accumulates during high
  neuronal activity.
  It acts as a natural brake —
  A1 adenosine receptors reduce
  excitatory neurotransmission
  and reduce neuronal firing rate.
  The post-seizure fatigue and
  sleep pressure is partly mediated
  by adenosine accumulation.
  Pre-ictally: adenosine levels
  may be depleted (or the system
  is not yet saturated) allowing
  the excitatory spiral to proceed.

  OTC adenosine system support:
  Caffeine is an adenosine receptor
  blocker — it is pro-convulsant
  for this reason.
  Therefore: caffeine withdrawal
  or avoidance is an OTC
  pre-ictal intervention.
  The geometry derives:
    Do NOT have caffeine during
    a recognised pre-ictal state.
    Caffeine blocks the brain's
    own anticonvulsant mechanism
    (adenosine) exactly when it
    is needed most.

  Is there an OTC adenosine
  enhancer? Not directly.
  Inosine (OTC supplement):
  A precursor in the adenosine
  synthesis pathway.
  Can increase adenosine availability.
  Mechanistically relevant.
  This is a weakly evidenced
  but geometrically derived target.
```

### Operation 0D: Support Metabolic Buffer

```
GEOMETRY:
  The landscape is degrading
  under the navigator's feet
  as metabolic reserve is consumed.
  The valley floor rises toward
  the rim as ATP depletes.
  Restoring ATP and providing
  alternative fuel substrates
  re-establishes the height
  difference between valley floor
  and rim.

THIS IS THE MOST UNDERAPPRECIATED
TARGET IN THE PRE-ICTAL WINDOW.

MECHANISM A: Ketone body
  supplementation.
  The brain normally runs on glucose.
  Ketone bodies (beta-hydroxybutyrate,
  acetoacetate) are an alternative
  fuel that the brain can use.

  THE KETOGENIC DIET IS THE
  MOST EVIDENCE-RICH NON-PHARMACOLOGICAL
  EPILEPSY INTERVENTION THAT EXISTS.
  It works by shifting brain fuel
  utilisation toward ketones.
  Its anticonvulsant mechanism
  is complex but includes:
    Direct potassium channel
    opening (KATP channels) —
    which hyperpolarises neurons
    (raises rim locally).
    Reduced glutamate synthesis
    (fewer carbons available for
    glutamate from ketone metabolism
    vs. glucose metabolism).
    Increased GABA synthesis
    (ketone metabolism generates
    acetyl-CoA which feeds the
    GABA synthesis pathway differently).
    Reduced reactive oxygen species.
    Mitochondrial biogenesis.

  The ketogenic diet takes weeks
  to establish ketosis.
  It is a chronic treatment.

  BUT:
  Exogenous ketone bodies can
  achieve ACUTE ketosis within
  30-60 minutes.
  Ketone esters and ketone salts
  are available OTC and as
  food supplements.

  A pre-ictal dose of exogenous
  ketones (beta-hydroxybutyrate
  supplement) taken at first
  warning symptom could:
    Rapidly provide alternative
    fuel to the metabolically
    stressed pre-ictal tissue.
    Restore the ATP-dependent
    sodium-potassium pump function
    that is flagging.
    Open KATP channels acutely —
    the same channels the
    ketogenic diet operates through.
    Reduce glutamate availability
    in the pre-ictal network.

  GEOMETRIC SIGNIFICANCE:
  This is the only pre-ictal OTC
  compound that directly addresses
  the metabolic buffer degradation
  that makes seizures physically
  inevitable once ATP drops far enough.
  It re-establishes the valley floor.

  Available OTC today:
  Beta-hydroxybutyrate (BHB) salts
  or esters (ketone supplements,
  sold for sports nutrition and
  ketogenic diet support).
  Cost: $2-5 per dose.

MECHANISM B: Glucose management.
  The pre-ictal network is
  hypometabolic relative to its
  demands.
  A rapidly available glucose
  source ensures the network
  does not become substrate-limited
  during the excitatory escalation.

  This is nuanced:
  Hyperglycaemia (too much glucose)
  can itself be pro-convulsant
  through osmotic and other mechanisms.
  Normal blood glucose is the target.

  For patients who are fasted
  or hypoglycaemic during a
  pre-ictal warning event:
  Oral glucose (a glucose tablet,
  fruit juice) is an OTC
  metabolic buffer intervention.
  Not always relevant.
  Relevant if the warning event
  is occurring in a fasted state
  or late-morning after skipping
  breakfast.

MECHANISM C: Creatine loading.
  Creatine phosphate is the
  brain's immediate ATP buffer.
  When ATP depletes rapidly,
  creatine phosphate donates
  its phosphate group to regenerate
  ATP from ADP — buying seconds
  to minutes of additional
  metabolic buffer time.

  OTC creatine (creatine monohydrate):
  Available. Inexpensive.
  Chronically supplemented creatine
  increases brain creatine phosphate
  stores.
  This gives the pre-ictal network
  more buffer time before ATP
  falls to the critical threshold.
  This is a CHRONIC pre-ictal
  preparation — not an acute dose.
  Daily creatine supplementation
  (3-5g/day) pre-loads the
  ATP buffer.
  When the pre-ictal metabolic
  crisis begins, a larger creatine
  phosphate reserve buys more time.
  More time = more opportunity for
  inhibitory systems to restore
  balance = lower probability of
  seizure.

  OTC. Cheap. Available today.
  Used widely in sports nutrition.
  The epilepsy application is
  geometrically derived here.
```

### Operation 0E: Reduce the Inflammatory Multiplier

```
GEOMETRY:
  The inflammatory multiplier
  accelerates both Feature 1
  (rising excitation) and Feature 2
  (declining inhibition) simultaneously.
  It is a force multiplier on
  the rate of rim approach.
  Reducing it in the pre-ictal
  window slows the convergence
  and may allow other interventions
  time to work.

  This is the same neuroinflammation
  cascade targeted by the post-ictal
  protocol — but the timing is different.
  Post-ictal: the cascade is running
  after the seizure and building the
  landscape modification.
  Pre-ictal: the cascade is contributing
  TO the threshold lowering that
  enables the seizure.

  THE SAME COMPOUNDS APPLY
  WITH DIFFERENT TIMING:

  Acute naproxen:
  Given at the FIRST warning symptom
  rather than at T+6h post-seizure.
  Target: reduce the prostaglandin-
  mediated GABA-A receptor desensitisation
  and NMDA potentiation that is
  accelerating the pre-ictal trajectory.
  Timing: within the pre-ictal window
  (which may be 30 minutes to hours
  before seizure onset).

  Acute magnesium:
  Again appearing in this window.
  Magnesium directly counters
  the NMDA potentiation that
  inflammation drives.
  Mg → NMDA partial block →
  reduces the inflammatory enhancement
  of excitatory signalling.

  Omega-3 (acute dose):
  EPA/DHA shifts prostaglandin
  composition pre-ictally.
  This is slower than naproxen
  but relevant in a longer
  prodromal window.
```

## PART III: THE FIVE WINDOWS OF THE
## PRE-ICTAL STATE

```
Just as the post-ictal cascade
has five timed windows,
the pre-ictal trajectory has
its own temporal structure.

The pre-ictal window works BACKWARDS
from seizure onset.
Instead of T+0 being seizure end,
T-0 is seizure onset.
We count backwards.

T-HOURS (prodromal phase):
  Hours before seizure.
  Features present: fatigue,
  mood change, photosensitivity,
  clumsiness, increased interictal
  discharges.
  What is happening:
    Inflammatory tone rising.
    GABAergic tone beginning to slip.
    Threshold beginning to descend.
  BEST WINDOW FOR INTERVENTION.
  The navigator is still far
  from the rim.
  The trajectory reversal operation
  requires the least force here.
  OTC compounds are most effective
  at this distance.

  TARGET: Feature 5 (inflammation)
  and Feature 2 (inhibitory tone).
  COMPOUNDS:
    Naproxen 500mg (immediate).
    Magnesium 400-600mg (immediate).
    Exogenous ketones (BHB, begin fuelling).
    L-theanine 200mg.
    5-HTP 50-100mg.
    Creatine (already pre-loaded daily).
    DARK QUIET ROOM — sensory load
    reduction is itself a trajectory
    deceleration by reducing excitatory
    input to the already-sensitised
    cortex.

T-30 to T-60 minutes:
  EEG changes becoming detectable.
  Network synchrony beginning to rise.
  Fist clenching, clear motor signs.
  What is happening:
    Excitatory tone rising rapidly.
    Network synchrony increasing.
    Metabolic consumption accelerating.
  MODERATE WINDOW FOR INTERVENTION.
  OTC compounds are partially effective.
  Prescription rescue is the priority.

  TARGET: Features 1, 2, 3, 4.
  COMPOUNDS:
    Prescription pre-ictal
    benzodiazepine (if available).
    Continue magnesium, ketones.
    REMOVE ALL SENSORY TRIGGERS.
    Lie down, stillness.
    This is not comfort seeking —
    it is seizure prevention.
    Motor activity increases
    metabolic demand and can
    accelerate the trajectory.

T-15 minutes:
  Clear pre-ictal EEG changes.
  Strong motor/sensory warning.
  All five features active.
  What is happening:
    The navigator is close to rim.
    The trajectory is steep.
```
