  "Magnesium supplementation shown
   to reduce seizures in:
     — Hypomagnesemia-induced epilepsy

       (improved outcomes vs standard

     — Some drug-resistant epilepsy
       subgroups."
  "A 2021 study: dietary magnesium
   intake associated with better
   seizure freedom even when serum
   levels did not correlate."

MAGNESIUM L-THREONATE SPECIFIC:
  "Liu et al. 2016: double-blind RCT
   in adults 50-70 with cognitive
   complaints. MgT (Magtein) over
   12 weeks showed significant
   improvements in executive function,
   working memory, attention vs placebo."
  "Consistent with animal data showing
   raised brain/hippocampal magnesium."
  "No direct measurement of human
   hippocampal magnesium after MgT —
   this confirmation is still needed."
  "Animal studies: specifically
   increases synaptic magnesium in
   hippocampus — the primary target
   region in temporal lobe epilepsy."

AED INTERACTION NOTE:

  interactions identified.

EVIDENCE GRADE FOR THIS COMPOUND:
  Mechanism: A (established, textbook)
  Animal efficacy: A (seizure induction
  via deficiency is a standard model)
  Human epidemiology: B (consistent
  association with seizure risk)
  Human supplementation trials: B

  Post-ictal acute replenishment:
  C (mechanistically derived,
  not directly trialled)
  Safety: A (well-established)

   melatonin might have PRO-CONVULSIVE
