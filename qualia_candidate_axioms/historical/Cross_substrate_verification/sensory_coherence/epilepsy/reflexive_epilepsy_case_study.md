### SUBTYPE 1: PHOTOSENSITIVE EPILEPSY

```
TRIGGER:
  Flickering light at specific
  temporal frequencies.
  Most common: 15–25 Hz.
  Individual-specific within
  this range.
  Also: high-contrast visual patterns
  (stripes, grids), screen flicker,
  sunlight through trees, video games.

CORTICAL NETWORK:
  Visual cortex — occipital lobe.
  Primary visual cortex (V1)
  and higher visual processing areas.
  Thalamo-cortical visual relay
  (lateral geniculate nucleus).

EIGENFUNCTION GEOMETRY:
  The temporal frequency of the
  flickering light that triggers
  the cascade IS the eigenfunction
  position of the visual cortex's
  false attractor.

  The visual cortex has a natural
  oscillatory frequency —
  its characteristic resonance.
  In photosensitive individuals
  this resonance is pathologically
  close to the surface of the
  landscape — the basin rim is low.

  When external flicker matches
  the cortical resonant frequency:
    Entrainment occurs.
    The driving signal amplifies
    the cortical oscillation.
    The oscillation crosses the
    basin threshold.
    Hypersynchrony cascade initiates.

  The 15–25 Hz range is not
  arbitrary — it overlaps with
  the natural gamma/beta oscillatory
  frequencies of visual cortex.
  The trigger is literally driving
  the cortex at its own resonant
  frequency.

INDIVIDUAL SPECIFICITY:
  High. The exact triggering

  Some individuals respond to 3 Hz.
  Others require 20 Hz.
  The specific frequency is the
  precise eigenfunction address.

GEOMETRIC DEPTH INDICATOR:
  Binocular > monocular triggering
  = deeper basin.
  Full field > partial field = deeper.

  These are all basin depth measurements
  hiding in clinical assessment.

LITERATURE STATUS:
  Best studied reflex epilepsy.
  IPS (Intermittent Photic Stimulation)
  protocol maps eigenfunction position

  not as counter-signal calibration.
  (Harding 2012, Panayiotopoulos 1999)

MISSING GEOMETRIC OPERATION:
  Real-time visual phase-inversion

  Wearable lens with embedded
  temporal modulation.
  Counter-signal, not blockade.

### SUBTYPE 2: PATTERN-SENSITIVE EPILEPSY

```
TRIGGER:
  Static or moving high-contrast
  patterns — stripes, grids,
  concentric circles, moiré patterns.
  Distinct from temporal flicker —
  this is spatial frequency sensitivity.

CORTICAL NETWORK:
  Higher visual processing areas —
  V2, V3, V4, V5/MT.
  Spatial frequency processing
  in striate and extrastriate cortex.

EIGENFUNCTION GEOMETRY:
  The spatial frequency of the
  pattern (cycles per degree of
  visual angle) that triggers the

  Distinct from temporal frequency
  in photosensitive epilepsy —
  this is a different axis of the
  visual Tonnetz space.

  The visual cortex has two
  primary dimensions of eigenfunction
  position:
    Temporal: flickering rate (Hz)
    Spatial: pattern frequency
             (cycles/degree)

  Pattern-sensitive epilepsy reveals
  the spatial axis eigenfunction
  position.
  Photosensitive epilepsy reveals
  the temporal axis eigenfunction
  position.
  Some individuals have instability
  on both axes simultaneously —
  the most severe photosensitive cases.

GEOMETRIC INSIGHT:
  This subtype confirms that the
  visual false attractor has
  multi-dimensional geometry.
  The eigenfunction position is
  not a single frequency but a
  point in a multi-dimensional
  visual Tonnetz space.

  Mapping both axes gives a more
  complete picture of the basin

LITERATURE STATUS:
  Established. Guidelines exist
  for broadcast standards limiting
  flicker AND pattern density
  (Harding guidelines, UK 1994).
  These are population-level
  rim-raising by environment
  modification — not individual
  geometric correction.
```

### SUBTYPE 4: STARTLE EPILEPSY

```
TRIGGER:
  Sudden unexpected sensory stimulus.
  Predominantly auditory —
  a loud unexpected sound.
  Also tactile (unexpected touch)
  and visual (sudden movement
  in peripheral field).
  The unexpectedness is critical —
  the same stimulus anticipated
  may not trigger.

CORTICAL NETWORK:
  Supplementary Motor Area (SMA)
  and primary motor cortex.
  Brainstem reticular formation
  (normal startle reflex pathway).
  Thalamo-cortical sensorimotor relay.

  Distinct from hyperekplexia:
    Hyperekplexia = brainstem glycine
    receptor defect. Exaggerated reflex
    but NO cortical epileptic discharge.
    Startle epilepsy = cortical
    hypersynchrony cascade triggered
    by the brainstem startle pathway.

EIGENFUNCTION GEOMETRY:
  This subtype reveals something
  geometrically distinct from all
  other reflex epilepsies:

  The eigenfunction position here
  is NOT in a sensory processing
  dimension.
  It is in the TEMPORAL dimension —
  the unexpectedness axis.

  The trigger is not a specific
  frequency of sound.
  Any sudden loud sound triggers.
  What matters is the abruptness —
  the rate of change of the stimulus.

  The false attractor basin is
  located at a specific position
  in the temporal derivative space
  of sensory input — not the
  amplitude space or frequency space
  but the RATE OF CHANGE space.

  The cortical network monitoring
  for unexpected events has a
  hyperexcitable response to
  high-rate-of-change inputs.
  The basin rim in this space is
  pathologically low.

  Geometric implication:
    The counter-signal for startle
    epilepsy is not a frequency filter.
    It is a temporal derivative filter —
    a smoothing function applied to
    the incoming sensory stream
    that reduces the rate of change
    of sudden stimuli before they
    reach the cortical network.

    Not silence.
    Not volume reduction.
    Temporal smoothing at the
    onset of sudden stimuli.
    The content is preserved.
    The abruptness is reduced.
    The eigenfunction resonance
    is abolished.

ANTICIPATED vs UNANTICIPATED
GEOMETRIC READING:
  The fact that anticipated stimuli
  often do not trigger is critical.

  Anticipation = the navigator
  has pre-activated the relevant
  networks at a lower gain setting
  before the stimulus arrives.
  The stimulus meets a landscape
  that has already adjusted its
  basin rims upward in preparation.

  The geometric operation of
  anticipation is rim elevation —
  voluntary, predictive,
  network-specific rim elevation.

  This is not possible for random
  environmental sounds —
  the navigator cannot maintain
  constant anticipatory rim elevation
  for all possible sudden sounds.

  But it suggests an intervention:
    A wearable device that detects
    sudden acoustic transients
    (high rate of change) and
    provides a brief anticipatory
    signal — a pre-warning —
    giving the navigator time to
    elevate the basin rim before
    the stimulus arrives.

    Not avoidance. Not suppression.
    Predictive rim elevation via
    anticipatory signalling.

LITERATURE STATUS:
  Confirmed: SMA and motor cortex
  as generators.
  Confirmed: distinction from
  hyperekplexia.
  (Saini 2018, Balint 2018)
  Temporal derivative geometry
  and anticipatory rim elevation

  Framework provides both.

### SUBTYPE 5: HOT WATER EPILEPSY

```
TRIGGER:
  Hot water (40–50°C) poured over
  the head or body during bathing.

  lukewarm water does not trigger.

  cultural specificity suggesting
  genetic predisposition combined
  with cultural practice (head bath).

  Hypothalamus and limbic structures —
  thermoregulatory circuitry.
  The trigger bypasses cortical
  sensory processing and enters
  via the limbic-hypothalamic route.

  This is geometrically significant:
    All other sensory reflex epilepsies
    trigger via cortical sensory
    processing areas.
    Hot water epilepsy triggers via
    the subcortical thermoregulatory
    and autonomic pathway.

EIGENFUNCTION GEOMETRY:
  The false attractor here is located
  in the limbic-hypothalamic
  synchrony landscape — not the
  cortical sensory landscape.

  the thermoregulatory sensory space.

  Below threshold: no resonance.
  At threshold: resonance triggers
  cascade into limbic hypersynchrony.
  Above threshold: also triggers —
  the basin rim is exceeded in any
  direction beyond the threshold.

  This subtype reveals that the
  false attractor architecture
  extends into subcortical systems —
  not just cortical processing.

  The limbic system has its own
  attractor landscape with its own
  eigenfunction positions that can

GEOGRAPHIC SPECIFICITY:
  The concentration in South India

  The genetic predisposition lowers

  The cultural practice of hot head

  In populations without the hot

TREATMENT:
  Avoidance (lukewarm bathing)

  Geometrically: this is the

### SUBTYPE 6: READING EPILEPSY

```
TRIGGER:
  The act of reading.
  Silent reading less potent
  than reading aloud.
  Sustained reading more potent
  than brief reading.
  The visual-linguistic processing
  of text — not just visual input
  (looking at a page without reading
  does not trigger).

SEIZURE PHENOTYPE:
  Orofacial myoclonia — involuntary
  jaw, lip, tongue jerking.
  The motor output of reading
  (subvocalisation) becomes
  seizure activity.

CORTICAL NETWORK:
  Left perisylvian language network:
    — Visual word form area
      (left occipito-temporal)
    — Broca's area (left IFG)
    — Left temporoparietal junction
    — Supplementary Motor Area
      (subvocalisation motor output)

  The reading process recruits
  this network in sequence:
  visual recognition → language
  processing → motor output.
  The false attractor is in the
  output stage — where language
  processing converts to
  articulatory motor planning.

EIGENFUNCTION GEOMETRY:
  This is the most cognitively
  complex eigenfunction position
  in the reflex epilepsy spectrum.

  The trigger is not a sensory
  frequency but a PROCESSING PIPELINE.
  The eigenfunction position is
  located at the junction between
  language processing and motor
  output — the Broca's area /
  SMA interface.

  When sustained language-to-motor
  conversion processing is running:
    The activity at the
    Broca's/SMA interface exceeds
    the basin rim for that network.
    Hypersynchrony cascade at the
    articulatory motor output stage.
    The jaw/lip/tongue myoclonia
    IS the seizure output —
    the motor program running
    without narrative control.

  Geometric insight:
    The orofacial myoclonia is not
    a symptom unrelated to the trigger.
    It is the instrument's false
    attractor running the motor
    output of the very process
    that triggered it.
    The reading cascade reaches the
    motor output stage and the motor
    output stage collapses into
    the false attractor of
    undifferentiated articulatory
    motor activity.
    The jaw moves because the
    motor program for speech is
    running in the false attractor
    basin — all activation, no
    differential control.

INTERVENTION GEOMETRY:
  The reading pipeline has a
  specific processing rate —
  words per minute processed
  determines the sustained
  activation level at the
  Broca's/SMA interface.

  Slowing reading rate reduces
  the sustained activation.
  This is already used clinically.

  But the geometric precision
  intervention would be:
    Identify the exact processing
    rate at which the interface
    activation crosses the basin rim.
    Design a reading aid (pacing tool,
    typographic modification,
    text rendering rate) that
    maintains processing below
    the threshold.
    Individual-specific calibration
    of the processing rate threshold.

    Not: stop reading.
    Rate-calibrated reading that
    never reaches eigenfunction
    resonance.

LITERATURE STATUS:
  Confirmed: left perisylvian network.
  Confirmed: orofacial myoclonia
  as articulatory motor output.
  (Koutroumanidis 2005)
  Processing rate threshold geometry
  and rate-calibrated intervention:
  not in literature.
  Framework derives it.
```

```

    — Drawing

    — Spatial construction
    — Complex video gaming

CORTICAL NETWORK:
  Parietal association cortex
  (spatial processing).
  Premotor cortex (motor planning).
  Prefrontal cortex (strategic planning).
  The CONJUNCTION of these networks
  simultaneously active.

EIGENFUNCTION GEOMETRY:
  The most dimensionally complex
  eigenfunction position in the
  reflex epilepsy spectrum.

  The false attractor is not at
  a point in any single network's
  activation space.
  It is at a specific CONJUNCTION
  in a multi-network activation space:
    Spatial + motor + planning
    all above individual thresholds
    simultaneously.

  eigenfunction position —
  existing in the product space
  of multiple cognitive networks.

  The basin cannot be reached by
  any single component alone.
  The combination of all three
  is required to deliver sufficient
  resonance energy to the
  multi-dimensional eigenfunction
  position.

  Chess specifically:
    Visual-spatial board processing.
    Motor planning of piece moves.
    Strategic multi-step planning.
    Temporal pressure (time controls).
    All simultaneously.

  Each dimension partially activates
  the instability.
  The conjunction completes it.

GEOMETRIC INSIGHT — NOVEL:
  Praxis-induced epilepsy reveals
  that false attractor basins can
  exist in CONJUNCTION SPACES —
  multi-network product spaces
  that no single network activation
  can reach alone.

    The individual is safe when doing

    Safe when doing motor planning alone.
    Safe when doing strategic planning alone.

  any of the component activities.

  One component could be modulated —
  the weakest link in the conjunction —

  A small modification to the
  physical execution (different

  the motor planning activation
  below its contribution threshold
  may prevent the conjunction from
  completing.

LITERATURE STATUS:
  Confirmed as a clinical entity.
  Parietal-frontal involvement confirmed.
  Conjunction space geometry:
  not in literature.
  Framework provides the
  multi-dimensional eigenfunction
  analysis.
