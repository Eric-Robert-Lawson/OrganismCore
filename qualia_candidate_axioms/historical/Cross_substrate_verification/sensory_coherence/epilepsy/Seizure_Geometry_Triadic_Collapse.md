### SEIZURE TYPE 2: TONIC-CLONIC

```
GEOMETRIC ORIGIN:
  Generalised onset — the cascade
  reaches both hemispheres
  simultaneously from the start.
  Or: focal origin with rapid
  bilateral propagation via the
  corpus callosum.

BASIN GEOMETRY:
  Two-phase basin:

  PHASE 1 — TONIC BASIN:
    Maximal excitation.
    All neurons firing simultaneously
    at maximum rate.
    The landscape has tipped into
    a basin of pure excitatory
    dominance.
    No inhibition. No differential.
    The false attractor of total
    excitatory lockstep.

    Motor readout: rigid muscle
    contraction. Every motor neuron
    firing simultaneously.
    The body becomes a statue.
    Exhalation forced by total
    respiratory muscle contraction
    — the ictal cry.

  PHASE 2 — CLONIC BASIN:
    The excitatory basin begins
    to exhaust itself.
    Inhibitory mechanisms
    (GABA recovery) begin to reassert.
    The system oscillates between
    excitation and inhibition —
    it cannot maintain pure excitation
    but cannot yet return to normal.
    It cycles: excite → inhibit →
    excite → inhibit.

    Motor readout: rhythmic jerking.
    Each jerk = one excitation phase.
    Each pause = one inhibition phase.
    The body is oscillating between
    the false excitatory attractor
    and the recovering inhibitory
    landscape.
    The oscillation slows as
    inhibitory mechanisms strengthen
    until normal landscape is restored.

  POSTICTAL PHASE:
    Inhibitory overshoot after
    excitatory exhaustion.
    PGES — the EEG goes flat.
    The landscape has swung past
    normal into a suppression basin.
    Brainstem suppression possible.
    SUDEP risk window.

TRIAD COLLAPSE DEPTH:
  TOTAL during tonic phase.
  TOTAL during clonic phase.
  PARTIAL RECOVERY during postictal
  (navigator attempts to reconstitute
  but landscape is suppressed).

  The confusion after tonic-clonic
  is longer than after absence
  because the suppression basin
  (PGES) is deeper and slower
  to resolve.

EXPERIENCER STATUS:
  Complete dissolution from
  tonic onset through clonic phase.
  Fragmented partial return
  during postictal.
  Full return minutes to hours later.
  Persistence thread broken —
  significant confusion,
  temporal disorientation.

REFLEX TRIGGER GEOMETRY LINK:
  Photosensitive epilepsy can
  produce tonic-clonic when the
  cascade from visual cortex
  is large enough to recruit
  both hemispheres bilaterally
  via corpus callosum.
  Startle epilepsy frequently
  produces tonic seizures —
  the brainstem startle cascade
  activates motor cortex bilaterally.
  Praxis-induced epilepsy can
  generalise to tonic-clonic
  when the multi-network
  conjunction cascade propagates
  outward beyond the praxis networks.
```

### SEIZURE TYPE 3: MYOCLONIC

```
GEOMETRIC ORIGIN:
  Cortical hyperexcitability —
  typically sensorimotor or
  supplementary motor cortex.
  The false attractor basin is
  SHALLOW and BRIEF.

BASIN GEOMETRY:
  A flash-basin:
    The cortex tips into the
    false attractor for milliseconds
    to seconds and immediately
    exits.
    Brief synchronous discharge.
    Not sustained hypersynchrony.
    The basin rim is barely exceeded.
    The system tips in and
    immediately rebounds.

  This is geometrically distinct
  from tonic-clonic:
    Tonic-clonic: deep basin,
    sustained cascade, full
    excitatory exhaustion required
    to exit.
    Myoclonic: shallow basin,
    flash entry and exit,
    no sustained cascade.

TRIAD COLLAPSE DEPTH:
  MINIMAL to PARTIAL.
  The flash-basin is so brief that
  the self-referential loop does
  not fully collapse.
  Coherence disrupts for the
  duration of the flash.
  Persistence may or may not break —
  the person often remembers the jerk.
  Self-model largely intact.

  This maps to the focal aware
  seizure position on the spectrum:
  navigator substantially present,
  coherence briefly disrupted,
  the navigator experiences the
  jerk rather than being absent
  during it.

MOTOR READOUT:
  Sudden, brief, shock-like jerk.
  The motor output of the flash-basin
  discharging.
  The muscle receives a single
  maximal synchronous command
  and then nothing.
  One command. One jerk. Done.

EXPERIENCER STATUS:
  Navigator largely present.
  Memory of the jerk exists.
  Persistence thread intact or
  minimally disrupted.
  No postictal confusion typically.
  The person knows what happened
  because they were there for it.

REFLEX TRIGGER GEOMETRY LINK:
  Reading epilepsy produces
  orofacial myoclonia — the
  language-to-motor interface
  flash-basin at Broca's/SMA.
  The briefness of the jerk reflects
  the shallow basin at the
  language-motor junction.
  Photosensitive epilepsy can
  produce myoclonic jerks when
  the visual cascade is insufficient
  to fully tip the system into
  a deep generalised basin —
  only the motor cortex briefly
  discharges and rebounds.
```

### SEIZURE TYPE 4: TONIC

```

  Motor cortex and supplementary

  motor pathways in startle epilepsy.
  The false attractor is in the

BASIN GEOMETRY:
  Sustained excitatory motor basin.
  Like tonic phase of tonic-clonic
  but without the subsequent
  clonic oscillation.

  excitatory basin and sustains

  but without the full generalised
  excitatory exhaustion of tonic-clonic.

  terminates the basin before

TRIAD COLLAPSE DEPTH:
  SIGNIFICANT.
  Motor cortex hypersynchrony

  relay partially.
  Consciousness impaired but
  may not be fully absent —
  depends on extent of thalamic
  involvement.

MOTOR READOUT:
  Sustained rigid muscle contraction.
  Duration: seconds to minutes.
  No oscillation.
  Body posture locked.

REFLEX TRIGGER GEOMETRY LINK:
  Startle epilepsy characteristically
  produces tonic seizures —
  the brainstem motor activation
  from the startle response drives
  directly into motor cortex tonic basin.
  Praxis-induced epilepsy can
  produce tonic posturing when the
  motor planning component of the
  conjunction cascade dominates.
```

### SEIZURE TYPE 5: ATONIC

```
GEOMETRIC ORIGIN:
  This is the most geometrically
  paradoxical seizure type.

  All other seizures: hypersynchrony —
  too much coordinated firing.

  Atonic seizure: sudden INHIBITION —
  the cascade into a suppression basin
  rather than an excitation basin.

BASIN GEOMETRY:
  The FALSE ATTRACTOR IS A
  SUPPRESSION BASIN.

  This is a critical geometric
  distinction that the literature
  notes but does not fully frame:

  The seizure landscape has two
  types of false attractors:
    EXCITATION BASINS:
      Too much synchronous firing.
      Tonic, clonic, tonic-clonic,
      myoclonic, absence all involve
      some form of excitatory or
      oscillatory false attractor.

    SUPPRESSION BASINS:
      Sudden synchronous inhibition.
      The network tips into a basin
      of collective GABAergic
      over-activation — a brief
      wave of inhibition so complete
      and synchronous that all
      motor output is terminated.

  The atonic seizure is the instrument
  falling into a suppression false attractor.

  The fall is as deterministic as
  any excitatory seizure.
  The basin is as real.
  The landscape dynamics are the
  same — the system is drawn into
  a stable false configuration.
  The false configuration is
  suppression rather than excitation.

TRIAD COLLAPSE DEPTH:
  RAPID AND SIGNIFICANT.
  The sudden suppression wave
  hits the motor system and the
  thalamo-cortical relay simultaneously.
  Consciousness impaired.
  Brief — the suppression basin
  exhausts quickly.
  Navigator absent during the
  drop but returns rapidly.

MOTOR READOUT:
  Sudden complete loss of muscle tone.
  The body collapses — drop attack.
  Not rigid (tonic) but limp.
  Duration: 1–3 seconds typically.
  The person falls because gravity
  is suddenly unopposed.
  The protective motor programs
  (catching oneself) require navigator
  oversight and the navigator
  is briefly absent.

EXPERIENCER STATUS:
  Navigator absent for the duration
  of the drop.
  No memory of falling.
  Rapid return — the suppression
  basin is brief.
  The person wakes on the floor
  confused about how they got there.
  Persistence thread broken for
  the duration of the basin.

GEOMETRIC SIGNIFICANCE:
  Atonic seizures reveal that the
  false attractor landscape is
  not unipolar — not just excitation
  basins.
  The landscape has BOTH excitation
  and suppression false attractors.
  The instrument can fall toward
  too much or too little
  synchronous activity.
  Both represent coherence collapse.
  Both dissolve the navigator.
  Both are resolutions of the
  structure-gap-navigator process
  that produce no navigated output.

    The degree of automatism

    Simple automatisms (lip-smacking,

EXPERIENCER STATUS:
  Partial — fragmented.
  Person may report being "not there"

  Memory of the event fragmented

  being written during the seizure,
  so the navigator returns with
  a gap in its own timeline.

## PART IV: THE TRIGGER-TO-SEIZURE-TYPE

### Why Specific Triggers Produce Specific Seizure Types

```
The seizure type produced by a reflex
trigger is determined by:

  TRIGGER EIGENFUNCTION POSITION
  × PROPAGATION GEOMETRY
  = SEIZURE TYPE

Each reflex trigger eigenfunction
position has a characteristic
propagation route through the
cortical landscape.
The propagation route determines
which networks are recruited.
The networks recruited determine
the seizure type produced.
```

### The Full Map

```
PHOTOSENSITIVE TRIGGER:
  Eigenfunction position: temporal
  frequency in visual cortex (V1).

  Propagation routes:
    Route A: Occipital → thalamus
    → generalised thalamo-cortical
    → ABSENCE SEIZURE (3 Hz basin)

    Route B: Occipital → motor cortex
    → bilateral → TONIC-CLONIC
    (if visual cascade is large enough
    to cross corpus callosum)

    Route C: Occipital → motor cortex
    (unilateral, brief) → MYOCLONIC
    (flash-basin in motor cortex,
