## 1. WHAT AGING IS NOT
##    CLEARING THE GROUND

```
Before stating what aging is
in attractor terms —
clear the ground of what it is not.

NOT PRIMARILY DAMAGE ACCUMULATION:

  The damage accumulation theory
  of aging (Harman, 1956) states:
    Reactive oxygen species damage
    DNA, proteins, and membranes
    over time.
    This damage accumulates.
    Eventually systems fail.

  The evidence against this as
  the PRIMARY cause:

  1. Naked mole rats live 10x longer
     than mice of the same body size.
     They accumulate MORE oxidative
     damage than mice, not less.
     Damage accumulation does not
     explain the lifespan difference.

  2. Caloric restriction extends
     lifespan in every species tested.
     It does not primarily reduce
     oxidative damage.
     It changes gene expression
     patterns — specifically the
     epigenetic state of cells.

  3. Partial reprogramming reverses
     aging WITHOUT clearing damage.
     Old cells with accumulated
     damage can be made young
     again by resetting their
     epigenetic state.
     If damage were primary —
     this would not work.

  Damage is a DOWNSTREAM MARKER
  of aging.
  The same relationship as:
    Amyloid is a downstream marker
    of Alzheimer's neuronal
    attractor instability.
    Tau is a downstream marker.
    The markers are real.
    They are not the cause.

NOT PRIMARILY TELOMERE SHORTENING:

  Telomeres shorten with each
  cell division.
  This is real.
  It limits replicative lifespan
  in dividing cells.

  But:
  Post-mitotic cells — neurons,
  cardiomyocytes — do not divide.
  Their telomeres do not shorten.
  They age anyway.

  Telomere shortening explains
  replicative senescence in
  specific cell types.
  It does not explain aging
  of the whole organism.

  Telomere shortening is a
  downstream consequence of
  attractor shallowing in
  dividing cell populations.
  Not the primary cause.

NOT PRIMARILY MITOCHONDRIAL DYSFUNCTION:

  Mitochondria accumulate
  mutations with age.
  Mitochondrial function declines.

  downstream of attractor shallowing

  machinery.

  The same relationship as
  every other aging hallmark.
```
