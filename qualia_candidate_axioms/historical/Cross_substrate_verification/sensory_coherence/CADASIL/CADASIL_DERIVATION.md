## PART I: THE STRUCTURAL BIOLOGY —
## WHAT CADASIL ACTUALLY IS

### 1.1 The Genetic Basis

```
CADASIL is caused by mutations in the
NOTCH3 gene on chromosome 19.

NOTCH3 encodes a transmembrane receptor
expressed primarily — and with highest
clinical relevance — in vascular smooth
muscle cells (VSMCs) of small arteries
and arterioles.

CRITICAL DEVELOPMENTAL POINT:
  NOTCH3 is NOT primarily a neuronal gene.
  NOTCH3 mutations do NOT cause
  developmental abnormalities.
  Patients with CADASIL develop normally.
  Cognitive and neurological function
  is normal in childhood and early
  adulthood.
  The nervous system was built correctly.
  What was built incorrectly was the
  long-term stability of the vascular
  smooth muscle cells that serve it.

  This is the foundational geometric
  distinction:

  EPILEPSY: the navigator substrate
  itself has a structural defect.

  CADASIL: the delivery infrastructure
  serving an intact navigator substrate
  has a progressive degenerative defect.

  These are different geometric problems
  requiring different geometric solutions.

The specific mutation mechanism:
  Nearly all pathogenic NOTCH3 variants
  are missense mutations affecting the
  EGF-like repeat domain of the
  extracellular domain (ECD).
  These mutations cause gain or loss
  of a cysteine residue.
  Cysteine residues form disulfide bonds
  that stabilise the EGF-like repeat structure.
  An odd number of cysteines means
  one cysteine has no pair.
  The unpaired cysteine forms
  aberrant intermolecular bonds
  with other NOTCH3 proteins.
  This causes aggregation.
  The aggregated, misfolded NOTCH3 ECD
  accumulates on the surface of VSMCs
  as granular osmiophilic material (GOM).

  Literature confirmation:
  Joutel et al., Nature 1996 —
  original NOTCH3 identification.
  Joutel et al., 2010 — ECD mechanism.
  Liem & Goodwin, Frontiers in Aging
  Neuroscience 2022 — mechanism review.
```

```

  Two independent mechanisms disrupt
```

```
  It is partial.
  But partial alternative fuel
```
