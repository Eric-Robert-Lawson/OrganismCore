## SECTION 1
## THE COGITATE RESULT AND THE OPEN FIELD

### 1.1 What Cogitate Was

The Cogitate Consortium was the most
rigorous adversarial empirical test
of consciousness theories ever conducted.

Design: Over 250 participants. Combined
fMRI, MEG, and intracranial EEG. Both
theory camps specified their predictions
in advance. Preregistered. Multi-site.
Theory-neutral labs. Joint analysis.

The two theories tested:

**Integrated Information Theory (IIT)**
Tononi and collaborators.
Core claim: Consciousness = Φ, the
quantity of integrated information
a system generates irreducibly —
the degree to which the whole exceeds
the sum of its parts causally.
Predicted signature: sustained
synchronized activity in posterior
cortical regions — the "hot zone" of
integrated sensory processing.

**Global Neuronal Workspace Theory (GWT/GNWT)**
Baars, Dehaene, Changeux.
Core claim: Consciousness = information
being globally broadcast across a
fronto-parietal network. Conscious
access occurs when information "ignites"
the global workspace — a non-linear
burst of activity making content
available to the entire brain.
Predicted signature: strong prefrontal
cortex "ignition" correlated with
conscious perception, especially at
stimulus onset and offset.

### 1.2 What Cogitate Found

Published 2023-2025 in Nature and
associated journals. Results:

**IIT:** The predicted sustained
synchronized activity in posterior
regions was not observed. The "hot zone"
integration signature was absent under
direct, preregistered testing conditions.

**GWT/GNWT:** The predicted prefrontal
ignition at conscious access was mostly
absent. Content encoded in prefrontal
activity was limited to coarse categories.
The hypothesized global broadcast
signature was not consistently found.

**Conclusion from Birmingham press release
(2025):** *"Neither comes out unscathed."*

**The adversarial test found no winner.**
Both theories suffered major empirical
setbacks. Neither was fully falsified —
both retain partial supporters and
ongoing revision programs. But neither
produced the clean confirmatory signal
that a successful empirical test requires.

### 1.3 The Open Field

The Cogitate result created a genuine
vacancy in consciousness theory.

The two most empirically tested theories
both failed their predictions. The field
is openly acknowledging the need for
new frameworks. The remaining active
contenders — the Free Energy Principle
(Friston), Higher Order Theories, and
various pluralist approaches — have
not yet been subjected to equivalent
adversarial testing.

**This is the moment when a new framework
with genuine predictive power, external
empirical grounding, and mathematical
clarity can enter the field.**

The spectral geometry framework is that
framework. This artifact establishes why.

**It enters with a further advantage:**
it is not a lone theoretical proposal.
It is the mathematical expression of
a principle — gap navigation that has
become self-referential — that has been
independently derived by two other
frameworks (IGH, Ship of Theseus) within
the same research program, starting from
entirely different entry points. The
convergence of three frameworks on the
same object from different starting
positions is itself evidence of contact
with something real.

### 2.1 IIT — What It Gets Right
### And Why It Failed

**What it gets right:**

IIT correctly identifies that consciousness
is connected to integration — to the
degree a system forms a unified whole
rather than a collection of independent
parts. The mathematical intuition is
sound: a system that cannot be
decomposed without losing information
is doing something that a decomposable
system is not doing.

IIT also correctly identifies that
consciousness is intrinsic — it is a
property of the system's causal structure,
not of what the system is doing or
representing externally.

**Why it failed:**

IIT is substrate-focused rather than
geometry-focused.

It asks: how much does this substrate
integrate information? It does not ask:
what geometry is the system navigating,
and what is the structure of that geometry?

More precisely in gap navigation terms:
IIT asks what the system IS. It does
not ask what the system is DOING — what
gap it is crossing, what geometry it is
traversing, whether the traversal has
become self-referential.

The Cogitate prediction failure reveals
this: IIT predicted specific activity
patterns in specific brain regions because
it was looking for integration in the
substrate — in the neural tissue itself.
But integration in the substrate is not
the same as the system navigating a
geometric space with eigenfunction
structure.

A system can have high Φ without ever
navigating gaps with sufficient depth
to find stable eigenfunction modes.
A system can be doing the thing that
matters — self-referential gap navigation
— without displaying the specific
posterior cortical signatures IIT
predicted.

The theory pointed at the wrong level
of description. It found a real property
(integration) but misidentified it as
the fundamental one. The fundamental
property is the gap navigation that
integration enables.

**The deeper failure:**

IIT cannot answer the pebble problem
internally. A pebble has a well-defined
causal structure. Friston noted this.
Tononi's response involves additional
constraints on the type of integration —
but these constraints are not derived
from the mathematical core of IIT. They
are added conditions to exclude
counterexamples.

A pebble does not navigate gaps.
A pebble has no navigation history.
The spectral geometry framework answers
the pebble problem from its mathematical
core. See Section 4.

navigator finds itself among the nodes
