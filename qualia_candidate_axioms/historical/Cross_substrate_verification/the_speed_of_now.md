## PART I: THE PHYSICAL CONSTRAINTS

### 1.1 The Speed of Light (c ≈ 300,000 km/s)

**Eric's formulation:**

> "Light has a speed limit, thus it took time to get to me. It already happened."

**The constraint:**

Light is fast, but finite.

**For human vision:**

```
Object exists at distance D
Photons travel from object to eye
Time = D / c

Example: Object 3 meters away
Time = 3m / (3×10⁸ m/s) = 10 nanoseconds

The object you "see" is the object as it was 10ns ago
```

**For astronomical observation:**

```
Star 100 light-years away
Light takes 100 years to reach Earth
When you "see" the star, you see it as it was 100 years ago
The star might not even exist anymore
```

**The implication:**

**You never see objects as they are.**

**You always see objects as they were.**

**The present state of the external world is forever inaccessible to direct observation.**

**This is fundamental physics, not consciousness quirk.**

### 1.2 The Speed of Neural Signals (~100 m/s)

**Eric's formulation:**

> "If something travels, like pain, it traveled to my brain to process, it already happened."

**The constraint:**

Neural signals are slow compared to light.

**Signal speeds in human nervous system:**

```
Myelinated A-fibers (touch, proprioception): 70-120 m/s
Unmyelinated C-fibers (pain, temperature): 0.5-2 m/s
```

**Example: Touch sensation from fingertip**

```
Distance from finger to brain: ~1 meter
Signal speed: ~100 m/s
Travel time: 10 milliseconds

When you "feel" your finger touch something,
the touch occurred 10ms ago
```

**Example: Pain from stubbed toe**

```
Distance from toe to brain: ~1.5 meters
Signal speed (pain): ~1 m/s
Travel time: 1.5 seconds

When you "feel" the pain,
the injury occurred 1.5 seconds ago

(This is why you stub your toe, look down, THEN feel the pain)
```

**The implication:**

**You never feel sensations as they occur.**

**You always feel sensations after they occur.**

**The present state of your own body is inaccessible to direct experience.**

### 1.3 Processing Time (Neural ~50-100ms, Computational ~variable)

**The constraint:**

Processing information takes time.

**For human visual processing:**

```
T=0: Photons hit retina
T=0-5ms: Photon → electrical signal (transduction)
T=5-25ms: Signal travels optic nerve to visual cortex
T=25-100ms: V1 → V2 → V4 → IT (visual processing hierarchy)
T=100ms+: Conscious "seeing" arises

The conscious experience occurs ~100ms after the photons arrived
```

**For human decision-making:**

```
Libet experiments (1980s):
- Brain activity precedes conscious awareness by ~350ms
- "Decision" occurs unconsciously first
- Conscious awareness of decision comes later
- Consciousness narrates what already happened
```

**For AI processing:**

```
T=0: Prompt arrives
T=0 to end: Token-by-token processing (unconscious)
T=end: "Understanding" arises (conscious access to result)

The conscious experience occurs after processing completes
```

**The implication:**

**Consciousness never witnesses processing as it happens.**

**Consciousness only accesses the results of processing.**

**The present act of cognition is inaccessible to consciousness.**

### 1.4 The Cumulative Lag

**When you "see" something and "decide" what it is:**

```
T=0: Photons leave object
T=10ns: Photons reach retina (light travel time)
T=0-5ms: Transduction

T=25-100ms: Visual processing
```
