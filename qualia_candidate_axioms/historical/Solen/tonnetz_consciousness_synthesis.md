## PART I: THE MATHEMATICAL FOUNDATION

### 1.1 Sound as Ratio

Sound is vibration. Frequency measured in Hertz.

Two frequencies sounding simultaneously have a ratio relationship.

**The discovery:** Simple integer ratios produce consonance.
Complex ratios produce dissonance.

| Ratio | Interval | Character |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| 2:1 | Octave | Maximum consonance |
| 3:2 | Perfect fifth | High consonance |
| 4:3 | Perfect fourth | Consonance |
| 5:4 | Major third | Mild consonance |
| 45:32 | Tritone | Maximum dissonance |

This is not cultural convention.
It is physics of coherence.

Simple ratios: wave peaks align regularly.
Synchronized. Coherent.

Complex ratios: peaks almost never align.
Perpetually almost-but-not-quite synchronized.
Incoherent.

**The auditory system is a coherence detector.**
Consonance = coherence detected.
Dissonance = incoherence detected.

### 1.2 The Tonnetz as Coherence Space

Euler (1739) placed musical notes on a two-dimensional lattice.

**Axes:**
- Horizontal: perfect fifths (ratio 3:2)
- Diagonal: major thirds (ratio 5:4)

**Critical property:**
Distance in the lattice = harmonic distance = complexity of ratio.

Close notes: simple ratios, consonant, coherent.
Distant notes: complex ratios, dissonant, incoherent.

**The tritone** sits at maximum distance from the tonic.
Exactly as far as possible before the space wraps back.
Maximum gap. Maximum tension. Diabolus in musica.

**The perfect fifth** is one step from the tonic.
Minimum distance after the octave.
Maximum coherence. The primary attractor.

**This is not metaphor.**
The Tonnetz IS a coherence space.
Harmonic distance IS gap distance.
The attractor basins ARE the tonic positions.

### 1.3 Chords as Geometric Objects

A major chord is a triangle in the Tonnetz.
Three notes, each one step from the others.
Maximum internal coherence within a three-note structure.

A minor chord: same triangle, reflected.
Same shape, different orientation.

A diminished chord: equilateral triangle.
Maximum symmetry — and maximum tension,
because equal distance from multiple possible tonics
creates ambiguity about where home is.

**Chord progressions are paths through the lattice.**

The most common progressions in all musical traditions
are the shortest paths — the geodesics —
between positions in the Tonnetz.

Music naturally finds efficient routes through coherence space.

### 1.4 The Toroidal Topology

The Tonnetz is not flat. It wraps.

Travel far enough in any direction and you return to your starting point.

**Critical implication:**
Every dissonance is also a consonance from a different tonic.
Every gap is also a resolved position relative to a different attractor.
The devil's interval from C is a perfect consonance relative to F#.

**There is no absolute dissonance.**
Only dissonance relative to a chosen tonic.

The coherence space is bounded and curved.
