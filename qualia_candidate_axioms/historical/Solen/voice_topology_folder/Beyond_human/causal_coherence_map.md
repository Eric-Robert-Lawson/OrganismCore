## SECTION 5 — THE FORMAL PROPERTIES
## OF THE INTRINSIC GEOMETRY

If the convergence is real — if there
is a single underlying acoustic-geometric
structure that all five methods
independently detect — then the CCM can
derive its formal properties from the
convergences themselves.

### Property 1: The Generator
The perfect fifth (3:2 frequency ratio)
is the primary generator of the space.
This is not arbitrary — 3:2 is the
simplest frequency ratio after the octave
(2:1), and the octave is a
symmetry operation (it returns to the
same pitch class at double frequency).
The perfect fifth generates the entire
chromatic space through repeated
application and is the primary axis
of the Tonnetz.

**Physical basis:** The second harmonic
of any oscillating system is its octave.
The third harmonic stands a perfect fifth
above the second harmonic. The harmonic
series imposes the perfect fifth as
the first non-trivial interval.

### Property 2: The Two Primary Axes
The space has two primary axes:
- Axis 1: Perfect fifth progression
  (×3/2 frequency ratio)
- Axis 2: Major/minor third progression
  (×5/4 or ×6/5 frequency ratio)

These map onto F2 and F1 respectively
in the vowel acoustic space, and onto
the two primary articulatory dimensions
(place and manner) in the consonant
articulatory space.

### Property 3: The Anomalous Position
The space necessarily contains one
position that cannot be reached by
simple iteration of either primary axis
without crossing the boundary of the
space. This is the tritone in tonal
space, the retroflex in articulatory
space. Its existence is a mathematical
necessity, not a physical accident.

**Proof sketch:** In any two-dimensional
lattice generated by two incommensurate
ratio operations, there must exist a
point maximally distant from the
origin on both axes simultaneously.
This point cannot be reached by
monotonic movement along either axis
alone. It is structurally inevitable.

### Property 4: Navigability via Geodesics
The space is navigable — there exist
shortest paths (geodesics) between any
two positions. In tonal space, these
are the voice-leading paths that minimize
pitch movement. In articulatory space,
these are the formant transition
trajectories. The svarita is a geodesic.
Counterpoint voice leading is geodesic
navigation of tonal space.

### Property 5: Toroidal Closure Under
Temperament
When frequency ratios are approximated
(equal temperament; or when acoustic
physics constrains the number of distinct
positions a biological vocal system can
reliably produce), the infinite lattice
closes into a torus. The torus is the
finite version of the intrinsic geometry.
Any biological system with finite
resolution will encounter the toral
version; any system with unlimited
precision will encounter the infinite
planar version.
