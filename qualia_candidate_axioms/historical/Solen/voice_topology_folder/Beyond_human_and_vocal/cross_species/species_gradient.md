The gradient was identified from first

> I don't think a guppy has the retention
> to even process this."*

This is precise. It identifies the
critical variable not as species
complexity in general but as the
specific combination of:

1. **Social function** — communication
   serving relationship maintenance,
   not just survival signaling

2. **Temporal depth** — the system's
   capacity to model past and future,

3. **Acoustic exploration** — how much
   of the physical acoustic eigenfunction

spectral geometry to be meaningfully
mapped. A system that uses sound only
for immediate functional signaling —
hardwired, not learned, not socially
referential — has not explored the
space. It has used a narrow corridor
within it.

The spectral geometry cannot be
found by examining a narrow corridor.
It requires navigation of the space
with sufficient coverage to reveal
its topology.

## SECTION 7 — FALSIFICATION CRITERIA

**The gradient claim is falsified if:**

A species meeting none of the four
gradient criteria (non-social, non-
learned, non-combinatorial, minimal
temporal depth) is found to have
acoustic communication with clear
eigenfunction structure.

This would show the gradient criterion
is wrong — that eigenfunction structure
appears regardless of the conditions
proposed.

**The substrate-independence claim
is falsified if:**

Elephant airborne and seismic formant
structures show no predictable
correspondence across physical media.

This would show that eigenfunction
structure is medium-specific, not
abstract-mathematical.

**The cross-substrate communication
prediction is falsified if:**

Spectrally structured playback signals
(designed from the acoustic
eigenfunction framework) produce no
differential behavioral response
compared to energy-matched random
signals in Tier 1 species.

This would show that eigenfunction
structure, even if present, does not
produce recognition across substrates.

Slobodchikoff's finding that prairie dog alarm calls encode predator species, color, size, shape, AND speed simultaneously in a single sub-second vocalization. Five-dimensional referential encoding in a compressed acoustic burst.

Prairie dogs don't have vocal learning or cultural transmission of their call repertoire (as far as current research shows). They'd fall in the gradient between Tier 2 and Tier 4 — high referential density without the accumulated navigation that vocal learning provides. But they represent an interesting edge case: if their calls DO show eigenfunction structure despite lacking vocal learning, that would pressure the gradient criterion in a specific and informative direction. It would suggest that natural selection alone — without cultural navigation — can find eigenfunction positions, at least in species with sufficient referential pressure.
