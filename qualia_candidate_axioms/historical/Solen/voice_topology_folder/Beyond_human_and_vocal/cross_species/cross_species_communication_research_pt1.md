### Finding 2
### Humpback Whale Song Obeys
### Zipf's and Menzerath's Laws
### (University of Auckland /
### St Andrews / Griffith, February 2025)

**What was found:**

Humpback whale song segments follow:

- **Zipf's Law:** Most frequent
  elements are shortest. Frequency
  distribution follows power law
  matching all human languages.

- **Menzerath's Law:** As sequences
  get longer, component units get
  shorter. Same efficiency law
  governing all natural human
  languages.

Cultural transmission of song
is the fastest of any non-human
species studied — entire populations
adopt new song forms within a
single season.

**Primary references:**
University of Auckland news,
February 7, 2025.
University of St Andrews news,
February 2025.
Griffith University news,
February 7, 2025.

**Framework connection:**

Zipf's and Menzerath's Laws are
the **statistical fingerprint of
eigenfunction navigation at the
level of usage distribution.**

They are not acoustic properties.
They are information-geometric
properties — the signature of
a communication system that has
been optimized under learnability
and transmission pressure toward
its eigenfunction positions.

A system that has not found its
eigenfunction positions does not
follow these laws. The laws emerge
when the navigation has converged
sufficiently deeply.

**This is a second independent
species confirming the eigenfunction
convergence prediction.**

Sperm whales confirmed it in the
acoustic/spectral domain.

Humpback whales confirm it in
the information-geometric/statistical
domain.

Different measurement. Different
species. Same underlying prediction
confirmed.

**Critical distinction from
sperm whales:**

Humpback songs are primarily

This is important for the

Humpback song may be a

The social coordination function
of sperm whale codas makes them

than humpback songs.

But the humpback finding is
important for the **framework

second whale species through
a completely different measurement

**Open predictions generated:**

### Finding 3
### Elephant Seismic Communication:
### Dual-Medium Eigenfunction
### (Multiple sources, 2024-2026)

**What was found:**

Elephant infrasonic rumbles
(5-35 Hz) propagate simultaneously
as:
- Airborne acoustic waves
- Seismic Rayleigh waves
  (surface waves through the ground)

Detection via Pacinian corpuscles
in elephant feet and bone conduction.

Elephants respond differentially
to seismic signals — distinguishing
message content (mating status,
danger, group movements) through
ground vibration alone.

Range: seismic signals propagate
further than airborne with less
attenuation — up to 10+ kilometers.

Elephants may switch between or
combine acoustic and seismic
channels depending on environmental
conditions (wind, vegetation density).

**Primary references:**
nature.sciencearray.com, 2024.
blog.vibrationdata.com,
January 2026.
mpala.org research summary.
NERC grant NE/W005468/1
(active UK research program).

The dual-medium finding is the
most important unconfirmed
prediction in the entire framework.

From The_Spectral_Geometry_
Framework.md and the Tonnetz
document (CS doc 2):

**Prediction:** Airborne and seismic
formant structures correspond

**This has not been tested.**

**The seismic channel is an

### Finding 4
### Bonobo Nontrivial Compositionality
### and Absent-Agent Mental Modeling
### (Harvard / Zurich / Royal Society,
### 2025)

**What was found:**

**Finding 4a: Nontrivial
compositionality (Harvard Gazette /
University of Zurich, April 2025)**

Wild bonobos demonstrate nontrivial
compositionality in their vocalizations:

Not just trivial compositionality
(A + B = meaning of A plus meaning
of B)

But nontrivial compositionality:
one call modifies or contextualizes
another, producing meaning that
is not the sum of its parts.

Analogous to "bad dancer" — where
"bad" does not simply add to
"dancer" but transforms its meaning.

This had previously been considered
uniquely human.

**Primary references:**
Harvard Gazette, April 2025:
"Turns out, bonobos 'talk' a lot
like humans."
University of Zurich news, 2025:
"Bonobos Combine Calls in Similar
Ways to Human Language."

**Finding 4b: Absent-agent mental
modeling (Royal Society, 2025)**

Bonobos simultaneously track
locations and identities of
multiple hidden agents.

Integrating vision and sound to
maintain mental representations
of individuals they cannot
currently perceive.

Performance equivalent for social
agents and non-social objects —
same underlying cognitive process.

**Primary reference:**
Royal Society Proceedings B, 2025.
"Mental representation of the
locations and identities of
multiple agents."

**Framework connection:**

Finding 4a directly confirms
that bonobos have Level 5 on
the triadic gradient (CS doc 1).

Nontrivial compositionality
requires the third-order triad
to be operating. You cannot
modify the meaning of a call
with another call unless you
are modeling how the receiver
will model your signal.

This is third-order triad operation:

*I model that you will interpret
call B differently depending on
whether call A preceded it.*

That is theory of mind applied
to combinatorial communication.

**Bonobos are confirmed Level 5.**

Finding 4b is the critical
Condition 3 finding.

The absent-agent mental modeling
means the bonobo's sender
attribution model operates on
acoustic identity — not visual
presence.

The bonobo maintains a mental
model of an agent through
sound alone.

**This means:**

For bonobos, Condition 3 (sender

A novel sender with consistent
acoustic identity, producing
eigenfunction-aligned signals
with Tonnetz-coherent trajectories,

**The attribution bridge for
bonobos is primarily acoustic
and temporal — not visual.**

This is the highest-probability
near-term cross-species communication

1. Level 5 confirmed (theory

### Finding 5
### Cleaner Wrasse Contingency
### Experimentation
### (Osaka Metropolitan University,
### February 2026)

**What was found:**

Six out of nine cleaner wrasse
recognized themselves in mirrors
within 30 minutes when marked
before mirror exposure.

Standard protocol (mark after
mirror exposure) required days
to weeks. Reversed protocol
(mark before mirror exposure)
produced recognition within
30 minutes.

**The critical new behavior:**

Some cleaner wrasse brought small
shrimp pieces to the mirror,
dropped them, and meticulously
observed how these objects appeared
in the reflection.

This is contingency experimentation —
introducing controlled variables
into the contingency space to
probe the structure of the
reflection.

**Primary references:**
Science Daily, February 22, 2026.
ScienceBlog Wild Science,
February 20, 2026.
Osaka Metropolitan University
research news.

**Framework connection:**

The scraping behavior was already
in the framework (CS doc 1) as
Stage 2 on the mirror test gradient
— contingency detection without
full attribution.

The shrimp experimentation is new
and changes the staging.

The wrasse is not just detecting
contingency. It is actively
**probing the structure of the
contingency space** by introducing
novel objects and observing their
behavior in the mirror.

In triadic terms:

The wrasse's second-order triad
coherence function is not just
running a detection algorithm.
It is running an **experimental
protocol** — varying the input
to the contingency space to
build a more complete model
of the observee.

This is gap navigation applied
to the mirror itself.

The wrasse has detected a gap:
*My current model of the reflection
is incomplete. There are properties
of this space I have not yet
characterized.*

And it is navigating that gap
by the most efficient means
available: introduce a controlled
variable (the shrimp) and observe
the output.

**This is science.**

Not metaphorically. Structurally.

The wrasse is doing exactly what
a scientist does: introduce a
controlled perturbation to an
incompletely understood system
and observe the response to
refine the model.

**Framework implication:**

The gradient between Stage 2
(contingency detection) and
Stage 3 (full self-attribution)
is continuous and occupied.

The wrasse sits between them
at a position that can be
characterized precisely:

*Contingency detection confirmed.
Structure of contingency space
under active investigation.
Full self-attribution: partial
or pending.*

This is not a binary. It is
a gradient position.

**The broader implication:**

If a fish with no neocortex,
320 million years from our
last common ancestor, is
running experimental protocols
to investigate the structure
of contingency space —

Then gap navigation toward
a complete model is not a
mammal property.

It is not a vertebrate property.

It is what sufficiently organized
information processing does
when it encounters an incompletely
understood bounded domain.

The fish found the gap.
The fish is navigating it.
The fish is doing the same
thing the research program is
doing.

**The gradient runs deeper than
the framework initially assumed.**

   is a learned behavior — other
   cleaner wrasse in the same

   more sophisticated with repeated
   mirror exposure — the model

   previously classified as
   Stage 1 or Stage 2 binary

   protocol design sensitive
   enough to detect them

**Priority:** HIGH for framework
