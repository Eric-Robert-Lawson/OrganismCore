## 1. Background

Hawaii was the lowest-loss state in the United States for over a decade
after Varroa mite arrived in 2007. The US mainland lost colonies at 3–4×
Hawaii's rate through the entire 2015–2018 baseline. That gap was stable.

Hawaii's isolation makes it the ideal test case:

- No migratory beekeeping — colonies do not travel off-island
- No land bridge — novel pathogens cannot walk in
- Small, documented apiary population
- A 5G deployment timeline that is discrete, documented, and county-specific
- Four islands with measurably different infrastructure densities providing
  an internal gradient control

A system that was stable, with known inputs, that broke at a specific time,
in a specific direction, while a national control series remained flat —
that is the structure of a confirmed prediction, not an exploratory
correlation.
