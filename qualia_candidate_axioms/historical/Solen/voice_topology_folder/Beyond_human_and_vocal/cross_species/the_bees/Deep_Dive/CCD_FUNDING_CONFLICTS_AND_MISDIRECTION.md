## PART I: THE FUNDING ARCHITECTURE

### Who Pays for CCD Research

The primary funders of Colony Collapse Disorder
research in the United States are:

**Federal Government:**
- USDA Agricultural Research Service (ARS)
- USDA National Institute of Food and Agriculture
  (NIFA, formerly CSREES)
- Environmental Protection Agency (EPA)
- National Science Foundation (NSF)

**Industry:**
- Bayer CropScience (neonicotinoid manufacturer)
- Syngenta (neonicotinoid manufacturer)
- Agricultural chemical trade associations

**Non-profit/Coalition:**
- Pollinator Partnership / North American Pollinator
  Protection Campaign (NAPPC)
- Various university endowments with industry ties

### What the USDA CCD Action Plan Actually Is

The USDA CCD Action Plan — the congressionally
mandated framework that structures federal CCD
research funding ��� organizes investigation around
four causal categories:

1. Parasites and pathogens (Varroa, Nosema,
   viral load)
2. Pesticides (neonicotinoids, fungicides,
   miticides)
3. Nutrition and habitat
4. Hive management and stress

**Electromagnetic infrastructure is not a
category.**

This is not an oversight. The funding categories
were set when the Action Plan was established,
based on the causal hypotheses that were active
in the scientific community at that time. Once
set, the categories determine what questions
get funded. Questions outside the categories
do not get funded. Researchers who cannot frame
their work within the categories cannot get grants.
Researchers who cannot get grants do not produce
results. Results that are never produced never
enter the consensus.

**The grant category architecture is the
first mechanism of exclusion.**

The RF infrastructure causal hypothesis was not
excluded by evidence. It was excluded by not
being in the grant category list.
