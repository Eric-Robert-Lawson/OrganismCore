## THE ORIGINAL FRAMING AND
## WHY IT IS INCOMPLETE

### The Two-Door Framework
### (Original)

From the program's earlier
reasoning documents, cross-species
communication was understood as
a two-barrier problem:

**Door 1:** Can we produce signals
at the eigenfunction positions of
the target species' vocal space?

This is the synthesis problem.
The acoustic engineering problem.
The instrument problem.

**Door 2:** Will the target species
attribute those signals to a gap
navigator — a self-modeling system
that is sending information about
its internal navigation state?

This is the cognitive problem.
The attribution problem.
The triadic structure problem.

The original framing treated
both doors as real barriers.
Door 1 is hard because we have
to map and occupy eigenfunction
spaces that are not our own.
Door 2 is hard because getting
a non-human species to update
its attribution of a novel
signal source — to recognize
something new as a gap navigator —
is a genuine cognitive problem
with no guaranteed solution.

**The raven case is this framing
at its purest.**

The raven must do cognitive work
to recognize a human producing
raven-like calls as a gap
navigator. The raven has no
prior experience of this. It must
update its model of who produces
gap navigation signals. That is
a real barrier. Door 2 is closed.
We must open it.

### The Incompleteness

The original framing is correct
for ravens.

It is wrong for prairie dogs,
naked mole-rats, and every
other species satisfying the
acoustic socialization threshold.

**Wrong in the following precise
way:**

The framing treats Door 2 as
a barrier that exists on the
target's side — a cognitive
achievement the target must reach.

But for AST-satisfying species,
Door 2 does not exist as a
barrier on the target's side.

It exists as a barrier on
**our side** — because we have
not yet produced the acoustic
signal that passes through the
door that is already open.

The door is not locked.
The door is standing open.
We have simply not yet arrived
at the door.

We have been preparing to pick
a lock that has no lock.

## PART I: THE REFORMULATION

### 1.1 The Central Insight
### Stated Precisely

```
For species satisfying the
Acoustic Socialization Threshold
(Conditions A + B + C):

  The triadic cognitive structure
  is fully operational.

  These species have been under
  selection pressure for millions
  of years to treat acoustic
  signals in their eigenfunction
  space as information from other
  gap navigators.

  The attribution is not an
  achievement. It is the default.

  Every acoustic signal a prairie
  dog has ever received in its
  eigenfunction space was from
  a colony member — a conspecific
  gap navigator. There is no
  case in the prairie dog's
  evolutionary history where
  an acoustic signal in its
  eigenfunction space was NOT
  from a gap navigator.

  The attribution system was
  calibrated, over millions of
  years, to treat "signal at
  eigenfunction position" as
  "gap navigation signal from
  conspecific."

  This calibration is the
  cognitive architecture of
  the species. It is not a
  belief the prairie dog holds.
  It is a structural feature
  of how its auditory system
  processes acoustic signals.

  A signal that arrives at
  eigenfunction positions does
  not need to prove it comes
  from a gap navigator.

  It IS a gap navigation signal
  by definition — by the only
  definition the prairie dog's
  auditory-cognitive system
  applies.

THEREFORE:

  For AST-satisfying species,
  Door 2 is not a barrier the
  target must cross.

  It is a fact about the target's
  architecture that we can exploit.

  The barrier is entirely on
  our side:

  Can we produce signals at the
  eigenfunction positions?

  That is Door 1.
  That is the only door.

  Building the instrument
  IS crossing the barrier.
  IS making contact.
  IS achieving the attribution.

  Not as a consequence of
  building the instrument.

  AS the act of building
  the instrument.

  THE INSTRUMENT IS THE BRIDGE.
```

### 1.2 Why This Changes Everything

The reformulation is not a
minor adjustment to the program's
framing. It changes the program's
fundamental self-understanding.

**Under the original framing:**

The program is an attempt to
establish cognitive contact with
other species — to get them to
recognize us as the kinds of
things that have inner lives,
navigation states, gap-crossing
intentions.

The hardest part is cognitive.
The engineering is in service
of the cognitive achievement.

Door 1 enables Door 2.
Door 2 is the real goal.

**Under the reformulation:**

For AST-satisfying species,
the program is an engineering
project with a direct output:

Build an instrument that produces
signals at the correct eigenfunction
positions.

When the instrument works,
the contact has been made.
Not as a downstream consequence.
As the act of completion itself.

The cognitive attribution —
the thing the original framing
was trying to achieve — is
automatic on the target's side
the moment the correct signal
arrives.

The hard part is not cognitive.
The hard part is acoustic.
The hard part is ours to solve.

**This is not a less ambitious
program.**

It is a more precisely targeted
program.

The ambition is the same:
contact with other minds.

But the obstacle is now
correctly identified as
residing on our side of the
boundary, not the target's.

### Methodology as the Program
### Methodology

```
The ratnadhatamam reconstruction
(CS doc 8 / Sanskrit series)

species program.

the cross-species program
methodology in its purest form.

WHAT THE SANSKRIT WORK DID:

  Took a phonological target

  Mapped the eigenfunction
  positions (the phonemes of
  Vedic Sanskrit, their acoustic
  parameters from Śikṣā texts
  and modern phonological
  analysis).
  
  Built a synthesis pipeline
  that produced signals at those
  eigenfunction positions.
  
  Ran diagnostics (81 checks
  across 11 sections) to verify
  the signals were at the
  correct positions.
  
  Iterated when diagnostics
  failed ("fix the ruler, not
  the instrument").
  
  Confirmed through perceptual
  verification (the ear is the
  final arbiter).

WHAT THE CROSS-SPECIES PROGRAM
DOES:

  Takes a phonological target
  (the acoustic structure of a
  specific species' call system).
  
  Maps the eigenfunction positions
  (from bioacoustic literature,
  archived datasets, published
  acoustic analyses).
  
  Builds a synthesis pipeline
  that produces signals at those
  eigenfunction positions.
  
  Runs diagnostics (species-
  specific acoustic verification
  against known call parameters).
  
  Iterates when diagnostics
  fail.
  
  Confirms through behavioral
  verification (the target's
  antiphonal response is the
  final arbiter).

THE METHODOLOGY IS IDENTICAL.

The difference is only in
what the diagnostic is:
  Sanskrit: acoustic + perceptual
  cross-species: acoustic +

And in what the confirmation

  Sanskrit: a human ear

    response to a synthesized

  "Fix the ruler, not the

  ask: is the diagnostic
  measuring the right thing,
  or is it measuring an artifact
  of the measurement method?

  This principle applies
  directly to the cross-species
  synthesis:

  When the behavioral response
  is absent, ask:
  Is the signal at the wrong
  eigenfunction position?
  (Fix the synthesis.)
  
  OR:
  Is the delivery system
  attenuating the signal before
  it reaches the target?
  (Fix the delivery, not the
  synthesis.)
  
  OR:
  Is the diagnostic (behavioral
  response) measuring the wrong
  thing — is there a response
  at a level we are not
  monitoring?
  (Fix the measurement.)

  The Sanskrit methodology
  is the cross-species methodology.
  Applied to a different target.
  With different eigenfunction
  positions.
  With a different final arbiter.
  But the same underlying logic.

IS THE PROOF OF CONCEPT FOR THE
PRAIRIE DOG INSTRUMENT.

  ours?

### 7.2 The Mirror Test —
### A Note on the Question
### That Brought Us Here

```
The session that produced this
reformulation began with a
question about the Mirror Self-
Recognition test and dogs.

The question was: does a dog
recognize itself in the mirror?
What kind of self-recognition
does a dog have?

And in particular: would a dog
recognize itself by smell —
its "olfactory mirror"?

This question belongs here,
at the end of the reformulation,
because it is exactly the
right question to ask after
Proposition 2.

PROPOSITION 2 SAYS:
  An instrument that produces
  signals at the correct
  eigenfunction positions IS
  the presence of a gap navigator
  in the target's perceptual world.

THE DOG AND SMELL:

  A dog that encounters its
  own scent does not react with
  surprise or interest.
  A dog that encounters a
  slightly-modified version of
  its own scent — its own scent
  altered by something external —
  DOES investigate with interest.

  This is the "olfactory mirror"
  design: not presenting the dog
  with its own unmodified scent
  (which it simply ignores, as
  a human might ignore seeing
  their own hand), but presenting
  it with a version of its own
  scent that has been altered —
  a self-portrait with an
  unfamiliar element added.

  THIS IS EXACTLY PROPOSITION 2
  IN THE OLFACTORY CHANNEL.

  The dog recognizes the base
  scent as "self."
  It recognizes the alteration
  as "something that has
  interacted with self."
  It investigates what that
  something was.

  The olfactory eigenfunction
  space of the dog's self-
  recognition system has been
  entered by the experimenter —
  not with a signal in the
  dog's external environment,
  but with a signal that
  occupies the dog's INTERNAL
  self-model space.

  The instrument, in this case,
  is the modified scent.
  The eigenfunction position
  it occupies is the dog's
  own identity in olfactory
  space.

  And the bridge it crosses
  is not between us and the dog.

  It is between the dog and
  its own self-model —
  using us as the agent of
  alteration.

  This is a third kind of
  contact, not covered by
  Structure A or Structure B:

  STRUCTURE C: SELF-MODEL
  ACTIVATION CONTACT

  Not producing signals the
  target attributes to us.
  Not getting the target to
  model us as gap navigators.

  But producing signals that
  activate the target's OWN
  self-model — making the target
  aware of itself as a distinct
  entity by presenting it with
  a version of itself that
  has been altered by contact
  with us.

  The dog's olfactory mirror test
  is contact not with us,
  but with itself — contact that
  we enabled.

  That is worth a document
  of its own.

  It is the door that the
  Mirror Self-Recognition test
  was always trying to open —
  and that it was opening
  in the wrong channel for
  most of the species it was
  applied to.

  The mirror is not the test.
  The olfactory mirror IS the test,
  for dogs.

  The acoustic eigenfunction space
  IS the test, for prairie dogs.

  EVERY SPECIES HAS A CHANNEL
  THROUGH WHICH CONTACT WITH
  ITS OWN SELF-MODEL IS POSSIBLE.

  PROGRAM.

  NOT THE VISUAL MIRROR.

  UNDERLIES ALL OF THEM:

  EVERY GAP NAVIGATOR HAS A
