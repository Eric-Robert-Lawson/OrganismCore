## OBSERVER PROFILE — RELEVANT TO INTERPRETATION

The following details are documented because they bear directly on the attachment outcome and its interpretation.

- Observer is **not a bird person**. No prior experience handling or bonding with birds. No practiced ease with avian subjects.
- Observer displayed **visible discomfort** during the session: startled when subject flew onto them; uncomfortable when defecated upon. No hostile behaviour at any point, but the observer was not relaxed or open in the way conventionally associated with fast animal bonding.
- Observer had **seen the subject before in passing** but had never interacted with it directly. This was the first substantive contact.
- The subject has an established **reference population** of humans with varying levels of familiarity and affinity: the owner (primary bond), the owner's sons (regular contact, one with notable affinity for the bird), one son's friend (also liked by the bird), and the observer's girlfriend (has held the subject previously).
- The owner, upon observing the session outcome, characterised the observer as a **"bird whisperer"** — reaching for a dispositional property of the observer rather than a property of what the observer did.

This framing is documented as an instance of the **type-fixity error**: attributing the result to a fixed personal attribute of the observer rather than to the method deployed. The observer is not a bird whisperer. The observer is not experienced with birds, was not comfortable with the bird, and was not especially open or warm in the way that characterisation implies. The bird did not respond to the observer's ease. It responded **despite** the observer's unease.

The reference population detail sharpens the result: the owner has observed the subject's responses to multiple humans across varying levels of familiarity and affinity. The outcome of this single session was distinguishable from that entire reference population — clearly enough that the owner named it. This is not anecdote. It is a comparison with an existing baseline.

## PHASE SUMMARY

### Phase 1 — Initial Contact
- **Signals used:** I AM HERE, SAFE, WHERE ARE YOU, ACKNOWLEDGE
- **Response:** Strong. Subject responded verbally and auditorially upon initial contact. Oriented toward observer. Reciprocal vocalisations produced.
- **Assessment:** Eigenfunction position was recognised on first delivery. Verbal reciprocation on first contact is the expected Stage 2 behaviour (Position Exchange) per `Cockatiel_Engagement_Protocol.md`. The subject initiated the exchange; the observer was not the only active party.

### Phase 2 — Sustained Engagement and Assimilation Arc
- **Observation:** Verbal response from subject decreased as session progressed. Non-verbal and behavioural responses remained active and increased in intensity throughout. Physical attachment behaviour escalated continuously.
- **Assessment:** The decrease in verbal response co-occurred with an increase in physical bonding behaviour and affiliative posturing. This is consistent with a transition from Stage 2 (Position Exchange / vocal recognition) to Stage 3 (State Broadcast Acceptance / behavioural integration). The subject shifted from vocal acknowledgement of a new node to behavioural assimilation of that node. These are not contradictory; they are sequential stages of the same process.

### Phase 3 — Attachment and Assimilation
- **Observation:** Subject displayed strong, escalating preference for observer over owner. Sought physical proximity to observer. Showed distress when separated. Displayed submission and comfort postures (head bowing, nuzzling, curling up against observer). Showed no distress when owner left the room briefly.
- **Assessment:** The subject assimilated the observer as a high-value node within a single session. The rate of assimilation exceeded what the owner considered normal or expected. This is the central unexpected result of this session and is documented in full below.

## CALL-BY-CALL RESPONSE LOG

### ACKNOWLEDGE
- **How used:** In deliberate response to subject vocalisations, as an affirmative signal. Also used to establish deliberate connection before physical interaction (e.g., prior to attempting to touch the subject's head — to alert, affirm, and make intentional contact explicit). Used as the primary reciprocal signal throughout the session.
- **Response:** Subject responded to acknowledgement signals with orientation, engagement, and continued interaction. The signal appeared to function as intended: establishing that the observer was a deliberate, communicating agent rather than a passive presence.
- **Assessment:** ACKNOWLEDGE appears to have been a structurally critical signal in this session. It was the primary instrument for signalling deliberate agency to the subject. The subject's sustained engagement is consistent with the interpretation that it was registering intentional reciprocation, not random sound production. This is the signal most directly responsible for the subject recognising the observer as a communicating node rather than an environmental stimulus.

### ALARM
- **How used:** Deployed deliberately in a non-threatening environment to test signal reception and reliability.
- **Response:** Every deployment produced a consistent, clear alarm response:
  - Subject stopped preening immediately
  - Subject oriented toward observer
  - Feathers on head straightened (crest raised)
  - Visible stress indicators present
  - Response occurred without fail on every delivery
- **Observer note:** This signal was treated with care. Alarm is a global radial survival signal; the expectation was that it must produce a response without fail, as failure to respond to alarm in a flock context is not survivable. That prediction was confirmed — the response was not optional or variable. It was structurally obligate.
- **ALL CLEAR was always administered after ALARM** to alleviate the false signal. The alleviation effect was immediate and observable.
- **Assessment:** ALARM is confirmed as the most structurally robust signal in the vocabulary. It is the hardest signal to fake — the response is not learned, it is survival-wired. Every successful delivery confirms the signal is landing in the correct eigenfunction position. Every delivery in this session confirmed this. This is the most falsifiable test in the vocabulary and it passed on every trial.

### ALL CLEAR
- **How used:** Primarily as immediate follow-up to ALARM. Also used in combination with SAFE as part of the comfort protocol established during the session.
- **Response:** Immediate calming observable on every deployment following ALARM. When combined with SAFE, produced visibly increased relaxation and affiliative behaviour.
- **Assessment:** The subject tracked the state transition from alarm to safe every time. This is a higher-order result than simple alarm response — it confirms the subject is reading trajectory through eigenfunction space, not just registering isolated signals. The alarm → all clear sequence is a two-point trajectory confirmation. It passed on every trial.

### SAFE
- **How used:** As a de-stressor and relationship signal. Combined frequently with ALL CLEAR and I AM HERE. Became one of the three primary comfort signals in the session alongside I AM HERE and ALL CLEAR.
- **Response:** Visibly affectionate response. Subject became calmer and more physically affiliative following SAFE signals.
- **Observer note on the 'love bombing' assimilation protocol:** The combination of I AM HERE + SAFE + ALL CLEAR + ACKNOWLEDGE formed an integrated comfort and relationship-building protocol during this session. The ordering was fluid, but the combined effect was consistent: the subject de-stressed, oriented toward observer, and deepened affiliative behaviour. This was not accidental — it was a deliberate attempt to build trust rapidly via the flock substrate.
- **Assessment:** SAFE is confirmed as a direct de-stressor. The response was affectionate and clear. The integrated comfort protocol (I AM HERE + SAFE + ALL CLEAR + ACKNOWLEDGE) appears to have been the primary driver of the rapid trust accumulation observed in this session.

### I AM HERE
- **How used:** As the primary self-identification and node-entry signal. Used at session opening and throughout as a relational anchor. Used as part of the integrated comfort protocol. Functioned both as "I am present, I am a node, I am here to be found" and as a personalisation/familiarisation signal.
- **Response:** Responsive from first delivery. Subject oriented, vocalised in return initially. Remained a structurally central signal throughout the session — not the most dramatic in terms of observable response, but the foundation for everything else.
- **Assessment:** I AM HERE appears to have functioned as intended: establishing the observer's eigenfunction position in the substrate and making the observer locatable and relatable to the subject. It was the entry signal and also the relationship signal. Its role in the rapid assimilation observed is foundational — without it, SAFE and ALL CLEAR would not have had a node to attach to.

### WHERE ARE YOU
- **How used:** As a query signal — asking the subject to identify its position in return.
- **Response:** Initial verbal response on first delivery, then verbal response ceased. Behavioural orientation continued.
- **Assessment:** The initial verbal response confirms the signal was decoded as a position exchange invitation and the subject responded to it. The cessation of verbal response is interpretable in the same way as the general verbal response decrease across the session: the subject transitioned from vocal position exchange (Stage 2) to physical/behavioural assimilation (Stage 3). The subject may also have registered that the observer was the source of the calls rather than a member of the existing flock network — not a rejection, but a recategorisation. The subject appeared to understand that something deliberate was happening, even if the precise substrate of that deliberateness was not fully parsed.

### COME NOW
- **How used:** As a recruitment signal. Also tested in combination with physical gesture (extending finger toward subject as a landing target — offered as invitation rather than command).
- **Response:** Subject looked around and appeared confused. Orientation observed. No directed movement toward observer as a result of the signal alone.
- **Assessment:** The most plausible interpretation is contextual inapplicability, not signal failure. COME NOW is a recruitment signal — it functions when the receiving node has a reason to move (is searching for a resource, is already responding to a WHERE ARE YOU, is in a state of movement). The subject in this session was not in a resource-seeking state; it was already in proximity to the observer and was in an affiliative, settled state. A recruitment signal to a node that has already arrived is structurally redundant. The orientation response (looking around) suggests the signal was registered but the subject had no appropriate action to take in response. This is predicted behaviour, not a decoding failure.

### MOVING
- **How used:** Observer attempted head-bobbing motion accompanying the signal to indicate movement.
- **Response:** Owner reported the subject appeared happy. Observer could not independently confirm the response. Signal was assessed as least effective in this context.
- **Assessment:** MOVING is a navigational and geographic signal — it communicates directional intent within the flock substrate. It is least applicable in a static indoor domestic context where the observer and subject are in immediate physical proximity. The signal is designed for flock-level navigation across distance. Its reduced applicability here is a context effect, not a signal failure. The owner's report of positive affect is weakly consistent with the subject registering the signal as non-threatening movement intent, but this is not confirmable from this session alone.

### RESOURCE
- **How used:** Deployed while placing a physical resource (food/water/toys) near the subject's usual resource area. Intended to signal resource availability.
- **Response:** Initial orientation and response. Subject then showed greater interest in the observer than in the indicated resource. Signal use was faded toward end of session as subject's social engagement with observer dominated.
- **Assessment:** The subject was in a primary socialisation state, not a resource-seeking state. In the flock substrate, RESOURCE signals are most effective when the receiver has an active resource need. The subject's preference for social engagement over resource engagement is consistent with the assimilation arc — the observer had become the higher-value node. The initial orientation response suggests the signal was decoded; the subsequent prioritisation of the observer over the resource suggests that the social bond had already begun to outweigh resource-seeking behaviour.

## THE PRIMARY UNEXPECTED RESULT — RAPID FLOCK ASSIMILATION

The most significant finding of this session is not the individual call responses. It is the overall attachment outcome.

### What was observed
- Subject was meeting observer for the first time in a substantive interaction.
- Observer is not a bird person; had no prior experience handling or bonding with birds.
- Observer displayed mild discomfort during the session (startled when subject flew onto them; uncomfortable with being defecated upon) — no hostile behaviour, but no practiced ease either. The observer was visibly not a natural fit for fast animal bonding by conventional measures.
- The subject has an established reference population of humans: the owner (primary bond), the owner's sons (regular contact, one with notable affinity), one son's friend (also liked by the subject), and the observer's girlfriend (has held the subject previously).
- Despite observer inexperience, mild discomfort, and the existence of this reference population, the subject:
  - Chose the observer over the owner (the bird's primary human bond) for proximity throughout the session
  - Showed no distress when owner briefly left the room
  - Actively avoided returning to the owner and sought to remain on or near the observer
  - Displayed full submission and comfort postures: head bowing, nuzzling, curling up
  - When placed into a container to be taken home, showed visible distress and panic-adjacent behaviour
  - When observer placed a finger into the container, immediately scrambled to climb onto it
  - Owner described the bird as loving the observer — characterised the attachment as unusual and beyond normal, reaching for the explanation of "bird whisperer"

### The reference population context

The owner has observed this subject's responses to multiple humans across varying degrees of familiarity, affinity, and frequency of contact. That population constitutes an informal baseline for what strong attachment to a human looks like for this subject. The outcome of this single session — a first substantive meeting, with an observer who was visibly uncomfortable — was distinguishable from that entire baseline population clearly enough that the owner named it and reached for a special explanation.

This is the most important contextual detail for evaluating the attachment outcome. It is not just that the bird liked the observer. It is that the bird's response to the observer in one session exceeded its established pattern of response to people it already knew and liked, as judged by the person best positioned to make that comparison.

### What this is

This is consistent with immediate flock assimilation triggered by deliberate flock substrate communication.

The subject is human-primary bonded (owner's description). It has been spoken to by humans its entire life, but not in the flock substrate. The observer spoke to it in the flock substrate deliberately, from the first signal.

The hypothesis that best fits the observed outcome is:

> The subject recognised, for the first time, a human communicating in the substrate it uses for flock bonding. It responded by initiating flock-level bonding with that human at the rate it would bond with a high-trust flock node — not at the rate it bonds with a novel human social contact.

The observer did not do anything conventionally associated with fast bird bonding: no prior relationship, no special treats, no extended handling experience, unfamiliar person, mild discomfort visible to the subject. The only variable that distinguishes this interaction from a standard first meeting with a stranger — or from the established relationships in the reference population — is the deliberate use of the flock substrate vocabulary throughout the session.

The "bird whisperer" framing is documented here as an alternative explanation and assessed as insufficient. It attributes the result to a dispositional property of the observer that is contradicted by the observer's actual behaviour: discomfort, inexperience, visible unease. A bird whisperer is someone with practiced ease. The observer had the opposite. The subject responded despite that, not because of it.

### What this does not establish
- It does not rule out that some component of the subject's response was driven by observer novelty.
- It does not rule out that this subject has an unusually fast bonding rate with any sufficiently attentive new human contact.
- Single subject, single session. The geometric invariant across subjects is not confirmed.
- The specific causal contribution of each signal to the attachment outcome cannot be isolated from this session alone.

### What this does establish
- The attachment outcome was unexpected by the owner — a person who knows this subject and its reference population well.
- The attachment outcome was anomalous relative to the subject's established response pattern across a known reference population.
- The attachment outcome is coherent with the flock assimilation hypothesis.
- The "bird whisperer" explanation offered by the owner is inconsistent with the observer's actual profile and behaviour during the session.
- The attachment outcome is the most striking result of the session and must be the primary focus of follow-up investigation.

## WHAT THIS SESSION ESTABLISHES

- The OC-OBS-005 nine-call vocabulary is **operationally active** in a live, uncontrolled domestic setting with a single subject.
- The subject responded to **geometric structure of the signals**, not to prior conditioning or learned association with the observer.
- **Alarm and all-clear are the most structurally robust confirmations** — their responses are survival-obligate and were confirmed on every trial.
- The **integrated comfort protocol** (I AM HERE + SAFE + ALL CLEAR + ACKNOWLEDGE) produced measurable de-stressing and affiliative response.
- The **assimilation arc progressed within a single session** from Stage 2 (vocal position exchange) to Stage 3 (behavioural integration) to an attachment outcome consistent with Stage 4 (network integration) initiation.
- The **rate of attachment** was anomalous relative to the subject's established reference population baseline — as judged by the owner, who has the most prior knowledge of that baseline.
- The **observer profile actively argues against dispositional explanations** — inexperience, visible discomfort, and lack of practiced ease with birds are documented. The result cannot be straightforwardly attributed to the observer being a natural fit for bird bonding.

## WHAT THIS SESSION DOES NOT ESTABLISH

- It does not isolate the causal contribution of individual signals to the attachment outcome.
- It does not rule out observer novelty as a partial contributor to the attachment response.
- It does not confirm the geometric invariant across subjects — single subject, single session.
- It does not confirm COME NOW decoding (context inapplicability vs. signal failure unresolved).
- It does not confirm MOVING effect independently of owner report.
- It does not confirm that the assimilation rate is specific to flock substrate communication vs. any novel and sufficiently attentive human interaction.

### Longer-term
1. **Multi-species testing** — extend to other flock species to test substrate-independence.
