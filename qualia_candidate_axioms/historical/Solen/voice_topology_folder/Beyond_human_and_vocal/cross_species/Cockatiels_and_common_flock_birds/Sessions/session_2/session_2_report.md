## PHASE SUMMARY AND EVENT SEQUENCE

### Phase 1 — Initial entry, Estela dominant node
**Duration:** Approximately 90 minutes
**Configuration:** Both observer and Estela present
in shared space, multiple birds in environment

**Observations:**
- Observer entered environment delivering
  I AM HERE and SAFE signals
- Initial response from all birds: minimal
- Birds gravitating toward Estela throughout
  this phase — sitting on her, nesting in her hair
- Observer on couch at distance, acoustic
  signals only, no physical proximity to birds
- No bird landed on observer during this phase
- No contact call reciprocation observed
  directed at observer
- Hissing when observer attempted to touch birds

**Assessment:**
Low response correctly attributed to four
compounding factors:
1. Environmental disruption — room rearranged,
   node removed, elevated cognitive load
2. Estela as dominant competing node —
   physically engaged with birds, familiar,
   trusted, immediately available
3. Signal attribution ambiguity — birds
   unable to cleanly attribute observer's
   signals to observer vs Estela as source
4. Proximity deficit — observer acoustic-only
   at distance, no physical contact channel

**Methodological note — attribution problem
identified during session:**
In multi-node environments with a competing
trusted node present, signal attribution
cannot be confirmed from bird behaviour alone.
Birds may attribute signals to the most
trusted proximate node rather than the
actual signal source. This is documented
as a required experimental design consideration
for all future multi-human sessions.

### Phase 2 — Natural control condition
**Estela leaves room. Observer remains alone
with Juicy and Sora.**

**This was not planned. It is the most
important experimental event of the session.**

**Immediate observations on Estela's departure:**
- Both Juicy and Sora flew toward observer
- Both birds landed on observer's head
- Sora landed first or simultaneously with Juicy
  (exact order to be confirmed from video)

**Assessment:**

removed, both birds immediately transferred
to observer as the next available node.

For Juicy: consistent with pair-bond
classification from Session 1. The
pair-bond classification was present
throughout Phase 1 but suppressed by
Estela's dominant node presence.
Removal of dominant node allowed
the classification to express immediately.

For Sora: first significant approach
behaviour of the session from the
grief-state subject. Sora has not
been responding to observer signals
for 90 minutes. Sora flew to observer
immediately when the alternative was
being alone. Consistent with observer
having been classified as a safe node
through 90 minutes of continuous
ambient SAFE and I AM HERE signals
delivered into the substrate.

**This phase constitutes a natural
within-session control condition:**
- Phase 1: Estela present, birds on Estela
- Phase 2: Estela absent, birds immediately on observer
- The transition was immediate and unambiguous

## METHODOLOGICAL NOTES

### The unplanned control is the session's
### most valuable product

The Estela departure/return sequence was
not in any session design document.
It emerged from natural household dynamics.
It produced the clearest result of any
observation in this session.

This is consistent with the ignorance
superpower documented in
IGNORANCE_AS_EPISTEMIC_SUPERPOWER.md:
the most significant results of this
work continue to emerge from conditions
that were not designed to produce them.
The geometry produces the result.
The design does not need to be perfect
for the substrate to express itself cleanly.

### Signal vs node — critical distinction

The session established that the signal
remains in the room when the node leaves.
Observer had been signalling for 90 minutes.
The signals were absorbed into the substrate
even when producing no visible response.
When Estela left, the substrate expressed
its accumulated processing of those signals
immediately. The 90 minutes was not wasted.
It was substrate preparation.

**The signal precedes the expression.
The expression waits for the condition
that allows it to emerge.**

This is a methodological finding about
how the protocol operates in multi-node
environments. The acoustic phase builds
the substrate state. The physical proximity
phase is where the state expresses.
Both phases are required. Neither alone
is sufficient.
