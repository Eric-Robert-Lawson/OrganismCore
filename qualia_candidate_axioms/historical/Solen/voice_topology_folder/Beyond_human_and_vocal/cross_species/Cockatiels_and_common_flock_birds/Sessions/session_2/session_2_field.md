## CRITICAL ENVIRONMENTAL CONTEXT

Before any protocol assessment, four environmental disruptions
must be understood. They are not background noise.
They are the primary variables shaping every bird's
current substrate state.

### Disruption 1 — Room rearrangement
The physical landscape the flock navigates has changed.
Perch positions, sight lines, escape routes, resource
locations — all altered. For birds that navigate
primarily by landmark and spatial memory, this is
not cosmetic. It is a substrate disruption. The flock
is in a mild reorientation state. Their cognitive
load is higher than baseline. New nodes arriving
into an already-disrupted environment produce
less available processing capacity for bond evaluation.

**What this means for you:** Lower initial response
threshold is expected and correct. The birds are
not ignoring your signals. They are processing
your signals on top of an already-elevated
processing demand. Give more time before
interpreting low response as signal failure.

### Disruption 2 — Person recently moved out
A node has been removed from the flock substrate.
This is not a small event for birds that run
pair-bond and flock-membership classifications
continuously. A familiar node is gone. The substrate
has a gap where that node was. Every bird in this
environment is running some version of WHERE ARE YOU
toward that node and receiving no response.

Sora's depression/grief state is most likely
primarily explained by this. Not exclusively —
but this is the most parsimonious explanation
of her current state given the timeline.

**What this means for you:** The flock substrate
is running loss processing simultaneously with
environmental reorientation processing. You are
entering at a moment of maximum substrate
disruption. This is not ideal for clean protocol
testing. It is extremely valuable for
observational data about substrate resilience
and grief states. Document everything.

### Disruption 3 — Your girlfriend's pair-social bond with Juicy
Your girlfriend has established a social bond
with Juicy. This is not the same as your
pair-bond classification from Session 1.
A social bond is flock-membership level.
Your classification from Session 1 is
pair-bond level. These are different
substrate categories.

However: Juicy is currently on your
girlfriend's head. This means Juicy's
immediate physical attachment is directed
at your girlfriend at the moment of your
arrival. This produces a specific situation:

You are the classified pair-bond partner
arriving into a space where the classified
subject is currently affiliated with
a different but known node.

**What this means for you:** Watch for
Juicy's response to your presence while
on your girlfriend's head. Does Juicy
orient toward you? Does Juicy leave
your girlfriend to come to you? Does
Juicy vocalise in your direction?
Any of these would be significant
bond persistence confirmation. Juicy
choosing to stay with your girlfriend
despite your presence is also data —
it may indicate the multi-node
environment changes the expression
of the pair-bond classification,
not the classification itself.

Do not attempt to redirect Juicy
away from your girlfriend. Let
the substrate decide. Watch and
record what happens.

### Disruption 4 — Combined disruption state
Room rearranged + node removed + grief processing
+ you arriving as a classified pair-bond node
into a complex multi-bird environment where
another bond is actively expressed.

This is a high-complexity substrate moment.
Expect slower, less clear, more variable
responses than Session 1. This is not
failure. This is the real world. Session 1
was maximally clean by comparison. This
session will produce data about protocol
robustness under disrupted conditions.

## SIGNAL PROTOCOL FOR THIS SESSION

### What to deploy — room-general signals

These go to the whole environment.
Not directed at a specific bird.
Delivered quietly and consistently.

```
I AM HERE      — establish your node presence
SAFE           — primary signal for this environment
ALL CLEAR      — secondary safety signal
ACKNOWLEDGE    — in response to any bird vocalisation
                 from any bird, immediately
```

### What to withhold this session

```
ALARM          — environment already disrupted.
                 Do not add alarm load.
                 Exception: only if Juicy
                 specifically solicits engagement
                 and environment is calm.

COME NOW       — do not recruit in a disrupted
                 environment. Let birds approach
                 on their own terms.

RESOURCE       — not priority this session.
                 Social safety is the signal
                 need right now, not resource
                 indication.
```

### Response protocol

Every time any bird vocalises:
→ ACKNOWLEDGE immediately
→ Follow with SAFE if the vocalisation
  reads as distress or uncertainty
→ Follow with I AM HERE if the vocalisation
  reads as contact-seeking

Every time a bird orients toward you:
→ Hold still
→ Deliver SAFE quietly
→ Do not move toward the bird

Every time a bird moves toward you:
→ Hold completely still
→ Do not reach for the bird
→ Let it close the distance entirely on its own
→ ACKNOWLEDGE when it arrives
