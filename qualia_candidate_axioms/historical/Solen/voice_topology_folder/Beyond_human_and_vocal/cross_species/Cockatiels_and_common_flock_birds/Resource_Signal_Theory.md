## THE STANDARD FEEDING MECHANISM

```
HOW A BIRD NORMALLY FINDS FOOD:

  Step 1: Visual detection.
    Bird notices something in the
    environment that may be food.
    This requires proximity —
    the bird must already be
    within visual range.

  Step 2: Neophobia assessment.
    Is this safe to approach?
    Is there a threat associated
    with this novel object?
    New = potentially dangerous.
    The bird delays.

    Neophobia delay in bold species
    (chickadee, house sparrow):
    minutes to hours on first encounter.
    In cautious species (most finches):
    hours to days.

  Step 3: Social learning bypass.
    If another bird approaches and
    eats without consequence,
    the observing bird's delay
    is shortened dramatically.
    The observer is outsourcing
    the neophobia assessment.

  Step 4: Habituation over sessions.
    Over 2-4 weeks of consistent
    resource presence, the neophobia
    extinguishes.
    The resource is categorised as
    known, safe, reliable.
    Approach becomes immediate.

  WHAT THE HUMAN IS IN THIS MODEL:
    A vending machine.
    The birds are using the human.
    The human provides resource
    unconditionally.
    The birds' relationship is
    to the FOOD, not to the node.
    Remove the food and the
    relationship collapses.
    Remove the human and nothing
    changes — the food is still there.

  TIMELINE: 2-4 weeks to reliable approach.
  MECHANISM: Neophobia extinction.
  NODE RELATIONSHIP: None.
  SCALABILITY: Location-specific only.
```
