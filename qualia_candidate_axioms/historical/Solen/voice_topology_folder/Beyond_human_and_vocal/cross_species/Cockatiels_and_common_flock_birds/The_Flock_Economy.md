### The Honeyguide Economy
**Spottiswoode, Begg & Begg 2016 — Science**

```
STRUCTURE:
  Two agents.
  Each holds something the other
  cannot efficiently acquire alone.
  Signal protocol maintained by both.
  Both parties enforce accuracy.
  Both parties benefit.

HONEYGUIDE PROVIDES:
  Location of bee nest.
  The human cannot find it efficiently.
  The honeyguide cannot open it alone.

HUMAN PROVIDES:
  Labour to open the nest.
  Access to wax and larvae
  the bird cannot reach independently.

CURRENCY: Signal accuracy.
  Honeyguide that leads to empty nests:
  not recruited. Goes out of business.
  Human that does not open nest
  after being led: stops getting guides.
  Both parties enforce the economy.

WHAT MAKES IT AN ECONOMY AND NOT
CONDITIONING:
  Conditioning: one party provides
  stimulus, other party provides
  response. One directional.
  Economy: both parties provide
  services. Both enforce accuracy.
  Both benefit. Bidirectional.
  Self-regulating.

WHAT IT REQUIRED:
  Thousands of years of coevolution
  in a specific geographic location
  with a specific human culture.
  Not derivable from first principles.
  Not transferable to other contexts
  without that history.

  this exchange is ongoing.

NOTE: The crow is running the economy
  proactively. It is paying for

  tokens that signal reliability.
  The human is not running an

  The crow is.

  The other is not.

THE PIGEON FEEDER NON-ECONOMY

STRUCTURE: NOT an economy.
  Human provides food unconditionally.
  No signal accuracy requirement.
  No service exchange.
  Pigeon gives nothing in return
  except presence.
  Vending machine relationship.

WHY IT IS NOT AN ECONOMY:
  The pigeon does not need to do
  anything to receive the resource
  except be present.
  The human does not need to do
  anything to receive integration
  because integration is not
  what they are seeking.
  There is no mutual enforcement
  of accuracy.
  There is no exchange.

WHAT IT PRODUCED ANYWAY:
  Partial integration through
  visual association and habituation.
  Integration despite the absence
  of deliberate substrate navigation.
  This demonstrates that the substrate
  is so robust it integrates even
  non-deliberate participants
  who accidentally satisfy
  the SAFE + RESOURCE conditions
  through physical presence and food.

## THE SIGNAL ACCURACY RULES
## What you must never violate

```
These are the economic rules.
Violating them spends trust
you cannot easily re-earn.

RULE 1 — RESOURCE ACCURACY:
  Never transmit RESOURCE without
  resource present or immediately
  available.
  One false RESOURCE reduces the
  signal value for all future
  RESOURCE transmissions.
  The economy enforces this through
  reduced approach latency —
  if your RESOURCE is confirmed once
  but not twice, the flock holds
  its position longer on the third.
  Consistent inaccuracy = no response.

RULE 2 — SAFE ACCURACY:
  Never transmit SAFE when a
  threat is present or approaching.
  SAFE followed by threat =
  the most damaging possible
  signal inaccuracy.
  You told the flock the network
  was stable while it was not.
  Predators exploit exactly this.
  The flock's nervous system is
  tuned to penalise this error
  maximally.
  One SAFE + threat event can
  require 10+ accurate SAFE
  broadcasts to recover.

RULE 3 — ALARM ACCURACY:
  Only transmit ALARM with
  genuine threat present.
  ALARM without threat = cry wolf.
  Repeated false ALARMs produce
  habituation — the flock stops
  responding to your ALARM.
  An ALARM signal that produces
  no response is an ALARM that
  cannot protect the flock when
  real threat arrives.
  You have spent a signal
  you cannot recover.

RULE 4 — CONSISTENCY:
  Same location. Same time.
  Same signal sequence.
  Inconsistency is not inaccuracy
  but it is unreliability.
  A node that appears randomly
  with inconsistent signals
  is not integrated as a
  persistent node.
  It is catalogued as an
  occasional event.
  Events are not trusted.
  Nodes are trusted.
  Consistency is what makes you
  a node rather than an event.

RULE 5 — SILENCE INTEGRITY:
  When you transmit SAFE and
  then go silent — stay silent.
  Do not fill the silence with
  more transmissions.
  The silence IS the SAFE signal
  continuing.
  Breaking the silence with
  more transmissions says:
  I am not actually settled.
  That is inaccurate.
  Silence integrity is the most
  frequently violated rule by
  new operators.
  Do not talk over the signal
  you just sent.
