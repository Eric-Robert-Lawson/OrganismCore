### Phase 1 — Arrival and long-distance entry

```
DISTANCE: Outside flock's immediate
visual assessment range.
For small passerines: 15-20 metres.
For corvids: 20-30 metres.

Step 1:
  Stop. Do not move toward flock yet.
  Play WHERE_ARE_YOU_HIGH once.
  Wait 15 seconds.
  Listen for response calls.

  PURPOSE:
    You are announcing your approach
    before you are in visual range.
    The flock hears a valid separation
    call before it sees you.
    When you do come into view,
    the acoustic assessment has
    already begun processing you
    as a potential substrate node.
    Not as a predator.
    Not as a threat.
    As a node that is searching
    for the network.

Step 2:
  Move slowly into visual range.
  Stop again. Sit or crouch if possible.
  45 degree body angle. Not facing
  directly toward the flock.

Step 3:
  Play I_AM_HERE_MID × 3.
  Full sequence with gaps.
  Stop. Wait.

  PURPOSE:
    Position announcement.
    You are here.
    You are rising toward the
    network's eigenfunction centre.
    Come toward me or acknowledge me.
```

### Phase 2 — Safe state establishment

```
After I_AM_HERE sequence completes:

Wait for any flock response.
  If birds call back:
    Play ACKNOWLEDGE once.
    Wait.
    Then proceed to SAFE.

  If no call back but birds
  have oriented toward you:
    Proceed to SAFE.

  If no response at all:
    Play I_AM_HERE once more.
    Wait 20 seconds.
    If still nothing — sit quietly
    for 2 minutes before trying again.
    Do not rush to SAFE.
    SAFE without acknowledged
    presence is background noise.

Play SAFE × 3.
  Full sequence, 1200ms gaps.
  Do not move during SAFE transmission.
  You are broadcasting settled state.
  Moving while broadcasting settled
  state is a contradiction on
  the visual channel.
  Be still.

After SAFE:
  Remain still and quiet for
  60-90 seconds.

  PURPOSE OF THE SILENCE:
    You have announced position.
    You have broadcast settled state.
    The flock is assessing whether
    your settled state broadcast
    is accurate.
    Is this node actually settled?
    Is there actually no threat here?
    The silence is the confirmation.
    A node that transmits SAFE and
    then immediately transmits more
    signals is not settled.
    Silence after SAFE IS the SAFE signal
    continuing on the acoustic channel.
```

### Phase 3 — Resource signal and delivery

```
Only transmit RESOURCE after
SAFE silence period completes
AND after at least one of:
  — A bird has called back to you
  — A bird has moved toward you
  — A bird is visibly attending
    to your position

If none of these have occurred,
the network has not accepted
your node yet.
Transmitting RESOURCE to an
unaccepted node produces no effect.
The signal requires an accepted
node to carry it.

When ready:

Step 1:
  Hold food visibly in open hand
  OR place it on the ground/surface
  in front of you.
  The resource must be physically
  present before you transmit.
  You are not lying to the network.
  The signal is accurate.

Step 2:
  Orient slightly toward the resource.
  The RESOURCE terminal stays high —
  the trajectory points outward.
  Your body geometry should
  complement the signal geometry.
  You are pointing at the basin.

Step 3:
  Play RESOURCE_MID × 4.
  Sustained purposeful rhythm.
  600ms gaps between repeats.

Step 4:
  Go still.
  Wait.

WHAT SHOULD HAPPEN:
  Birds that have accepted your node
  will orient toward the resource.
  Not necessarily immediately.
  The signal has told them there
  is a stable positive basin here.
  They will assess whether the
  signal is accurate before committing.
  First approach is typically
  one bird — the most exploratory
  or least threat-sensitive.
  Others follow if the first
  bird confirms the resource.

  This is the network operating
  correctly.
  One node receives your RESOURCE.
  It confirms it physically.
  It becomes a secondary transmitter
  of the resource information.
  The network propagates the
  confirmation you started.
```

### Phase 4 — Maintain the node during feeding

```
While birds are feeding:

  TRANSMIT periodically:
    SAFE × 3 every 3-4 minutes.
    This maintains the settled state
    broadcast during the feeding period.
    The flock is in a vulnerable
    state while feeding — heads down,
    reduced vigilance.
    Your SAFE broadcast is actively
    useful to them right now.
    It is one more node saying:
    the network is stable,
    continue feeding.

  ACKNOWLEDGE any calls:
    If birds call while feeding,
    play ACKNOWLEDGE once.
    Do not interrupt with
    I_AM_HERE or RESOURCE during feeding.
    The network is already at
    the resource.
    You do not need to redirect it.

  DO NOT MOVE suddenly.
  DO NOT make rapid gestures.
  DO NOT allow other humans to
  approach quickly.
  You are maintaining a SAFE
  state broadcast with your
  body and your speaker
  simultaneously.
  Contradiction between channels
  collapses the safe state
  immediately.

  If a threat does appear
  (dog, cat, person rushing):
    Let the birds respond naturally.
    Do NOT play ALARM.
    The birds will alarm-call themselves.
    Your ALARM adds nothing.
    After the threat passes:
    Play ALL_CLEAR × 3.
    This is where ALL_CLEAR
    is genuinely useful —
    you are actively
    de-escalating the network
    back to resource state
    faster than it would
    de-escalate on its own.
```

```
    The node is not alarming.

    The network's last record
    of this node is: SAFE.
    That is the record you want
    to leave.
    It is the record that makes
    the next session faster to
    re-establish.
```
