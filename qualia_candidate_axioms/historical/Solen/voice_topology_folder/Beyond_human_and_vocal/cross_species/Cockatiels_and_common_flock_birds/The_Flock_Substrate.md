## WHAT THE LITERATURE CONFIRMS

### Magrath et al. 2015 — Biological Reviews
Alarm calls converge structurally across
unrelated species in mixed flocks.
High-pitched tonal calls appear independently
in unrelated lineages under the same
predation pressure.
This is convergent evolution of call
STRUCTURE, not just function.

### Goodale & Kotagama 2006 — Behavioral Ecology
Birds in mixed flocks respond to heterospecific
calls based on ACOUSTIC SIMILARITY, not species
identity. The closer the acoustic structure of
a call is to the receiver's own call space,
the stronger the response.
This is eigenfunction overlap producing
cross-species response.
They did not use that language.
That is what they measured.

### Wheatcroft & Price 2013 — Current Biology
Call structure influences association patterns
across species lines. Birds preferentially flock
with species whose calls are acoustically similar
to their own. The shared acoustic substrate is
actively used to select flock partners.

### Srinivasan & Quader 2012 — Animal Behaviour
Birds use interspecific social information —
not just alarm calls but contact and movement
signals — across species lines in mixed flocks.
The information transfer is bidirectional
and social in character.
