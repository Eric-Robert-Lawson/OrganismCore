## PREAMBLE: WHAT THIS DOCUMENT
## DERIVES AND WHY

The standard framing of animal
communication research asks:

*Which species are intelligent
enough to have language-like
communication?*

This framing is wrong at the root.
It makes intelligence the causal
variable. But intelligence is not
the cause. It is a correlate.

The correct question — derived
from the OrganismCore gap
navigation framework — is:

*Which species faced selection
pressure severe enough to force
the emergence of triadic
self-modeling, and what ecological
conditions generate that pressure?*

The answer is not about
intelligence. It is about
architecture.

**When the collective survival
structure of a species depends
on information that cannot
propagate through the visual
channel — because the visual
channel is structurally
interrupted — the acoustic channel
must carry that information.**

**To carry that information, the
acoustic channel must encode
multi-dimensional perceptual
content.**

**To encode multi-dimensional
perceptual content, the sender
must model itself as a distinct
information node — a perspective-
holder whose internal state is
not shared by the rest of the
collective.**

**That self-modeling IS the triadic
structure. And the triadic structure
IS the precondition for gap
navigation as socialization.**

The threshold is therefore not
about intelligence. It is about
the intersection of three
ecological and anatomical facts.

This document names those three
facts precisely, derives the
threshold from them, and runs
the prediction engine against
every species for which sufficient
data exists.

## PART I: THE THREE CONDITIONS

### CONDITION A: COLONIAL SURVIVAL
### DEPENDENCY
### (The Collective Stakes Condition)

```
FORMAL STATEMENT:
  The species lives in a collective
  structure where individual
  survival — specifically the
  survival probability of the
  individual organism — is
  significantly elevated by the
  coordinated response of the
  collective to threat or resource
  information.

WHAT THIS MEANS:
  The individual cannot survive
  as well alone as within the
  collective. The collective
  provides a survival benefit
  that depends on INFORMATION
  SHARING — not just on physical
  proximity (as in body warmth)
  or on synchronized motion (as
  in flocking).

  The critical distinction:
  INFORMATION-DEPENDENT survival
  benefit vs. PROXIMITY-DEPENDENT
  survival benefit.

  A fish in a school benefits from
  proximity — the school is a
  confusion effect for predators,
  and individual survival improves
  simply by being embedded in
  many moving bodies. No
  information transfer required.

  A prairie dog in a colony
  benefits from INFORMATION —
  the sentinel's call must reach
  the individual and be correctly
  interpreted for the survival
  benefit to actualize. If the
  call does not carry the right
  content, the individual responds
  incorrectly and dies.

THRESHOLD VALUE:
  Condition A is satisfied when
  the survival benefit of the
  collective structure is
  information-dependent, not
  merely proximity-dependent.

WHY THIS CONDITION IS NECESSARY:
  Without collective survival
  dependency, there is no
  selection pressure for
  communication at all. Any
  organism that has no need to
  coordinate with others can
  evolve in silence.

WHY THIS CONDITION IS NOT
SUFFICIENT ALONE:
  Many species have collective
  survival dependency but express
  it through visual or chemical
  channels rather than acoustic
  channels. Condition A determines
  THAT communication will evolve.
  Conditions B and C determine
  WHICH channel it will evolve
  through and HOW RICH it will be.
```

### CONDITION B: VISUAL GAP
### (The Channel Interruption Condition)

```
FORMAL STATEMENT:
  The visual channel is structurally
  interrupted in the primary
  operational environment of the
  species — meaning that
  information-bearing observations
  by one member of the collective
  cannot reliably propagate to
  all other members through direct
  visual contact.

INTERRUPTION TYPES:

  B1: UNDERGROUND ARCHITECTURE
    The collective structure
    involves underground living,
    burrow networks, or
    subterranean habitats where
    visual contact between members
    is physically impossible for
    significant portions of
    daily activity.
    EXAMPLES: prairie dogs,
    naked mole-rats, mole rats,
    ground squirrels, pikas.
    SEVERITY: HIGH to TOTAL.

  B2: NOCTURNAL OPERATION
    The species' primary active
    period is at night or in
    light conditions too poor
    for reliable visual
    communication.
    EXAMPLES: bats, most rodents,
    some primates (lorises,
    bush babies).
    SEVERITY: HIGH.

  B3: VISUAL OCCLUSION HABITAT
    Dense forest, tall grass,
    rocky terrain, or other
    structural environments
    where the line of sight between
    collective members is frequently
    blocked.
    EXAMPLES: forest monkeys
    (Diana, Campbell's, colobus),
    meerkats in tall grass,
    many forest birds.
    SEVERITY: MODERATE to HIGH.

  B4: LARGE SPATIAL DISTRIBUTION
    The collective operates over
    distances large enough that
    visual contact is impossible
    between all members even in
    open environments. The collective
    is too spatially distributed
    to maintain visual coherence.
    EXAMPLES: elephants (savanna
    herds spanning kilometers),
    sperm whales (deep-diving
    pod members separated by
    hundreds of meters of water),
    ravens (foraging individually
    over large territories).
    SEVERITY: MODERATE.

  B5: AQUATIC DEPTH OPACITY
    Water attenuates light rapidly
    with depth. At depth, the
    visual channel is functionally
    absent.
    EXAMPLES: sperm whales
    (hunting at 1000m+ depth),
    deep-sea species.
    SEVERITY: TOTAL below ~200m.

THRESHOLD VALUE:
  Condition B is satisfied when
  the visual gap is present for
  a significant fraction of the
  time during which threat or
  resource information needs to
  propagate through the collective.
  The gap need not be constant.
  It must be frequent enough to
  make visual-only information
  propagation an unreliable
  survival strategy.

WHY THIS CONDITION IS NECESSARY:
  If the visual channel is intact
  and reliable, there is no
  selection pressure to develop
  acoustic encoding of the same
  information. Visual signals are
  cheaper to produce and faster
  to propagate at short range.
  Acoustic encoding only becomes
  necessary when the visual
  channel cannot carry the
  required information.

  This is the causal heart of
  the threshold.

  The visual gap does not merely
  correlate with acoustic encoding
  richness. It CAUSES it, by
  removing the cheaper channel
  and forcing evolutionary
  investment in the acoustic
  alternative.

WHY SEVERITY MATTERS:
  The severity of the visual gap
  predicts the richness of the
  acoustic encoding.

  TOTAL visual gap (naked mole-
  rats underground) → maximum
  acoustic encoding pressure →
  richest encoding per unit
  of available anatomy.

  PARTIAL visual gap (meerkats
  in Kalahari scrub) → partial
  acoustic encoding pressure →
  multi-dimensional encoding
  present but less rich than
  in total-gap species.

  MINIMAL visual gap (open-
  savanna herding animals with
  good sight lines) → minimal
  acoustic encoding pressure →
  simple alarm calls, limited
  dimensional encoding.

  PREDICTION: Acoustic encoding
  dimensionality correlates with
  visual gap severity across
  species satisfying Conditions
  A and C.

  This is a testable quantitative
  prediction.
```

### CONDITION C: BOUNDED RESONATING
### VOCAL ANATOMY
### (The Instrument Condition)

```
FORMAL STATEMENT:
  The species possesses a bounded
  resonating vocal anatomy — a
  sound-producing structure with
  physically constrained geometry
  — capable of multi-parameter
  modulation across at least two
  independent acoustic dimensions.

WHAT THIS MEANS:
  Not all sound-producing organs
  are equally expressive.

  A bounded resonating vocal
  anatomy is one where:
  - The geometry constrains the
    achievable signal space to
    a finite, structured domain
    (the eigenfunction space)
  - The sender can independently
    modulate at least two parameters
    (e.g., fundamental frequency
    AND formant structure, or
    frequency AND duration, or
    temporal pattern AND spectral
    envelope)
  - The modulations can be combined
    to produce combinatorially
    distinct signals

  ONE dimension of modulation:
  binary alarm signal (loud vs.
  quiet, call vs. silence).
  Cannot encode multi-dimensional
  content. Insufficient.

  TWO dimensions of modulation:
  can encode a two-dimensional
  information space. Can begin
  to distinguish predator TYPE
  (one dimension) from predator
  URGENCY (second dimension).
  Minimal sufficient for
  multi-dimensional encoding.

  THREE+ dimensions of modulation:
  can encode predator type, size,
  speed, direction, color. Rich
  multi-dimensional encoding.
  Prairie dog case.

MAMMALIAN VOCAL ANATOMY:
  All mammals with laryngeal
  sound production have bounded
  resonating anatomy that satisfies
  Condition C at least minimally.
  The larynx and supralaryngeal
  tract constitute a bounded
  resonating system with:
  - Fundamental frequency (F0)
    controlled by vocal fold
    tension
  - Formant structure controlled
    by supralaryngeal tract shape
  - Duration controlled by
    expiratory timing
  - Amplitude controlled by
    subglottal pressure

  This is a minimum four-
  dimensional modulation space
  available to all mammals with
  a larynx.

  Condition C is satisfied by
  virtually all mammals.

  The variation across species
  is in HOW MUCH the instrument
  has been NAVIGATED — how deeply
  the species has explored its
  eigenfunction space under
  selection pressure.

INSTRUMENT NAVIGATION DEPTH:
  A species that satisfies
  Conditions A and B will face
  selection pressure to navigate
  its vocal instrument more deeply.

  The depth of navigation
  determines the richness of
  the encoding.

  Species with the longest
  evolutionary history of
  Conditions A+B will have
  navigated their instruments
  most deeply — their calls will
  show the most eigenfunction
  structure, the most stable
  attractor positions, the most
  combinatorial capacity.

  This is why prairie dogs can
  encode five dimensions:
  millions of years of A+B
  pressure have navigated the
  prairie dog vocal instrument
  deep into its eigenfunction
  space.

WHY THIS CONDITION IS NECESSARY:
  Without a bounded resonating
  anatomy, there is no structured
  signal space to navigate.
  A species that communicates
  only through substrate vibration
  or electrical signal (absent a
  vocal anatomy) would need the
  equivalent bounded instrument
  in that other channel for the
  threshold to apply.

  The threshold generalizes to
  ANY bounded resonating instrument
  channel (acoustic, substrate,
  electric) — not only to vocal
  anatomy specifically.

  The vocal anatomy is the
  most common case in colonial
  mammals.
```

## PART II: THE THRESHOLD STATED
## FORMALLY

```
THE ACOUSTIC SOCIALIZATION
THRESHOLD (AST):

  Let S be any species.
  
  S will develop multi-dimensional
  referential acoustic encoding —
  a proto-language — if and only if:
  
  CONDITION A: Individual survival
    in S is information-dependently
    elevated by collective
    coordination.
  
  AND
  
  CONDITION B: The visual channel
    is structurally interrupted
    for S in its primary
    operational environment with
    sufficient frequency and
    severity to make visual-only
    information propagation an
    unreliable survival strategy.
  
  AND
  
  CONDITION C: S possesses a
    bounded resonating sound-
    producing anatomy capable
    of at least two-dimensional
    independent modulation.

COROLLARY 1 (SEVERITY PREDICTION):
  The dimensionality of S's acoustic
  encoding scales with the severity
  of Condition B.
  
  Higher visual gap severity →
  higher encoding dimensionality.

COROLLARY 2 (NAVIGATION DEPTH
PREDICTION):
  For species satisfying A+B+C,
  the eigenfunction structure of
  the vocal space will be deeper
  (more precisely organized, more
  stable attractor positions) in
  species with longer evolutionary
  history under A+B pressure.

COROLLARY 3 (PROGRAM IMPLICATION):
  For species satisfying A+B+C,
  Door 2 of the cross-species
  communication program
  (triadic attribution) is
  pre-solved by the ecology.
  
  These species already model
  acoustic signals from their
  environment as gap navigation
  signals from distinct information
  nodes. They have been doing
  this for millions of years.
  
  Door 1 (eigenfunction mapping
  and synthesis) is the only
  remaining barrier.
  
  These are the highest-priority
  targets for the program.

FALSIFICATION CONDITIONS:
  F1: Find a species satisfying
    all of A+B+C that has NOT
    developed referential acoustic
    encoding.
    → Would require revision of
      the threshold or addition
      of a fourth condition.
  
  F2: Find a species with rich
    multi-dimensional referential
    acoustic encoding that does
    NOT satisfy Condition B.
    → Would require revising B
      as non-necessary.
  
  F3: Find that encoding
    dimensionality does NOT
    correlate with visual gap
    severity across species
    satisfying A+C.
    → Would falsify Corollary 1.
  
  F4: Find that Door 2 is NOT
    pre-solved for an A+B+C
    species — that despite rich
    referential encoding, the
    species does not respond to
    synthesized eigenfunction-
    position signals as gap
    navigation signals.
    → Would require revising
      Corollary 3.
  
  None of F1-F4 have been
  observed in the literature
  reviewed. The threshold
  currently stands unfalsified.
```

## PART III: THE SPECIES SURVEY —
## RUNNING THE PREDICTION ENGINE

### 3.0 Survey Method

For each species, three assessments:

```
A-SCORE: Colonial survival
  dependency (information-
  dependent)?
  0 = absent
  1 = partial / proximity-based
  2 = clear, information-dependent

B-SCORE: Visual gap severity?
  0 = minimal (open habitat,
      diurnal, good sight lines)
  1 = partial (intermittent,
      habitat-variable)
  2 = high (frequent occlusion
      or nocturnal operation)
  3 = severe / total (underground
      or deep aquatic)

C-SCORE: Vocal anatomy capacity?
  0 = minimal / absent
  1 = present, limited modulation
  2 = present, multi-dimensional

THRESHOLD MET: A≥2, B≥1, C≥1

PREDICTED OUTCOME:
  A+B+C MET → multi-dimensional
  referential encoding predicted.

OBSERVED OUTCOME:
  What the literature confirms.

MATCH: Does prediction = observation?
```

The threshold predicts encoding.

```

  (B-score 1–2)

  the species' vocal instrument
  toward eigenfunction positions
  that encode multi-dimensional
  referential content.
  
  Navigation occurs at the
  EVOLUTIONARY timescale.
  Each generation refines
  the call parameters.
  
  Individual animals produce
  calls that are innately tuned
  to eigenfunction positions
  — they are born knowing
  the calls, with parameter
  calibration from social
  exposure.
  
  EXAMPLES: Prairie dog, vervet,
  meerkat, ground squirrel.

TRANSMISSION
  (B-score 2–3, sustained over
  deep evolutionary time)
  
  Colony or population-specific
  dialect variants emerge —
  the specific parameter values
  at each eigenfunction position
  vary between groups, and
  individuals learn the LOCAL
  parameters through social
  exposure.

  — the call STRUCTURE is innate.
  The specific PARAMETER VALUES
  are culturally calibrated.
  
  EXAMPLES: Prairie dog dialects,
  naked mole-rat colony dialects,
  elephant population variants.

LEVEL 3: VOCAL LEARNING
  (B-score 3, combined with high
  total acoustic cognitive load —
  social + navigational + prey)

  becomes learnable and modifiable
  within a lifetime. The individual
  can acquire novel calls not
  in its innate repertoire.
  
  RARFL is now running on the
  vocal instrument within the
  individual's lifetime, not
  only across generations.

  elephants (partial), humans.

GRAMMAR

  RARFL social pressure over
  cultural generations)
  
  The combinatorial structure
  of the eigenfunction space

  not just by parametric variation
  but by structural recombination.
  
  Recursion becomes possible.
  Displacement (communication
  about things absent in time
  and space) becomes rich.
  
  EXAMPLES: Humans (confirmed).
  Possibly humpback whale

  recursive compositional
  properties — unconfirmed).
```

## PART VIII: THE THEORETICAL
## CLOSING

### 8.1 What This Framework Is Not

It is not a theory of animal
intelligence. It makes no claims
about brain size, neural complexity,
or cognitive capacity as causal
variables.

It is not a comparative linguistics
framework. It makes no claims
about syntactic recursion, the
language acquisition device,
or Chomskyan universal grammar.

It is not a behavioral ecology
model. It does not model fitness
consequences of individual call
decisions.

### 8.2 What This Framework Is

It is a **structural derivation**
from gap navigation theory of
the ecological conditions that
force second-order gap navigation
to emerge.

Second-order gap navigation is:
*navigating the gap between my
perceptual state and another
navigator's perceptual state.*

This requires:
- A self-model (knowing that
  my state differs from theirs)
- A shared channel (acoustic)
  through which the externalization
  can propagate
- A receiver that attributes
  the signal as carrying navigation
  content

The three threshold conditions
are the ecological preconditions
that make second-order gap
navigation necessary:

- Condition A: makes the gap
  matter for survival
- Condition B: removes the
  visual channel, forcing the
  acoustic alternative
- Condition C: provides the
  instrument for the acoustic
  channel

**The threshold does not explain
everything about animal communication.**

It explains one thing precisely:

*Which species face ecological
pressure sufficient to force
the emergence of triadic
self-modeling, and why.*

The answer is not about
intelligence. It is about
the architecture of the gap.

**Where the visual channel is
broken and the collective depends
on what only one member can see —
there, in that specific failure
of the visual system, socialization
was born.**

The prairie dog's call is not
a sophisticated behavior.

**It is the sound of a gap that
could not be crossed any other way.**
