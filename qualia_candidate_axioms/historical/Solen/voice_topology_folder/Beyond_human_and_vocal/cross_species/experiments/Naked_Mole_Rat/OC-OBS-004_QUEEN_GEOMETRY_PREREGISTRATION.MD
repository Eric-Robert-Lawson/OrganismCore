## THE THEORETICAL CLAIM

### Statement of the claim

> The colony's Tonnetz position is not a population-level
> statistical property that emerges from averaging across
> many individuals. It is a geometric structure anchored
> by a single source: the queen's vocal eigenfunction
> geometry. The colony's coherence is the colony's
> alignment to that geometry. Coherence collapse upon
> queen death is not a social disruption — it is the
> removal of the geometric anchor from which the entire
> colony's eigenfunction space was organized.

This is a stronger claim than OC-OBS-004 V2 tested.
V2 showed that colony positions are stable and structured.
This document asks: where does that structure come from?
The answer proposed is: from the queen.

### Why this follows from the Universal Tonnetz

In the Universal Tonnetz framework, a Tonnetz position
is not chosen arbitrarily. It is a position in a
physically-determined eigenfunction space. For a colony
of naked mole-rats in total sensory darkness underground,
the only continuously present, high-bandwidth signal
capable of anchoring a shared Tonnetz position is vocal.

The queen is the only individual whose vocalizations
are colony-wide in reach, continuous in time, and
hierarchically dominant. Her calls suppress reproductive
physiology in all other females. Her shoving behavior
combined with specific call patterns maintains the
social gradient across the entire colony.

Under the Universal Tonnetz framework, this is not
merely behavioral dominance. The queen's vocal
eigenfunction geometry is the reference coordinate
system from which every other animal in the colony
derives its position in eigenfunction space.

The colony does not have a shared Tonnetz because
the animals agreed on one. It has a shared Tonnetz
because they are all calibrated to the same source.

## THE GEOMETRIC COHERENCE MODEL

### The queen as geometric anchor

Define the queen's vocal eigenfunction geometry as Q:

  Q = { e₁, e₂, e₃, ... eₙ }

where eᵢ are the dominant eigenfunction components
of the queen's soft chirp vocalizations, derived from
spectral decomposition of her calls alone.

The claim is:

1. Q is more stable over time than any worker's
   eigenfunction geometry
2. Q is more distinct from the colony mean than
   any individual worker
3. Worker eigenfunction geometries are closer to Q
   than to any other individual's geometry
4. The colony centroid in eigenfunction space is
   a function of Q, not an average of all workers

These are four separate falsifiable predictions.

### Coherence collapse as geometric consequence

When the queen dies:
- Q is removed
- Workers have no geometric anchor
- Their eigenfunction positions begin diffusing
- The colony centroid loses stability
- Variance in eigenfunction space increases
- The colony fragments or a new Q is established

This is not a metaphor. It is a geometric prediction
about measurable quantities in eigenfunction space:

  Before queen loss:   low variance around stable centroid
  During succession:   high variance, no stable centroid
  After new queen:     low variance around new centroid

The trajectory of variance in eigenfunction space
through a queen transition event is a direct test
of whether the colony coherence is geometrically
anchored by the queen's eigenfunction structure.

### The differentiation hypothesis

If the queen's Tonnetz geometry is the anchor of the
colony's coherence, then:

  Different queens → different Tonnetz geometries
  → Different colony eigenfunction spaces
  → Different communication coordinate systems
  → Communication across colonies requires
     translation between coordinate systems

This has a direct implication for cross-species
communication with NMR:

> The universal language for NMR is not a fixed
> phonological system shared across all colonies.
> It is a physical eigenfunction space whose
> specific instantiation in any given colony is
> determined by the queen's geometry. To communicate
> with a colony, you must first identify the queen's
> Tonnetz — the specific coordinate system she has
> established — and address your signals within
> that coordinate system.

This reframes the cross-species communication
program from "decode the NMR language" to "calibrate
to the queen's geometry."

### The self-model implication

The queen's Tonnetz geometry is not just a broadcast
signal. Under the Universal Tonnetz framework, it is
the queen's acoustic self-model — the eigenfunction
structure of her own vocal instrument expressed
continuously into the colony's acoustic environment.

The colony's coherence is the colony's alignment to
that self-model. Every worker's calls are navigations
within the coordinate system the queen's self-model

The NMR colony is therefore a distributed system
organized around a central geometric self-model.
The queen does not command the colony. She defines
the coordinate system within which the colony's
acoustic reality is constructed.

Death of the queen is not loss of leadership.
It is loss of the coordinate system itself.
The colony does not know what direction to face
because the reference direction is gone.

## THE INTER-COLONY ISOLATION PREDICTION

### Why hive communication is not comprehensive

If each colony's Tonnetz is anchored by its queen's
unique geometry, then:

1. Colony A's eigenfunction space is organized around
   queen A's geometry
2. Colony B's eigenfunction space is organized around
   queen B's geometry
3. A signal that is meaningful in colony A's coordinate
   system will not be meaningful in colony B's, unless
   the two queens happen to share eigenfunction geometry

This predicts inter-colony communication is:
- Not comprehensive (signals do not transfer directly)
- Not coherent (no shared coordinate system)
- Bounded by the distance between queen geometries
  in the universal eigenfunction space

The OC-OBS-004 V2 result showed that baratheon is
far from dothrakib and martell in eigenfunction space
(distances 9.87 and 6.73). Dothrakib and martell are
8.35 apart from each other. These distances are
measurements of how different their queen geometries
are — if the hypothesis is correct.

### The biological function of geometric isolation

The Tonnetz geometry differentiation between colonies
serves a biological function: it prevents colony
co-optation. A signal from another colony cannot
trigger coordinated behavior in this colony because
the signal is in the wrong coordinate system. It is
not recognized as meaningful communication — it is
noise in this colony's eigenfunction space.

The queen's unique Tonnetz geometry is therefore
not just an identity marker. It is a cryptographic
key. The colony is responsive only to signals in the
queen's coordinate system. Signals from other colonies
are automatically excluded.

This is a geometric immune system for social coherence.

### The cross-species communication implication

To communicate intentionally with a specific NMR colony,
the procedure is:

  STEP 1: Record the queen's vocalizations
  STEP 2: Derive her eigenfunction geometry (Q)
  STEP 3: Map her Tonnetz — the specific coordinate
           system she has instantiated in this colony
  STEP 4: Synthesize signals at novel positions within
           her coordinate system
  STEP 5: Introduce those signals into the colony
           acoustic environment

Step 4 is the generative communication step.
A signal synthesized at a novel position within Q
is not noise, not a known call type, and not from
another colony. It is a signal in the correct
coordinate system — the queen's — that the colony
has no prior referent for.

What the colony does with that signal is the
communication experiment.

## FALSIFIABLE PREDICTIONS

All predictions are made before any analysis code
is written or run.

### P1 — Queen eigenfunction stability
The queen's eigenfunction geometry is more stable
over time than the mean worker stability.

**Measurement:** Compute longitudinal drift for
the queen (if identifiable in the dataset) and
compare to mean worker drift. Queen drift should
be significantly lower.

**Confirmation:** Queen drift < mean worker drift,
p < 0.05 by permutation test.

**Falsification:** Queen drift ≥ mean worker drift.

### P2 — Queen as colony centroid anchor
The colony centroid in eigenfunction space is
closer to the queen's eigenfunction geometry than
to any individual worker's.

**Measurement:** Compute distance from colony
centroid to each individual's mean eigenfunction
position. The queen should be the closest individual.

**Confirmation:** Queen is the closest individual
to the colony centroid in at least 2 of 4 PC
dimensions.

**Falsification:** Workers are systematically
closer to the colony centroid than the queen.

### P3 — Worker alignment to queen
Worker eigenfunction positions are closer to the
queen's geometry than to the population mean
derived without the queen.

**Measurement:** Compute mean worker distance
to queen vs mean worker distance to workerless
colony centroid. Queen-anchored centroid should
be closer.

**Confirmation:** Mean distance to queen < mean
distance to workerless centroid, p < 0.05.

**Falsification:** No significant difference.

### P4 — Inter-colony distance reflects queen distance
The distance between colonies in eigenfunction
space reflects the distance between their queens'
eigenfunction geometries, not just the population
means.

**Measurement:** If queen identities can be
established from the data (highest F0 stability,
or lowest longitudinal drift), compute queen-to-
queen distances and compare to colony centroid
distances.

**Confirmation:** Queen-to-queen distance
correlates with colony centroid distance (r > 0.7).

**Falsification:** No correlation between queen
geometry distance and colony centroid distance.

### P5 — Tonnetz geometry differentiates colonies
The queen's eigenfunction geometry is more
different between colonies than worker geometries
are between colonies.

**Measurement:** Compute queen eigenfunction
geometry for each colony. Compute between-queen
distance. Compare to between-worker distance
distributions.

**Confirmation:** Between-queen distance falls
in the upper quartile of between-worker distance
distribution.

**Falsification:** Between-queen distance falls
within the normal between-worker distance range.

### P6 — Novel signal recognition requires queen calibration
A synthesized signal at a Tonnetz position defined
by the queen's geometry will produce measurable
behavioral response. A signal at the same absolute
position in universal eigenfunction space but NOT
within the queen's specific coordinate system will
not produce the same response.

**Note:** P6 requires a live experiment and cannot
be tested against the Barker 2021 dataset. It is
pre-registered here as the logical conclusion of
P1-P5 and the target of the synthesis protocol.

## QUEEN IDENTIFICATION PROBLEM

### The dataset does not label queens

The Barker 2021 dataset does not contain explicit
queen identity labels. Animal IDs are numeric codes.
We do not know which animal is the queen in any
colony session.

### How to identify the queen from the data alone

The queen's eigenfunction geometry should be
identifiable without labels, using the following
properties derived from the theoretical framework:

**Property 1 — Maximum stability:**
The queen is the animal with the lowest longitudinal
drift in eigenfunction space. Her geometry is the
anchor, so it should be the most stable over time.

**Property 2 — Maximum distinctiveness:**
The queen's calls should be the most distinct from
the worker mean — the clearest position on the
Tonnetz rather than a position close to others.

**Property 3 — Maximum F0 consistency:**
The queen's fundamental frequency should be the
most consistent over time (lowest F0 variance
across recording sessions).

**Property 4 — Maximum influence radius:**
The queen is the animal whose eigenfunction
geometry is most predictive of the colony centroid
position. Remove each animal in turn and recompute
the colony centroid. The animal whose removal
shifts the centroid the most is the queen candidate.

These four criteria can be computed from the
existing Barker dataset without any additional
labeling. The animal that scores highest on all
four criteria is the queen candidate.

If the framework is correct, one animal per colony
should consistently rank highest on all four criteria.

### Baratheon is the testable colony

Only baratheon has sufficient longitudinal data
(up to 12 recording dates per animal) to run
this identification procedure reliably. Martell
has 3 dates and dothrakib has 1 date — insufficient
for longitudinal stability analysis at the
individual level.

The queen identification analysis will be run
on baratheon only. The animal identified as the
queen candidate by the four criteria will be
compared against any available external records
of baratheon colony structure.

## WHAT THE ANALYSIS WILL COMPUTE

### Module 1 — Queen candidate identification
For each animal in baratheon with ≥ 4 recording
dates:

1. Longitudinal drift in eigenfunction space
   (lower = more queen-like)
2. Distance from colony centroid
   (closer = more queen-like)
3. F0 variance across sessions
   (lower = more queen-like)
4. Centroid shift upon removal
   (larger shift = more queen-like)

Rank animals on each criterion.
Identify the animal that ranks highest overall.
This is the queen candidate.

### Module 2 — Colony alignment to queen candidate
Once the queen candidate is identified:

1. Compute the queen candidate's eigenfunction
   geometry (mean position per session)
2. Compute distance from each worker to the queen
   candidate's geometry
3. Compute distance from each worker to the
   workerless colony centroid
4. Test P3: queen-anchored centroid is closer

### Module 3 — Temporal alignment test
For each recording session:

1. Compute the queen candidate's eigenfunction
   position in that session
2. Compute each worker's eigenfunction position
   in that session
3. Compute worker-to-queen distance per session
4. Test whether worker-to-queen distance is lower
   than worker-to-random-animal distance

### Module 4 — Inter-colony queen geometry comparison
Using the queen candidates from each colony
(baratheon confirmed, martell and dothrakib
attempted with limited data):

1. Compute queen-to-queen distances in
   eigenfunction space
2. Compare to colony centroid-to-centroid distances
3. Test P4: queen distances predict colony distances

### Module 5 — Generative synthesis specification
Using the baratheon queen candidate's eigenfunction
geometry:

1. Map the queen's Tonnetz — specific eigenfunction
   positions and the coordinate system they define
2. Identify positions in the queen's coordinate
   system that no known call type occupies
3. Specify the synthesis parameters for three
   novel signals at those positions
4. Document the synthesis specification for the
   live experiment

This module produces a synthesis specification,
not audio. The audio synthesis requires the
generative pipeline described in the NMR vocal
instrument document.

If P1-P5 do not confirm — if no single animal
consistently anchors the colony's eigenfunction
space — then the colony's Tonnetz position is
genuinely emergent from the population rather
than anchored by a single source.

This would mean:
- Colony coherence is distributed, not centralized
- The queen's role is behavioral, not geometric
- Cross-species communication must address the
  population average, not a single individual
- The coherence collapse upon queen death has
  a different explanation than geometric anchor
  removal

A null result here would be important information
for the communication program and would redirect
the synthesis protocol toward population-level
Tonnetz mapping rather than queen-calibrated
synthesis.

Both outcomes are informative. Both will be
reported in full.
