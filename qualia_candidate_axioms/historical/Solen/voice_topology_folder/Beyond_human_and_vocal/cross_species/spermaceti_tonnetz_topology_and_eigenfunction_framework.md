## PART III: THE TONNETZ TOPOLOGY
### The Geometric Structure of The
### Coda Space

### 3.1 What The Tonnetz Is

The Tonnetz (German: "tone network")
was originally developed in 18th-19th
century music theory (Euler, Riemann)
to represent harmonic relationships
between pitches.

In its modern mathematical form
(Cohn, 1997; Tymoczko, 2011):

The Tonnetz is a two-dimensional
topological space — specifically
a 2-torus — where:

- **Nodes** represent stable tonal
  positions (pitches, chords)
- **Edges** represent minimal
  combinatorial transitions between
  positions (one unit of change
  in one dimension)
- **The topology** — how nodes
  connect — is determined by the
  interval structure of the system

**The Tonnetz captures:**

Not just the positions but the
**geometry of the relationships
between positions** — what is
near, what is far, what transitions
are natural, what are forced.

---

### 3.2 The Spermaceti Tonnetz

The sperm whale coda space has
**two independent combinatorial
dimensions** (Beguš et al. 2025,
Sharma et al. 2024):

**Dimension 1: Timing**
- Click count (1-click, 2-click,
  3-click, 4-click, 5-click codas)
- Inter-click intervals
- Rhythm patterns (rubato, tempo)
- Discrete stable positions along

**Dimension 2: Spectral**
- Formant position (Category 1

- Discrete stable positions along

These two dimensions are:

The nodes are the joint positions

The edges are the minimal single-
dimension transitions between nodes.

The topology — how nodes connect —

- The interval structure of the

  2-click is a minimal step)

  spectral dimension (Category 1

**The full coda space is a
