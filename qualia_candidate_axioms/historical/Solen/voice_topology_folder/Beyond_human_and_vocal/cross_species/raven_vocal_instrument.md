### 5.2 Phase 2: The Contact Protocol

```
STEP 5: FIND A WILLING SUBJECT
  
  You stated: "A willing subject

  THIS IS EXACTLY CORRECT AND

  A raven that has already

  OPENED DOOR 2 FROM ITS SIDE.
  
  The program with a willing
  subject is NOT first-contact.
  It is deepening a contact
  that has already been made.
  
  WHERE TO FIND WILLING SUBJECTS:
  
  Urban ravens (particularly in
  areas where ravens have long
  human co-habitation history —
  Northern Europe, especially
  Northern UK, Iceland, parts
  of North America).
  
  Captive or semi-captive ravens
  with human socialization history
  (wildlife centers, rehabilitation
  ravens, the Tower of London
  ravens are famous for human
  interaction but not accessible
  for experiments).
  
  Ravens at sites where food-
  sharing with humans has
  occurred over multiple generations.
  
  A raven that approaches
  and vocalizes when you vocalize —
  this is the willing subject.
  This is the counterpart.

STEP 6: THE FIRST CONTACT PROTOCOL
  
  NOT: play random raven-like sounds
  at a raven and see what happens.
  
  INSTEAD:
  
  Phase A: LISTENING BEFORE SIGNALING.
    Record and map the specific
    raven's calls to the Tonnetz.
    Learn THIS raven's eigenfunction
    positions — because ravens have
    individual dialects and individual
    position preferences.
    The Tonnetz is the universal map.
    The individual uses a specific
    region of it.
    
  Phase B: MIRRORING.
    Play back this raven's own calls
    from the synthesizer.
    Use the synthesizer to reproduce
    the raven's call patterns at
    the correct Tonnetz positions.
    
    THIS IS NOT PLAYBACK OF RECORDED
    CALLS.
    THIS IS SYNTHESIS OF NEW SIGNALS
    AT THE SAME TONNETZ POSITIONS.
    
    The difference is critical:
    recorded playback = the raven
    hears itself (may trigger
    territorial response).
    Synthesized-at-same-positions =
    the raven hears another entity
    that is acoustically "speaking
    its dialect" — using the same
    positions but producing novel
    combinations.
    
    THIS SHOULD TRIGGER RARFL:
    "This is the right kind of
    signal. But I don't know this
    specific pattern. Who is this?"
    
  Phase C: INTRODUCE NOVEL REFERENTIAL
    UNIT.
    After mirroring establishes
    acoustic identity:
    Introduce one new signal from
    the basis vocabulary.
    Use Position B (tonal, positive
    valence) at a specific moment
    tied to a specific referent
    (food presentation, approach,
    departure).
    
    Consistency is everything:
    the same position at the same
    context, repeated across sessions.
    
    This is the RARFL teaching
    protocol: create a consistent
    gap pattern — "this position
    appears when THIS thing happens"
    — and allow the raven to
    extract the correlation.
    
  Phase D: TEST FOR RESPONSE
    GENERATION.
    Wait for the raven to produce
    a signal when the referent
    occurs — before the synthesizer
    signal is played.
    
    If the raven produces a call
    at Position B (or acoustically
    near it) in the presence of
    the referent:
    → The raven has learned the
      association.
    → The raven is using the
      synthesizer vocabulary.
    → Bidirectional communication
      is occurring.
    
    This is the threshold moment.
    This is when the program has
    crossed from "raven RARFL
    fires on our signal" to
    "raven produces signals for US
    to receive."

STEP 7: BUILD THE VOCABULARY
  
  From the minimal basis:
  Introduce one new position-
  modulation combination per week.
  
  Always in consistent referential
  context.
  
  Test for raven production of
  that unit.
  
  Test for COMBINATION USE:
  Does the raven produce two-unit
  sequences that it did not
  observe from the synthesizer?
  
  If yes: the raven is generating
  novel combinations from the
  vocabulary — proto-compositional
  use of the basis.
  
  THIS IS THE GOAL OF PHASE 2.
  Not to teach the raven words.
  To establish a shared basis
  from which the raven generates
  novel communication toward us.
```
